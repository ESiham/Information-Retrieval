<p>A <b>tropical climate</b> in the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> is a non-<a href="/wiki/Arid" title="Arid">arid</a> climate in which all twelve months have mean temperatures of at least 18 °C (64 °F). In tropical climates there are often only two seasons: a wet season and a dry season. Tropical climates are frost-free and changes in the solar angle are small. In tropical climates temperature remains relatively constant (hot) throughout the year.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subtypes">Subtypes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Subtypes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the tropical climate zone there are distinct varieties based on precipitation:</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tropical_rainforest_climate">Tropical rainforest climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tropical rainforest climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rainforest_climate" title="Tropical rainforest climate">Tropical rainforest climate</a> (<i>Af</i>):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McKnight_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-McKnight-1">[1]</a></sup> all twelve months have an average precipitation of at least 60 mm (2.4 in). These climates usually occur within 5–10° latitude of the equator. In some eastern coast areas, they may extend to as much as over 26° away from the equator. This climate is dominated by the <a href="/wiki/Doldrums" title="Doldrums">doldrums</a> Low Pressure System all year round, and therefore has no natural seasons.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tropical_monsoon_climate">Tropical monsoon climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tropical monsoon climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Tropical_monsoon_climate" title="Tropical monsoon climate">Tropical monsoon climate</a> (<i>Am</i>):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McKnight_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-McKnight-1">[1]</a></sup> is a type of climate that is common in <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a> and in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>, as well as part of Africa and Australia. It is dominated by the <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a> winds which change direction according to the seasons. This climate has a driest month (which nearly always occurs at or soon after the <a href="/wiki/Winter_solstice" title="Winter solstice">"winter" solstice</a> for that side of the equator) with rainfall less than 60 millimetres (2.4 in), but more than (100 − [total annual precipitation {mm}/25]).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tropical_wet_and_dry_climate">Tropical wet and dry climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tropical wet and dry climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Tropical wet and dry or <a href="/wiki/Tropical_savanna_climate" title="Tropical savanna climate">savanna</a> climate (<i>Aw</i>):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McKnight_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-McKnight-1">[1]</a></sup> These climates generally have a pronounced dry season, with the driest month having precipitation less than 60 millimetres (2.4 in) and less than (100 − [total annual precipitation {mm}/25]).</p>
<p><b>Examples</b></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Exceptions">Exceptions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Exceptions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Owing to different causes certain places within the tropics do not have a tropical climate, such as some desert areas and alpine regions.</p>
<ul>
<li>Examples:
<ul>
<li>Southern part of <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a>.</li>
<li>Alpine regions of the tropics. Mountaintops within the tropics, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Mount_Kenya" title="Mount Kenya">Mount Kenya</a>, can be cold. However, like lowlands in the tropics (and unlike cold winter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate_zone" title="Temperate zone">temperate zone</a> regions), there is little seasonal variation of temperature.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Intertropical_Convergence_Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Intertropical Convergence Zone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Because of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Effect_of_sun_angle_on_climate" title="Effect of sun angle on climate">effect of sun angle on climate</a> most areas within the tropics are hot year-round, with diurnal variations in temperature exceeding seasonal variations. Seasonal variations in tropical climate are dominated by changes in precipitation, which are in turn largely influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rain_belt" title="Tropical rain belt">tropical rain belt</a> or <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a> (ITCZ), a portion of the <a href="/wiki/Hadley_cell" title="Hadley cell">Hadley cell</a>. The ITCZ is shown, for July average, in the graphic. Areas of ascending air have heavy rainfall; areas of descending air are dry. The ITCZ somewhat follows the solar equator throughout the year, but with geographical variations, and in some areas (India) is heavily influenced by local large-scale <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoons</a>.</p>
