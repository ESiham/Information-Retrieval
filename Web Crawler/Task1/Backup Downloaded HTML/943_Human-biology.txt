<p><b>Human biology</b> is an interdisciplinary area of study that examines humans through the influences and interplay of many diverse fields such as <a href="/wiki/Human_genetics" title="Human genetics">genetics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Human_evolution" title="Human evolution">evolution</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_physiology" title="Human physiology">physiology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anatomy" title="Anatomy">anatomy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Epidemiology" title="Epidemiology">epidemiology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthropology" title="Anthropology">anthropology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Human_ecology" title="Human ecology">ecology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Human_nutrition" title="Human nutrition">nutrition</a>, <a href="/wiki/Population_genetics" title="Population genetics">population genetics</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sociocultural_context" title="Sociocultural context">sociocultural</a> influences. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is closely related to <a href="/wiki/Biological_anthropology" title="Biological anthropology">biological anthropology</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biological</a> fields.</p>
