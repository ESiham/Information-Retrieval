<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">East Asian rainy season</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>East Asian rainy season</b>, commonly called the <b>plum rain</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: 梅雨; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i>méiyǔ</i>; <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Japanese" title="Romanization of Japanese">rōmaji</a>: <i>tsuyu/baiu</i>; <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko">장마</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">romaja</a>: <i>jangma</i>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">затяжные</span>, <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Russian transliteration"><i>zatyazhnie</i></span>), is caused by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> along a persistent <a href="/wiki/Stationary_front" title="Stationary front">stationary front</a> known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mei-Yu_front" title="Mei-Yu front">Mei-Yu front</a> for nearly two months during the late <a href="/wiki/Spring_(season)" title="Spring (season)">spring</a> and early <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">summer</a> between eastern <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Wet_season" title="Wet season">wet season</a> ends during the summer when the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> becomes strong enough to push this front north of the region.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Asian_rainy_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An east-west zone of disturbed weather during spring along this front stretches from the east China coast, initially across Taiwan and Okinawa, later, when it has shifted to the north, eastward into the southern peninsula of South Korea and Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The rainy season usually lasts from May to June in Taiwan and Okinawa,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> from June to July (approximately 50 days) in Russian <a href="/wiki/Primorsky_Krai" title="Primorsky Krai">Primorsky Krai</a>, Japan and Korea and from July to August in Eastern China (especially the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River">Chang Jiang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huai_River" title="Huai River">Huai River</a> regions).</p>
<p>The weather front forms when the moist air over the Pacific meets the cooler continental <a href="/wiki/Air_mass" title="Air mass">air mass</a>. The front and the formation of <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">frontal depressions</a> along it brings precipitation to Primorsky Krai, Japan, Korea, eastern China, and Taiwan. As the front moves back and forth depending on the strength of cool and warm air masses, there is often prolonged precipitation and sometimes flooding in eastern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. However, in the years that it does not rain as much as usual, a drought might result. The rainy season ends when the warm air mass associated with the subtropical ridge is strong enough to push the front north and away.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects">Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Asian_rainy_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The high <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">humidity</a> in the air during this season encourages the formation of <a href="/wiki/Mold" title="Mold">mold</a> and <a href="/wiki/Decomposition" title="Decomposition">rot</a> not only on food but on fabrics as well. Environmentally, heavy rains encourage <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mudslides" title="Mudslides">mudslides</a> and flooding in all areas affected. The most rain in a one-hour period as recorded in Japan was in <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a> in 1982 with 153 mm. The highest overall recorded rainfall during the rainy season in Japan was in 2003, when <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki_Prefecture" title="Miyazaki Prefecture">Miyazaki Prefecture</a> recorded rains of 8670 mm.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Asian_rainy_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Japan, the rainy season lasts from early June to mid-July for most of the country (on the main island of <a href="/wiki/Honshu" title="Honshu">Honshū</a> and the islands of <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">Kyūshū</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shikoku" title="Shikoku">Shikoku</a>), approximately June 7 to July 20 for the main <a href="/wiki/Kansai_region" title="Kansai region">Kansai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_region" title="Kantō region">Kantō</a> regions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jg_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-jg-3">[3]</a></sup> It comes a month earlier to Okinawa in the south (early May through mid-June), but <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaidō</a> in the north is largely unaffected. The season is occasionally called <i>Samidare</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span lang="ja">五月雨</span>, literally "May Rain (in <a href="/wiki/Lunisolar_calendar" title="Lunisolar calendar">Japanese traditional calendar</a>)"; corresponds roughly to June in modern calendar)</span> on account of this timing. The <a href="/wiki/Enka" title="Enka">enka</a> artist Eiichi Ōtaki produced a popular song by this name, and a WW2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_destroyer_Samidare" title="Japanese destroyer Samidare">Japanese naval ship</a> was also given this name.</p>
<p>The rains in the middle of November - early December are often called <i>Sazanka Tsuyu</i>, literally "rainy season of the <a href="/wiki/Camellia_sasanqua" title="Camellia sasanqua">Camellia</a>" on account of the timing with the blossoming of the seasonal flower.</p>
<p>This period is generally avoided for tourism, but some sights are considered particularly atmospheric in the rain and fog, particularly mountain forests, notably <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Sites_and_Pilgrimage_Routes_in_the_Kii_Mountain_Range" title="Sacred Sites and Pilgrimage Routes in the Kii Mountain Range">Sacred Sites and Pilgrimage Routes in the Kii Mountain Range</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Mount_K%C5%8Dya" title="Mount Kōya">Mount Kōya</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jg_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-jg-3">[3]</a></sup> Vegetation, especially moss, is also rather lush at this time, and hence sights known for their moss, such as <a href="/wiki/Saih%C5%8D-ji_(Kyoto)" title="Saihō-ji (Kyoto)">Saihō-ji</a> (the moss temple) are also popular at this time of year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Korea">Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Asian_rainy_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The rainy season is between June and mid-July. It is caused by hot and humid high pressure forming in the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a> due to the North Pacific anticyclone combining with Asiatic continental high pressure. When the two meteorological events meet they form a long <i>jangmajeonseon</i> (<a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: 장마前線). Beginning in late-May, the North Pacific high pressure forces the weaker continental anticyclone south of the island of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a>. This fall to the south then reverses and gradually strengthens as it moves northwards back towards the Korean peninsula. On landfall, heavy monsoon rains lead to torrential downpours and flooding. By August the system has weakened as the southern systems retreat towards the Filipino archipelago.</p>
<p>By early autumn, the North Pacific high pressure system is pushed away as Asiatic continental cold high pressure moves southwards. This produces inclement weather although not on the scale of the summer monsoons. Korea can, however, be struck by typhoons during this period.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timing">Timing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Asian_rainy_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Timing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In some years, the rainy season's actual beginning and end are under debate. For example, in 2005, the subtropical ridge moved quickly northward in late June/early July. The weather front skipped the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangtze_River" title="Yangtze River">Chang Jiang</a> region and there was no rainy season there. Then, the ridge retreated southward and there was significant rainfall in the region. This gave rise to the question of whether this was the summer-type rainfall pattern that is common after the first rainy season or the second rainy season. Some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorologist" title="Meteorologist">meteorologists</a> even argued that the rainy period in late June was not a true rainy season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
