<p>The <b>Congolese forests</b> are a broad belt of lowland <a href="/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_moist_broadleaf_forests" title="Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests">tropical moist broadleaf forest</a> <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregion</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_moist_broadleaf_forests" title="Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests">Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests</a> <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">Biome</a>, which extends across the basin of the <a href="/wiki/Congo_River" title="Congo River">Congo River</a> and its tributaries in <a href="/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">Central Africa</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congolese_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Congolese forests cover southeastern <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a>, the northern and central <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a>, the northern and central <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> and portions of southern and southwestern <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congolese_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To the north and south, the forests transition to drier <a href="/wiki/Forest-savanna_mosaic" title="Forest-savanna mosaic">forest-savanna mosaic</a>, a mosaic of drier forests, <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savannas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grasslands</a>. To the west, the Congolese forests transition to the coastal <a href="/wiki/Lower_Guinean_forests" title="Lower Guinean forests">Lower Guinean forests</a>, which extend from western Gabon and Cameroon into southern <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benin" title="Benin">Benin</a>; these forests zones share many similarities, and are sometimes known as the Lower Guinean-Congolese forests. To the east, the lowland Congolese forests transition to the highland <a href="/wiki/Albertine_Rift_montane_forests" title="Albertine Rift montane forests">Albertine Rift montane forests</a>, which cover the Mountains lining the <a href="/wiki/Albertine_Rift" title="Albertine Rift">Albertine Rift</a>, a branch of the <a href="/wiki/East_African_Rift" title="East African Rift">East African Rift</a> system. The Congolese Forests are a <a href="/wiki/Global_200" title="Global 200">global 200</a> <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregion</a>.</p>
<p>The Congo Rainforest is the world’s second largest tropical forest, spans six countries, and contains a quarter of the world’s remaining tropical forest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> With annual forest loss of 0.3% during the 2000s,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the region has the lowest deforestation rate of any major tropical forest zone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecoregions">Ecoregions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Congolese_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecoregions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">World Wide Fund for Nature</a> divides the Congolese forests into a number of distinct <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregions</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Northwestern_Congolian_lowland_forests&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Northwestern Congolian lowland forests (page does not exist)">Northwestern Congolese lowland forests</a> (Cameroon, Central African Republic, Gabon, Republic of Congo)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Western_Congolian_swamp_forests" title="Western Congolian swamp forests">Western Congolese swamp forests</a> (Republic of the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eastern_Congolian_swamp_forests" title="Eastern Congolian swamp forests">Eastern Congolese swamp forests</a> (Democratic Republic of the Congo)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Central_Congolian_lowland_forests" title="Central Congolian lowland forests">Central Congolese lowland forests</a> (Democratic Republic of the Congo)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Northeastern_Congolian_lowland_forests" title="Northeastern Congolian lowland forests">Northeastern Congolese lowland forests</a> (Democratic Republic of the Congo, Central African Republic)</li>
</ul>
