<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Rainwater harvesting in Canada</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><a href="/wiki/Rainwater_harvesting" title="Rainwater harvesting">Rainwater harvesting</a> is becoming a procedure that many Canadians are incorporating into their daily lives, although data does not give exact figures for implementation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-1">[1]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainwater" title="Rainwater">Rainwater</a> can be used for a number of purposes including <a href="/wiki/Stormwater" title="Stormwater">stormwater</a> reduction, <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>, laundry and portable toilets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> In addition to low costs, <a href="/wiki/Rainwater_harvesting" title="Rainwater harvesting">rainwater harvesting</a> is useful for landscape <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>.</p>
<p>Provincial and municipal legislation is in place for regulating the rights and uses for captured rainwater. There has been substantial reform to Canadian property rights since the mid 2000's, but there is still substantial ambiguity amongst legislation in many provinces.</p>
<p>Multiple organizations and companies have developed in Canada to provide education, technology, and instillation for rainwater harvesting. These include the Canadian Association for Rainwater Management (CANARM),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:6_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:6-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Canada_Mortgage_and_Housing_Corporation" title="Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation">Canadian Mortgage and Housing Corporation</a> (CMHC), and CleanFlo Water Technologies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> CANARM is an association that prioritizes education, training and spreading awareness for those entering the rainwater harvesting industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:6_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:6-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Property_rights">Property rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Property rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Building_Code_of_Canada" title="National Building Code of Canada">National Plumbing Code</a> permits the collection of rainwater for non-portable toilet flushing and outdoor irrigation throughout <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup> The right to harvest and use rainwater is determined by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provincial_government" title="Provincial government">provincial government</a> and municipal bylaws.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup> However, a majority of the provinces lack any significant <a href="/wiki/Legislation" title="Legislation">legislation</a>. The law of capture is not well defined and it varies for residential and non-domestic uses. When building a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainwater" title="Rainwater">rainwater</a> storage system, it is required that the design follows closely to the provincial codes and municipal bylaws.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> Multiple publications and reports have been developed by the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Mortgage_and_Housing_Corporation" title="Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation">Canadian Mortgage and Housing Corporation</a> (CMHC) in order to aid Canadians in designing and installing rainwater capture systems that comply with these codes, regulations and bylaws.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Provincial_property_rights">Provincial property rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Provincial property rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many provinces have outlined little to no provincial property rights pertaining to rainwater. Listed below are the provinces with some provincial legislation. However, many local municipalities have bylaws outlining criteria for capture and storage containers among other articles related to rainwater harvesting.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Alberta">Alberta</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Alberta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> has a set of Rainwater Harvesting Guidelines for residential use. They are recommended for the safe design, construction and maintenance of residential rainwater harvesting systems. The guidelines provide additional detail than what is present in the current code and supports conformance to <i>CAN/CSA 128.1 Design and Installation of Non-Potable Water Systems/Maintenance and Field Testing of Non-Potable Water Systems</i>. The Alberta Building Code and the National Plumbing Code requirements have precedence over these guidelines. In addition to the guidelines, a handbook was developed to provide broader understanding of the technical components of the guidelines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Columbia">British Columbia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: British Columbia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:364px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Victoria_BC_Marriott_green_roof.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="879" data-file-width="1024" height="311" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Victoria_BC_Marriott_green_roof.jpg/362px-Victoria_BC_Marriott_green_roof.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Victoria_BC_Marriott_green_roof.jpg/543px-Victoria_BC_Marriott_green_roof.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Victoria_BC_Marriott_green_roof.jpg/724px-Victoria_BC_Marriott_green_roof.jpg 2x" width="362"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Victoria_BC_Marriott_green_roof.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Victoria BC Marriott green roof.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Rainwater in <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> appears to be common property subject to the <a href="/wiki/Rule_of_capture" title="Rule of capture">law of capture</a>. Effectively, rainwater belongs to no one and everyone until it is captured. While landowners do not have a property interest in water until it is captured, their right to harvest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainwater" title="Rainwater">rainwater</a> is unrestricted and not subject to concerns of downstream water users. Rainwater is not included in the Water Act’s system of prior allocation. The likely common law position does not recognize correlative rights of other users to share equitably in the common resource of rainwater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Ontario">Ontario</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ontario">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, the province permits the use of rainwater for flushing toilets and urinals, as well as for sub-surface and below ground <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a> systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">[7]</a></sup> A large number of standards and regulations have been put in place regarding catchments, conveyance networks, and storage containers in Ontario Guidelines for Residential Rainwater Harvesting Systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">[7]</a></sup> Since the temperature during winter months in Ontario drops below zero, rainwater is at a high risk of freezing. Therefore, the province of Ontario places a strong significance on guidelines to ensure that rainwater is in a controlled temperature environment. In addition, to avoid freezing in the pipe network, the water must be properly controlled via draining and temperature controls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">[7]</a></sup> Various systems and scenarios have been outlined regarding overflow management, pressurization, and back-flow prevention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:4_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Applications_of_rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada">Applications of rainwater harvesting in Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Applications of rainwater harvesting in Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:372px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rainwater_Harvesting_and_Plastic_Pond_3.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1067" data-file-width="1600" height="247" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Rainwater_Harvesting_and_Plastic_Pond_3.JPG/370px-Rainwater_Harvesting_and_Plastic_Pond_3.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Rainwater_Harvesting_and_Plastic_Pond_3.JPG/555px-Rainwater_Harvesting_and_Plastic_Pond_3.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Rainwater_Harvesting_and_Plastic_Pond_3.JPG/740px-Rainwater_Harvesting_and_Plastic_Pond_3.JPG 2x" width="370"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rainwater_Harvesting_and_Plastic_Pond_3.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A rainwater harvesting pool for irrigation and water for livestock.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In Canada, rainwater harvesting used as a means of irrigation is not popular among farmers. Because of a rise in <a href="/wiki/Ecological_farming" title="Ecological farming">ecofarming</a>, many farmers have implemented these systems. The uses for the rainwater harvested for agriculture includes irrigation and water for livestock. Often the water collected is redirected to a deep pit with percolation.</p>
<h3><span id="Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design_(LEED)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design_.28LEED.29">Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED)</a> is a rating system that is recognized as the international mark of excellence for green building in 150 countries. This includes Canada as recognized by the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Green_Building_Council" title="Canada Green Building Council">Canada Green Building Council</a>. They give points for obtaining certifications to buildings with rainwater harvesting technologies implemented. These credits include the following: <a href="/wiki/Stormwater" title="Stormwater">Stormwater</a> quantity and quality controls to reduce <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">runoff</a>, increase reuse, and stop <a href="/wiki/Pollutant" title="Pollutant">pollutants</a>; efficient <a href="/wiki/Landscape_design" title="Landscape design">landscaping</a> for rainwater reuse; innovative <a href="/wiki/Wastewater" title="Wastewater">wastewater</a> technologies for non-potable applications such as toilet flushing and process water; <a href="/wiki/Water_efficiency" title="Water efficiency">water efficiency</a> to reduce the burden on municipal water supply and wastewater systems; and recycled content for use in rainwater harvesting materials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:5_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-:5-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:371px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CIRS_Building_UBC.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3072" data-file-width="4608" height="246" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/CIRS_Building_UBC.jpg/369px-CIRS_Building_UBC.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/CIRS_Building_UBC.jpg/554px-CIRS_Building_UBC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/CIRS_Building_UBC.jpg/738px-CIRS_Building_UBC.jpg 2x" width="369"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CIRS_Building_UBC.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Centre_for_Interactive_Research_on_Sustainability" title="Centre for Interactive Research on Sustainability">Centre for Interactive Research on Sustainability</a> uses rainwater capture methods, which helped it to become the first LEED Platinum building at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Similar to agriculture, rainwater harvesting in Canada for industrial purposes is not widely used. If rainwater is utilized for industrial or commercial uses, a large <a href="/wiki/Cistern" title="Cistern">cistern</a> is generally implemented. These cisterns can hold up to 40,000 litres of water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-:3-9">[9]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Toronto_Area" title="Greater Toronto Area">Greater Toronto Area</a> where the temperature in the winter months reaches well below freezing, cisterns are placed in temperature controlled areas such as an underground parking lot or a basement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-:3-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Centre_for_Interactive_Research_on_Sustainability" title="Centre for Interactive Research on Sustainability">Centre for Interactive Research on Sustainability</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia (UBC)</a> makes use of various rainwater capture methods for plumbing, heating and cooling. Data is not available on the amount of buildings or businesses implementing rainwater harvesting.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Residential">Residential</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Residential">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rainwater harvesting for residential use can be achieved easily by placing a tank underneath a downspout outside the home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-1">[1]</a></sup> There is also use in <a href="/wiki/Green_roof" title="Green roof">green roofs</a> for irrigating plants naturally, providing a cooling system, and regulating <a href="/wiki/Condensation" title="Condensation">condensation</a>. In Canada, the regulations and <a href="/wiki/Legislation" title="Legislation">legislation</a> are still being developed for rainwater harvesting and <a href="/wiki/Greywater#Reuse" title="Greywater">greywater reuse</a> (water used for laundry, showers and sinks <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-:1-1">[1]</a></sup>).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impacts">Impacts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Impacts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In areas where water is scarce, rainwater harvesting can reduce the consumption of <a href="/wiki/Groundwater" title="Groundwater">groundwater</a> and in turn can be used to recharge the groundwater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> In the summer months of Canada, the practice can decrease the pressure on municipal systems and is therefore seen as a great "green" alternative for water supply.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Canada, rainwater harvesting has the potential to impact environmental flows and downstream water users. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrological_cycle" title="Hydrological cycle">hydrological</a> cycle contains surface water, groundwater and rainwater. The effects on the hydrological cycle would be significant if rainwater became an advantageous solution to water quality and availability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> However, the effects this practice has on a watershed is still unknown and further research must be conducted.</p>

<p>While the majority of the country has not yet faced water scarcity issues, it is believed that the potential for this to happen will increase because of climate change.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> However, one example of these issues is the <a href="/wiki/Okanagan" title="Okanagan">Okanagan</a> region in <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>. Currently, 89.5% of the surface water being used as a source is constrained because of licensing restrictions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> Because of low volumes of rainfall throughout the year, an increase in rainwater harvesting could cause less water to be available to license holders that are downstream.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Benefits">Benefits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Canada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Benefits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rainwater harvesting can limit the disruption of natural <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a> by reducing impervious cover, increasing on-site <a href="/wiki/Infiltration_(hydrology)" title="Infiltration (hydrology)">infiltration</a>, reducing or eliminating pollution from <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">stormwater runoff</a> and eliminating <a href="/wiki/Water_pollution" title="Water pollution">contaminants</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:5_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-:5-8">[8]</a></sup> Furthermore, it can reduce the use of <a href="/wiki/Drinking_water" title="Drinking water">potable water</a> or other natural surface/subsurface water resources available near sites for <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">landscape irrigation</a>. Rainwater harvesting also results in a reduction of <a href="/wiki/Wastewater" title="Wastewater">wastewater</a> generation and <a href="/wiki/Drinking_water" title="Drinking water">potable water</a> demand. In addition, it reduces the burden on <a href="/wiki/Water_supply_network" title="Water supply network">municipal water supply</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wastewater" title="Wastewater">wastewater systems</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:5_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-:5-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
