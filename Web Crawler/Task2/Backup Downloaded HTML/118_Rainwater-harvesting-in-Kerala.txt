<p>There are good opportunities for <b>Rainwater harvesting in Kerala</b> because <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a> is located in a geographical area with two <a href="/wiki/Wet_season" title="Wet season">rainy seasons</a>. Kerala faces severe <a href="/wiki/Water_scarcity" title="Water scarcity">water scarcity</a> between February and mid May every year. During summer, there are drinking water shortages. During this period <a href="/wiki/Drinking_water" title="Drinking water">drinking water</a> and other water purposes become unavailable. This is expected in the coming years. In spite of 44 rivers and world's largest <a href="/wiki/Water_well" title="Water well">water well</a> density, per capita <a href="/wiki/Surface_water" title="Surface water">surface water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Groundwater" title="Groundwater">groundwater</a> availability of the state is lower than that of <a href="/wiki/Arid" title="Arid">arid</a> states of India. Moreover, Kerala has one of the lowest per capita rainwater availability in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_sub-continent" title="Indian sub-continent">Indian sub-continent</a> and it is still decreasing over the time, even though it receives 3000 mm of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainfall" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a>, which is around 3 times the Indian national average. The high variations in spatial and temporal rainfall add to the complexity of problems associated with water management faced by the State.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rainwater_harvesting" title="Rainwater harvesting">Rainwater harvesting</a>, irrespective of the technology used, essentially means harvesting and storing water in days of abundance, for use in lean days. Storing of rainwater can be done in two ways; (i) storing in an artificial storage and (ii) in the soil media as groundwater. The former is more specifically called roof water harvesting and is rather a temporary measure, focusing on human needs providing immediate relief from drinking water scarcity, while the latter has the potential to provide sustainable relief from water scarcity, addressing the needs of all living classes in nature. Through the proposed individual rainwater harvesting, units will be made available to the beneficiaries. Rain water harvesting has gained popularity in Kerala through various projects implemented by different agencies. The Rain Water Harvesting Campaign of the Government and publicity by various media are responsible for popularizing rain water harvesting in the state. Rainwater harvesting is viewed as a water security measure for the State of Kerala, with two broad types of programmes.</p>
<p>Roof top harvesting</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Components">Components</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Components">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A Roof water harvesting system should have the following components:</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Down_pipe_and_first_flush_arrangement">Down pipe and first flush arrangement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Down pipe and first flush arrangement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is an arrangement to prevent the dust and other unwanted materials that may be seen on the roof, from reaching the storage tank. By fitting a down pipe with an end cap or valve can ensure that the washed water from the roof does not reach the storage tank. It is always safe to ensure that the first flush arrangement remains open during non- rainy days and should be closed after first rain up to the satisfaction of the end user.//</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filter_unit">Filter unit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Filter unit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The rainwater collected from the roof should be allowed to reach the storage tank only through a filtering mechanism. <a href="/wiki/Rubble" title="Rubble">Rubble</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sand" title="Sand">sand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charcoal" title="Charcoal">charcoal</a>, as used in the traditional three-pot <a href="/wiki/Filtration" title="Filtration">filtration</a>, can be adopted here. 10 cm thick 20mm rubble, 10 cm thick charcoal/<a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a> shell, 15 cm thick coarse sand, 5 cm thick 6mm rubble etc. may be arranged from bottom to top in the filter unit. The water that is passed through this filter should remain safe for a long period of storage. Charcoal/coconut shell is added to eliminate gaseous pollutants.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Storage_tank">Storage tank</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Storage tank">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mainly three types of storage tanks are constructed for roof water harvesting. They are above ground, underground or sub surface tanks.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Provision_for_drawing_water_and_spill_over">Provision for drawing water and spill over</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Provision for drawing water and spill over">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For drawing water from the storage tanks any method may be adopted provided it shall be drawn only when needed. The easier the method adopted for drawing water, the more will be the chances for its misuse. Three types of drawing water are usually adopted. They are by using a <a href="/wiki/Tap_(valve)" title="Tap (valve)">tap</a> (small capacity tanks with basement and over ground tanks), <a href="/wiki/Hand_pump" title="Hand pump">hand pump</a> (under ground tanks) or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric_pump" title="Electric pump">electric pump</a> sets (large capacity tanks).</p>
<p><br/></p>
<p>Mostly done on hill slopes of waynad and idukki</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Contour_bounding">Contour bounding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Contour bounding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Crops are cultivated in hills slopes of same height.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organic_fences">Organic fences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Organic fences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Plants like seemakkonna are cultivated as fences between fields. They become manure to crops too.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Check_dams">Check dams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Check dams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>They are created across streams and rivers to partially block the flow of water and allow it to seep into the water table</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bench_terracing">Bench terracing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bench terracing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Done where there is a slope of about 33%. It is a costly method.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stone_pitched_bounds.">Stone pitched bounds.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Stone pitched bounds.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>No farming is done here. Stone bounds are created at a height of1 -1.5 m and soil from top levels the bounds when it rains.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Maintenance">Maintenance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainwater_harvesting_in_Kerala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Maintenance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Before collecting water the roof, gutters and tank should be cleaned.</li>
<li>Let the first 2-3 rains flow out through the first flush system.</li>
<li>Remember to clean the tank once in a year.</li>
<li>Replace the filtering agents every year.</li>
<li>Keep the tank and surroundings clean and hygienic.</li>
<li>Apply white cement on the tank every year.</li>
<li>Make sure that sunlight does not pass through the manhole to prevent <a href="/wiki/Algae" title="Algae">algae</a> growth.</li>
<li>Remember to preserve water and use it judiciously.</li>
</ul>
