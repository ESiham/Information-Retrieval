<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Cagoule</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p>A <b>cagoule</b>, <b>cagoul</b>, <b>kagoule</b> or <b>kagool</b> (from the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> <i>cagoule</i> meaning <a href="/wiki/Balaclava_(clothing)" title="Balaclava (clothing)">balaclava</a>) is the <a href="/wiki/British_English" title="British English">British English</a> term for a lightweight (usually without lining), weatherproof <a href="/wiki/Raincoat" title="Raincoat">raincoat</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anorak" title="Anorak">anorak</a> with a hood, which often comes in knee-length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chambers_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-chambers-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_English" title="Canadian English">Canadian English</a> equivalent is <a href="/wiki/Windbreaker" title="Windbreaker">windbreaker</a> or K-Way.</p>
<p>In some versions, when rolled up, the hood or cross-chest front pocket doubles as a bag into which the shell can be packed.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_styles">History and styles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cagoule&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and styles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A cagoule which could be rolled up into a very compact package and carried in a bag or pocket was <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patented</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT_Leitch_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-DT_Leitch-2">[2]</a></sup> by former <a href="/wiki/Royal_Marines" title="Royal Marines">marine</a> Noel Bibby and launched in the UK under the brand name <a href="/wiki/Peter_Storm_(clothing)" title="Peter Storm (clothing)">Peter Storm</a> in the early 1960s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-invisible_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-invisible-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1965, the French cagoule brand K-Way was introduced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT_Leitch_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-DT_Leitch-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Original versions were lightweight and packable with generally an integral hood, elastic or drawstring cuffs, and a fastening at the neck. Usually, the cagoule could not open fully at the front and was pulled on over the head.</p>
<p>As a functional outdoor rain-garment, the original styling and proportions allowed the wearer's small items of personal luggage to be protected – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rucksack" title="Rucksack">rucksack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fanny_pack" title="Fanny pack">waist bag</a> and/or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camera_bag" title="Camera bag">camera bag</a>.</p>
<p>Later copied and marketed as a close-fitting cheap fashion accessory, the style became very popular in the United Kingdom during the 1970s.</p>
