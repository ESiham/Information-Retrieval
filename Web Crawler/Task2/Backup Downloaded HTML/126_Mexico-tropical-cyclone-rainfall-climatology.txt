<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Mexico tropical cyclone rainfall climatology</h1>

<p><b>Mexico tropical cyclone rainfall climatology</b> discusses precipitation characteristics of tropical cyclones that have struck <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> over the years. One-third of the annual rainfall received along the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Riviera" title="Mexican Riviera">Mexican Riviera</a> and up to half of the rainfall received in <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a> is directly attributable to tropical cyclones moving up the west coast of Mexico. The <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Plateau" title="Mexican Plateau">central plateau</a> is shielded from the high rainfall amounts seen on the oceanward slopes of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Oriental" title="Sierra Madre Oriental">Sierra Madre Oriental</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental" title="Sierra Madre Occidental">Occidental</a> mountain chains.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_characteristics">General characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mexico_tropical_cyclone_rainfall_climatology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: General characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tcaveragepercentofmxrainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="648" data-file-width="816" height="175" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Tcaveragepercentofmxrainfall.gif/220px-Tcaveragepercentofmxrainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Tcaveragepercentofmxrainfall.gif/330px-Tcaveragepercentofmxrainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Tcaveragepercentofmxrainfall.gif/440px-Tcaveragepercentofmxrainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tcaveragepercentofmxrainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Percent of annual rainfall contributed by tropical cyclones from the eastern Pacific Ocean.</div>
</div>

<p>Storms track near and along the western Mexican coastline primarily between the months of July and September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> These storms enhance the monsoon circulation over northwest Mexico and the southwest <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On an average basis, eastern Pacific tropical cyclones contribute about one-third of the annual rainfall along the Mexican Riviera, and up to one-half of the rainfall seen annually across Baja California Sur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Mexico is twice as likely (18% of the basin total) to be impacted by a Pacific tropical cyclone on its west coast than an Atlantic tropical cyclone on its east coast (9% of the basin total). The three most struck states in Mexico in the 50 years at the end of the 20th century were <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>On the western side of Mexico, the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental" title="Sierra Madre Occidental">Sierra Madre Occidental</a> keeps the central <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a> free of excessive rainfall, as tropical cyclones originating in the Eastern Pacific Ocean rain themselves out on the upslope sides of the topography. On the eastern side of Mexico, the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Oriental" title="Sierra Madre Oriental">Sierra Madre Oriental</a> has the same <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Relief_precipitation" title="Relief precipitation">orographic effect</a>, this time blocking tropical disturbances making <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. State maxima relating to tropical cyclones and their remnants are shown on the right, color-coded by amount.</p>
