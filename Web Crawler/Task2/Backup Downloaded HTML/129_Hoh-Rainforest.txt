<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hoh Rainforest</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Hoh Rainforest</b> is located on the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Peninsula" title="Olympic Peninsula">Olympic Peninsula</a> in western <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_(U.S._state)" title="Washington (U.S. state)">Washington</a> state, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">USA</a>. It is one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Temperate_rainforest" title="Temperate rainforest">temperate rainforests</a> in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Within <a href="/wiki/Olympic_National_Park" title="Olympic National Park">Olympic National Park</a>, the forest is protected from commercial exploitation. This includes 24 miles (39 km) of low elevation forest 394 to 2,493 feet (120 to 760 m) along the <a href="/wiki/Hoh_River" title="Hoh River">Hoh River</a>. The Hoh River valley was formed thousands of years ago by glaciers. Between the park boundary and the Pacific Ocean, 48 km (30 mi) of river, much of the forest has been logged within the last century, although many pockets of forest remain.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora">Flora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoh_Rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Flora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The dominant species in the rainforest are Sitka spruce (<i><a href="/wiki/Picea_sitchensis" title="Picea sitchensis">Picea sitchensis</a></i>) and western hemlock (<i><a href="/wiki/Tsuga_heterophylla" title="Tsuga heterophylla">Tsuga heterophylla</a></i>); some grow to tremendous size, reaching 95 meters (312 ft) in height and 7 m (23 ft) in diameter. Coast Douglas-fir (<a href="/wiki/Pseudotsuga_menziesii_var._menziesii" title="Pseudotsuga menziesii var. menziesii"><i>Pseudotsuga menziesii</i> var. <i>menziesii</i></a>), western redcedar (<i><a href="/wiki/Thuja_plicata" title="Thuja plicata">Thuja plicata</a></i>), bigleaf maple (<i><a href="/wiki/Acer_macrophyllum" title="Acer macrophyllum">Acer macrophyllum</a></i>), red alder (<i><a href="/wiki/Alnus_rubra" title="Alnus rubra">Alnus rubra</a></i>), vine maple (<i><a href="/wiki/Acer_circinatum" title="Acer circinatum">Acer circinatum</a></i>), and black cottonwood (<i><a href="/wiki/Populus_trichocarpa" title="Populus trichocarpa">Populus trichocarpa</a></i>) are also found throughout the forest.</p>
<p>Many unique mosses and <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichens</a> are also present in the rainforest, such as lettuce lichen (<i><a href="/wiki/Lobaria_oregana" title="Lobaria oregana">Lobaria oregana</a></i>), which "requires the cool, moist conditions found under the canopy of old-growth forests" and is consumed by deer, elk, and other animals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fauna">Fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoh_Rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many native fauna also make the Hoh Rainforest their home, including the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_tree_frog" title="Pacific tree frog">Pacific tree frog</a> (<i>Pseudacris regilla</i>), <a href="/wiki/Northern_spotted_owl" title="Northern spotted owl">northern spotted owl</a> (<i>Strix occidentalis caurina</i>), <a href="/wiki/Bobcat" title="Bobcat">bobcat</a> (<i>Lynx rufus</i>), <a href="/wiki/Cougar" title="Cougar">cougar</a> (<i>Felis concolor couguar</i>), <a href="/wiki/Raccoon" title="Raccoon">raccoon</a> (<i>Procyon lotor</i>), <a href="/wiki/American_black_bear" title="American black bear">Olympic black bear</a> (<i>Ursus americanus altifrontalis</i>), <a href="/wiki/Roosevelt_elk" title="Roosevelt elk">Roosevelt elk</a> (<i>Cervus canadensis roosevelti</i>), and <a href="/wiki/Black-tailed_deer" title="Black-tailed deer">black-tailed deer</a> (<i>Odocoileus columbianus</i>).</p>
<p>The area is also home to the <a href="/wiki/Banana_slug" title="Banana slug">banana slug</a> (<i>Ariolimax columbianus</i>), which has recently been threatened by the encroachment of a new species of slug, the <a href="/wiki/Black_slug" title="Black slug">black slug</a> (<i>Arion ater</i>), an <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive species</a> from Northern Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights">Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoh_Rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hoh_Rain_Forest_Entrance_Sign.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="3872" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Hoh_Rain_Forest_Entrance_Sign.jpg/220px-Hoh_Rain_Forest_Entrance_Sign.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Hoh_Rain_Forest_Entrance_Sign.jpg/330px-Hoh_Rain_Forest_Entrance_Sign.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Hoh_Rain_Forest_Entrance_Sign.jpg/440px-Hoh_Rain_Forest_Entrance_Sign.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hoh_Rain_Forest_Entrance_Sign.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a> sign at the entrance of the park.</div>

<p>The Hoh Rainforest is home to a National Park Service ranger station, from which backcountry trails extend deeper into the national park.</p>
<p>Near the visitor center is the <a href="/wiki/Hall_of_Mosses_Trail" title="Hall of Mosses Trail">Hall of Mosses Trail</a>, a short trail—0.8 miles (1.3 km)—which gives visitors a feel for the local <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a> and views of maples draped with large growths of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spikemoss" title="Spikemoss">spikemoss</a>. There is also the Spruce Nature Trail (1.2 miles (1.9 km)), which includes signs that identify various trailside trees and plants.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hoh_Rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The novel <i>The Other</i> (2008) by <a href="/wiki/David_Guterson" title="David Guterson">David Guterson</a> centers on a young man who chooses to live in isolation in the Hoh Rainforest, and who comes to be known as "The Hermit of the Hoh."</li>
<li>The Hoh Rainforest is the setting for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Channel" title="National Geographic Channel">National Geographic Channel</a> reality television series <a href="/wiki/Mick_Dodge" title="Mick Dodge">The Legend of Mick Dodge</a>.</li>
</ul>
