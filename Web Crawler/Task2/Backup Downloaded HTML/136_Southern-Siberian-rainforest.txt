<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Southern Siberian rainforest</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Southern Siberian rainforest</b> is an area of <a href="/wiki/Temperate_rainforest" title="Temperate rainforest">temperate rainforest</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Central_Siberia" title="South Central Siberia">South Central Siberia</a> that occurs primarily along the <a href="/wiki/Altai_Mountains" title="Altai Mountains">Altai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sayan_Mountains" title="Sayan Mountains">Sayan mountain</a> ranges in <a href="/wiki/Khakassia" title="Khakassia">Khakassia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tuva" title="Tuva">Tuva</a> as well as a small area in the <a href="/wiki/Chamar-Daban" title="Chamar-Daban">Chamar-Daban</a> Mountains near <a href="/wiki/Lake_Baikal" title="Lake Baikal">Lake Baikal</a> in <a href="/wiki/Buryatia" title="Buryatia">Buryatia</a>. The forest encompasses a total area of approximately <a href="/wiki/Orders_of_magnitude_(area)#108_to_1014_square_metres" title="Orders of magnitude (area)">6,000 square kilometres (2,300 sq mi)</a>. The larger portion of the forest in the Altai and Sayan Mountains runs across a latitude range that encompasses between 51.5 degrees to 56 degrees north latitude, and a longitude range running between 86 degrees to 95 degrees east longitude. The region overlaps with the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Mountains_of_Altai" title="Golden Mountains of Altai">Golden Mountains of Altai</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Heritage" title="World Heritage">World Heritage</a> Site. Ecological zones range from <a href="/wiki/Hemiboreal" title="Hemiboreal">hemiboreal</a> forest to a <a href="/wiki/Forest_steppe" title="Forest steppe">forest-steppe ecotone</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dellasala_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-dellasala-1">[1]</a></sup> and include a wider variety of plant species than surrounding areas.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Siberian_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The southern Siberian rainforest is primarily located within the Altai and Sayan mountain ranges across a range of elevations. Geographic features that the forest extends onto include high mountains and ridges as well as valleys carved by <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">glaciers</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Katunsky_Zapovednik_2013_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Katunsky_Zapovednik_2013-2">[2]</a></sup> and river basins. The forest-steppe ecotone occurs at elevations as low as 250 to 300 metres (820 feet to 985 feet) while the forest-tundra boundary is between 1600 and 1800 metres (5250 feet to 5905 feet). The <a href="/wiki/Katun_River" title="Katun River">Katun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biya_River" title="Biya River">Biya</a> Rivers, two tributaries of the Ob River that originate in the Altai Mountains, flow through the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kharuk.2C_Vyacheslav_I._2010_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kharuk.2C_Vyacheslav_I._2010-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora">Flora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Siberian_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Flora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Flora in the region represent a combination of species from four biomes: taiga, сool-temperate forests, tundra and steppe and are more diverse than flora of many of the surrounding areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The trees include a mix of coniferous and broadleaf tree species. These include <a href="/wiki/Scots_pine" title="Scots pine">Scots pine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Betula_pendula" title="Betula pendula">silver birch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Populus_tremula" title="Populus tremula">Eurasian aspen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pinus_sibirica" title="Pinus sibirica">Siberian pine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abies_sibirica" title="Abies sibirica">Siberian fir</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dellasala_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-dellasala-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Picea_obovata" title="Picea obovata">Siberian spruce</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tilia_sibirica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tilia sibirica (page does not exist)">Siberian linden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chytry_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-chytry-5">[5]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Amelin_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Amelin-6">[6]</a></sup> The hemiboreal zones are dominated by Scots Pine and silver birch in wet areas. Moss cover is present, but sparse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chytry_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-chytry-5">[5]</a></sup> Dry areas are dominated by either Scots pine or Siberian larch depending on temperature. Siberian larch is typically more prevalent in cooler areas while warmer and drier ares favor Scots pine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Siberian larch also dominates the forest-steppe ecotone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kharuk.2C_Vyacheslav_I._2010_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kharuk.2C_Vyacheslav_I._2010-3">[3]</a></sup> Epiphyte species include multiple genera of lichen. These include nitrophyte genera such as Physcia and Melanelia, acidophytes such as Usnea and Bryoria and other genera including Hypogymnia<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Sticta" title="Sticta">Sticta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dellasala_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-dellasala-1">[1]</a></sup> Understory plants include <a href="/wiki/Actaea_spicata" title="Actaea spicata">Eurasian baneberry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asarum_europaeum" title="Asarum europaeum">European wild ginger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anemone_baicalensis" title="Anemone baicalensis">Baikal anemone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chrysosplenium" title="Chrysosplenium">golden saxifrage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardamine" title="Cardamine">bittercress</a> and grasses of the genera <i><a href="/wiki/Carex" title="Carex">Carex</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Brachypodium" title="Brachypodium">Brachypodium</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dellasala_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-dellasala-1">[1]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Calamagrostis" title="Calamagrostis">Calamagrostis</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ivanova.2C_G._A._2010_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ivanova.2C_G._A._2010-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fauna">Fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Siberian_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Terrestrial animals in the region include <a href="/wiki/Brown_bear" title="Brown bear">brown bears</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_adder" title="European adder">European adders</a>, moles of the <a href="/wiki/Talpa_(genus)" title="Talpa (genus)">Talpa</a> genus, <a href="/wiki/Red_fox" title="Red fox">red foxes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red_deer" title="Red deer">red deer</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Earth_2013_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Earth_2013-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gray_wolf" title="Gray wolf">gray wolves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lynx" title="Lynx">lynx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mountain_hare" title="Mountain hare">mountain hares</a>, and several species of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mustelid" title="Mustelid">mustelids</a> including <a href="/wiki/Stoat" title="Stoat">stoats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wolverine" title="Wolverine">wolverines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Katunsky_Zapovednik_2013_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Katunsky_Zapovednik_2013-2">[2]</a></sup> Bird species include <a href="/wiki/Hazel_grouse" title="Hazel grouse">hazel grouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tree_pipit" title="Tree pipit">tree pipits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_three-toed_woodpecker" title="Eurasian three-toed woodpecker">Eurasian three-toed woodpeckers</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Earth_2013_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Earth_2013-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Golden_eagle" title="Golden eagle">golden eagles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_falcon" title="Peregrine falcon">peregrine falcons</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Katunsky_Zapovednik_2013_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Katunsky_Zapovednik_2013-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Black_stork" title="Black stork">black storks</a>, northern eagle owls, and <a href="/wiki/Gyrfalcon" title="Gyrfalcon">gyrfalcons</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Aquatic species include <a href="/wiki/Moor_frog" title="Moor frog">moor frogs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hucho_taimen" title="Hucho taimen">Siberian taimen</a>, fish of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Cottus_(genus)" title="Cottus (genus)">Cottus</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Earth_2013_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-Earth_2013-11">[11]</a></sup> Siberian grayling, <a href="/wiki/Lenok" title="Lenok">Asiatic trout</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_otter" title="European otter">European otters</a>. Insect fauna include the <a href="/wiki/Dendrolimus_superans" title="Dendrolimus superans">Siberian silk moth</a>, a plant-eating insect species that lives in the region and feeds on tree leaves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kharuk.2C_V._I._2009_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kharuk.2C_V._I._2009-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Siberian_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The climate in the Siberian temperate forest is continental and humid along the Altai and Sayan mountain ranges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Winter temperatures can be as low as -19 degrees Celsius (-2 degrees Fahrenheit) and summer temperatures can reach as high as 17 degrees (63 degrees Fahrenheit).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hulme_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-hulme-15">[15]</a></sup> The region primarily gets precipitation from Atlantic air masses brought in by westerly winds. The climates are more moderate nearby Teletskoje lake and Baikal. Lake Baikal has the effect of cooling incoming air as it reaches the Chamar-Daban Mountains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dellasala_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-dellasala-1">[1]</a></sup> The incoming air is forced over the mountains, causing moisture to precipitate out. This causes greater annual precipitation in this area than in the surrounding regions, and can exceed 1500 millimeters annually in some places.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dellasala_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-dellasala-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hulme_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-hulme-15">[15]</a></sup> In addition, relative humidity can average as high as 75% on an annual basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hulme_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-hulme-15">[15]</a></sup> The region also loses more water to evapotranspiration than surrounding areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Current climate change projections predict that local temperatures will increase, causing the forest to expand into nearby alpine tundra areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kharuk.2C_Vyacheslav_I._2010_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kharuk.2C_Vyacheslav_I._2010-3">[3]</a></sup> Climate change is also expected to result in decreased precipitation in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ivanova.2C_G._A._2010_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ivanova.2C_G._A._2010-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Threats">Threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Siberian_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Conifer trees in the region have been industrially logged, sometimes illegally,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> for more than a century. Clear cutting was particularly common with Siberian pine, which was seen as particularly valuable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dellasala_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-dellasala-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The removal of these trees is a cause of increased soil erosion due to a lack of roots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Fire also is a disturbance that is most common in areas with Scots pine stands and is expected to increase in frequency as temperatures rise due to climate change.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ivanova.2C_G._A._2010_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ivanova.2C_G._A._2010-9">[9]</a></sup> Forest regeneration in these areas is hindered by the faster growth and spread of grasses, particularly the genus <i>Calamagrostis</i>. Insect outbreaks are a threat to many of the tree species in the region. One example of this type of disturbance is the Siberian silkmoth, a species of moth that eats the leaves and needles of broadleaf and coniferous trees respectively. Its most recent outbreak was in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kharuk.2C_V._I._2009_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kharuk.2C_V._I._2009-13">[13]</a></sup> Tree mortality from insect outbreaks and fungal infestations further increases the risk of forest fires due to the increase of combustible material from dead trees, including the trunks which can act as ladder fuel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ivanova.2C_G._A._2010_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-Ivanova.2C_G._A._2010-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Siberian_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Golden Mountains of Altai World Heritage Site includes part of the forest in the Altai Mountains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The Russian government has also set up multiple <a href="/wiki/Zapovednik" title="Zapovednik">nature reserves</a>. Three are in the Altai Mountains: the Altaysky and Katunsky Nature Reserves, which are also in the Golden Mountains of Altai World Heritage Site,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Katunsky_Zapovednik_2013_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Katunsky_Zapovednik_2013-2">[2]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Ubsunur_Hollow_Biosphere_Reserve" title="Ubsunur Hollow Biosphere Reserve">Ubsunur Hollow Biosphere Reserve</a>, which is also a World Heritage Site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The Kuznetskiy Alatau Nature reserve is located near the Sayan Mountains. The region near Lake Baikal includes the Lake Baikal Nature Reserve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dellasala_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-dellasala-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
