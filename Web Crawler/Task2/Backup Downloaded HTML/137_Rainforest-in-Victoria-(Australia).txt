<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Rainforest in Victoria (Australia)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> contains approximately 32,000 hectares<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Temperate_rainforest" title="Temperate rainforest">temperate rainforest</a> in various regions. The areas with rainforest include: <a href="/wiki/East_Gippsland" title="East Gippsland">East Gippsland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strzelecki_Ranges" title="Strzelecki Ranges">Strzelecki Ranges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wilsons_Promontory" title="Wilsons Promontory">Wilsons Promontory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_Highlands_(Victoria)" title="Central Highlands (Victoria)">Central Highlands</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Otway_Ranges" title="Otway Ranges">Otway Ranges</a>. The rainforests vary between cool temperate, warm temperate, and mixed cool temperate.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Protection">Protection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainforest_in_Victoria_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Protection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Victorian forests subject to timber harvesting operations rainforest is protected by the Code of Practice for Timber Production (DSE 2007), which requires that:</p>
<dl>
<dd>"Rainforest communities in Victoria must not be harvested. Rainforest communities must be protected from the impacts of harvesting through the use of appropriate buffers to maintain microclimatic conditions and protect from disease and other disturbance."</dd>
</dl>
<p>However, mixed cool temperate rainforest has not been completely mapped in Victoria, and no pre-logging surveys occur to check for this forest type. Old growth forest, with trees dating from pre-1900s, are also meant to be protected.</p>
<p>Victorian rainforests occurring inside national parks are protected from logging.</p>
<p>Rainforest does not regenerate following clearfell, <a href="/wiki/Clearcutting" title="Clearcutting">coupe logging</a>, which is practiced in Victoria.</p>
