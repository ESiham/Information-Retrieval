<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Tasmanian temperate rainforests</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_temperate_rain_forests&amp;redirect=no" title="Tasmanian temperate rain forests">Tasmanian temperate rain forests</a>)</span></div>

<p>The <b>Tasmanian temperate rain forests</b> are part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australasia_ecozone" title="Australasia ecozone">Australasia ecozone</a>, which includes Tasmania and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a>, and adjacent islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-busby_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-busby-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rainforest communities in Australia are classified as closed forests in which the canopy comprises 70–100% cover.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reid_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-reid-2">[2]</a></sup> It can be divided into tropical, subtropical, monsoon and temperate rainforest. Tasmanian rainforest is classified and as <b>cool temperate rainforest</b>, it represents the most floristically complex and best developed form of this forest type in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In Tasmania, they can be found in the West, <a href="/wiki/Savage_River_National_Park" title="Savage River National Park">Savage River National Park</a>, South West, North East and in patches on the East Coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reid_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-reid-2">[2]</a></sup> On the mainland of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <b>cool temperate rainforest</b> have a wide variety of woodland trees, <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a> only has a limited number of woodland and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vascular_plants" title="Vascular plants">vascular plants</a> such as mosses, liverworts, lichen and fungi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jarman_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-jarman-4">[4]</a></sup> Because of this, the definition of Tasmanian <b>cool temperate rainforest</b> was redefined in the 1980s to allow for communities that did not meet the canopy requirements and clearly separate <b>cool temperate rainforest</b> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mixed_forest" title="Mixed forest">mixed forest</a>; The current definition states that <b>cool temperate rainforest</b> are those with trees usually greater than 8m in height and capable of regenerating in the absence of large scale catastrophic events such as fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reid_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-reid-2">[2]</a></sup> These forests are climax vegetation and are dominated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angiosperms" title="Angiosperms">angiosperms</a> (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_cunninghamii" title="Nothofagus cunninghamii">Nothofagus cunninghamii</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atherosperma_moschatum" title="Atherosperma moschatum">Atherosperma moschatum</a></i> and <i>Eucryphia lucida</i>) as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gymnosperms" title="Gymnosperms">gymnosperms</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Athrotaxis_selaginoides" title="Athrotaxis selaginoides">Athrotaxis selaginoides</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lagarostrobos_franklinii" title="Lagarostrobos franklinii">Lagarostrobos franklinii</a></i> and <i>Phyllocaldues aspleniifolius</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tabor_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tabor-5">[5]</a></sup> The limited number of woody species is thought to be due to repeated glaciation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worth_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-worth-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tasmanian cool temperate rainforest can be divided into four types: Callidendrous rainforest, Thamnic rainforest, Implicate rainforest and Open Montane. These four major types differ in many of their characteristics such as structure, floristics, distribution, level of <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemism</a> and ecology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reid_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-reid-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_composition_and_descriptions">Community composition and descriptions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_temperate_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Community composition and descriptions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tasmanian cool temperate rainforest is composed of two alliances, the myrtle-beech <i>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_cunninghamii" title="Nothofagus cunninghamii">Nothofagus cunninghamii</a>)</i> alliance and the pencil pine (<i><a href="/wiki/Athrotaxis_cupressoides" title="Athrotaxis cupressoides">Athrotaxis cupressoides</a></i>). The former is made up of callidendrous, thamnic and implicate, whilst the latter is all open montane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jarman2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-jarman2-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Callidendrous_rainforest">Callidendrous rainforest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_temperate_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Callidendrous rainforest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is the simplest rainforest community in <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>; It is represented typically by medium to tall forests dominated by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_cunninghamii" title="Nothofagus cunninghamii">Nothofagus cunninghamii</a></i> and/or <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atherosperma_moschatum" title="Atherosperma moschatum">Atherosperma moschatum</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Sassafras" title="Sassafras">sassafras</a>), <i>Leptospermum lanigerum</i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Acacia_melanoxylon" title="Acacia melanoxylon">Acacia melanoxylon</a></i> have also been found to accompany them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neyland_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Neyland-8">[8]</a></sup> Typically, these forests are characterised as &gt; 40m in height.</p>
<p>Trees are usually well formed and widely spaced, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Understorey" title="Understorey">understorey</a> is open and often described as 'park like'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reid_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-reid-2">[2]</a></sup> The diversity of woody species is low, they are usually sparse and inconspicuous throughout the understorey of most communities. However, at mid to low altitudes, the understorey may contain a few small trees or shrubs; <i>Olearia argophylla, Pimelea drupacea, Pittsporum bicolor, Aristotelia peduncularis</i> and <i>Comprosma quadrifida</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brown_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-brown-9">[9]</a></sup> Fern diversity is usually high in many areas, in areas where tree ferns provide a suitable substrate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epiphytes" title="Epiphytes">epiphytes</a> also flourish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brown_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-brown-9">[9]</a></sup> The dominant tree ferns are commonly <i><a href="/wiki/Dicksonia_antarctica" title="Dicksonia antarctica">Dicksonia antarctica</a></i> and/or <i>Polystichum poliferum</i>. Common <a href="/wiki/Epiphyte" title="Epiphyte">epiphyte</a> species include <i><a href="/wiki/Rumohra_adiantiformis" title="Rumohra adiantiformis">Rumohra adiantiformis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asplenium_bulbiferum" title="Asplenium bulbiferum">Asplenium bulbiferum</a>, Asplenium terrestre, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microsorum_diversifolium" title="Microsorum diversifolium">Microsorum diversifolium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hymenophyllum_flabellatum" title="Hymenophyllum flabellatum">Hymenophyllum flabellatum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hymenophyllum_australe" title="Hymenophyllum australe">Hymenophyllum australe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hymenophyllum_cupressiforme" title="Hymenophyllum cupressiforme">Hymenophyllum cupressiforme</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polyphlebium_venosum" title="Polyphlebium venosum">Polyphlebium venosum</a></i> and many more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kantvilas_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kantvilas-10">[10]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Histiopteris_incisa" title="Histiopteris incisa">Histiopteris incisa</a></i> and <i>Hypolepis rugosula</i> occur in disturbed sites such as along roads where there have been breaks in the canopy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hill_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-hill-11">[11]</a></sup> As altitude increases, the height of the forest decreases,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read3_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-read3-12">[12]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_cunninghamii" title="Nothofagus cunninghamii">Nothofagus cunninghamii</a></i> becomes multi-stemmed, develops crooked leaning stems and abundant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epiphytic" title="Epiphytic">epiphytic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bryophytes" title="Bryophytes">bryophytes</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lichens" title="Lichens">lichens</a> produces a patterned effect on the trunks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-morely_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-morely-13">[13]</a></sup> The typical understorey becomes dominated by <i><a href="/wiki/Tasmannia_lanceolata" title="Tasmannia lanceolata">Tasmannia lanceolata</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Telopea_truncata" title="Telopea truncata">Telopea truncata</a></i>, the ground layer becomes host to <i>Oxalis megellancia</i> and <i>Lagenifera stipitata</i>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pteridophytes" title="Pteridophytes">pteridophytes</a> diversity decreases and only small species such as <i><a href="/wiki/Lycopodium_fastigiatum" title="Lycopodium fastigiatum">Lycopodium fastigiatum</a>, Hymenophyllum peltatum, Grammitits billardierei</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Blechnum_penna-marina" title="Blechnum penna-marina">Blechnum penna-marina</a></i> persist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hickey_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-hickey-14">[14]</a></sup> – occurs in the eastern half of the state and in the northwestern and central Tasmania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-read-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Thamnic_rainforest">Thamnic rainforest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_temperate_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Thamnic rainforest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Characterised by well formed trees of medium height and a distinct shrub layer, the forest height is well below 40m. The canopy is commonly dominated by a mixture of 2–5 species, these include combinations of: <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_cunninghamii" title="Nothofagus cunninghamii">Nothofagus cunninghamii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eucryphia_lucida" title="Eucryphia lucida">Eucryphia lucida</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atherosperma_moschatum" title="Atherosperma moschatum">Atherosperma moschatum</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lagarostrobos_franklinii" title="Lagarostrobos franklinii">Lagarostrobos franklinii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eucryphia_milliganii" title="Eucryphia milliganii">Eucryphia milliganii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phyllocladus_aspleniifolius" title="Phyllocladus aspleniifolius">Phyllocladus aspleniifolius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Athrotaxis_selaginoides" title="Athrotaxis selaginoides">Athrotaxis selaginoides</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_gunnii" title="Nothofagus gunnii">Nothofagus gunnii</a></i> (rarely).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brown_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-brown-9">[9]</a></sup> The shrub layer is more prominent and has an increased diversity of woody trees. Common species are: <i><a href="/wiki/Anopterus_glandulosus" title="Anopterus glandulosus">Anopterus glandulosus</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anodopetalum_biglandulosum" title="Anodopetalum biglandulosum">Anodopetalum biglandulosum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acradenia_frankliniae" title="Acradenia frankliniae">Acradenia frankliniae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archeria_hirtella" title="Archeria hirtella">Archeria hirtella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archeria_eriocarpa" title="Archeria eriocarpa">Archeria eriocarpa</a>, Cenarrhenes nitida, Trochocarpa cunninghamii, Trochocarpa gunnii, Orites diversifolia</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prionotes_cerinthoides" title="Prionotes cerinthoides">Prionotes cerinthoides</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Richea_pandanifolia" title="Richea pandanifolia">Richea pandanifolia</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Richea_scoparia" title="Richea scoparia">Richea scoparia</a></i> (at high altitudes only).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brown_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-brown-9">[9]</a></sup> The increase in understorey shrub for this rainforest type is due to the larger numbers of gaps in the canopy and the differing light requirements of competing species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read4_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-read4-16">[16]</a></sup> Fern diversity decreases in this type because of the decrease in light from the understorey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brodribb_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brodribb-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Blechnum_wattsii" title="Blechnum wattsii">Blechnum wattsii</a> is the main ground fern and is able to survive as it prefers damp shaded areas on forest floor; it forms a very dense layer in some areas but can be absent in others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coates_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-coates-18">[18]</a></sup> Small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epiphytes" title="Epiphytes">epiphytes</a> are widespread, these include <i>Hymenophyllum rarum</i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammitis_billardierei" title="Grammitis billardierei">Grammitis billardierei</a></i>, <i>Apteropteris applanata</i> can occur in areas containing <i>Athrotaxis</i>. Other larger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epiphytes" title="Epiphytes">epiphytes</a> are usually present, but they are rarely prominent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kantvilas2_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kantvilas2-19">[19]</a></sup> At higher altitudes, the general appearance resembles that of a callidenderous rainforest, but the floristic differences remain the same. – occur mostly in western and southwestern Tasmania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-read-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Implicate_rainforest">Implicate rainforest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_temperate_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Implicate rainforest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Low in stature, broken uneven canopies, height reduced below 20m. Dominance is usually shared between several species: <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_cunninghamii" title="Nothofagus cunninghamii">Nothofagus cunninghamii</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_gunnii" title="Nothofagus gunnii">Nothofagus gunnii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eucryphia_lucida" title="Eucryphia lucida">Eucryphia lucida</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atherosperma_moschatum" title="Atherosperma moschatum">Atherosperma moschatum</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lagarostrobos_franklinii" title="Lagarostrobos franklinii">Lagarostrobos franklinii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eucryphia_milliganii" title="Eucryphia milliganii">Eucryphia milliganii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phyllocladus_aspleniifolius" title="Phyllocladus aspleniifolius">Phyllocladus aspleniifolius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Athrotaxis_selaginoides" title="Athrotaxis selaginoides">Athrotaxis selaginoides</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diselma_archeri" title="Diselma archeri">Diselma archeri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leptospermum_scoparium" title="Leptospermum scoparium">Leptospermum scoparium</a>, Leptospermum glaucescens, <a href="/wiki/Leptospermum_nitidum" title="Leptospermum nitidum">Leptospermum nitidum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leptospermum_lanigerum" title="Leptospermum lanigerum">Leptospermum lanigerum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acacia_mucronata" title="Acacia mucronata">Acacia mucronata</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Melaleuca_squarrosa" title="Melaleuca squarrosa">Melaleuca squarrosa</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jarman_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-jarman-4">[4]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atherosperma_moschatum" title="Atherosperma moschatum">Atherosperma moschatum</a></i> is usually represented by small diameter depauperate plants. Understorey is tangled and barely distinguishable from the canopy layer. It can from a continuous layer from the ground to the canopy but some scattered emergents can occur. Species diversity is high for trees and shrubs in this community, but very low for ferns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jarman_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-jarman-4">[4]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Anopterus_glandulosus" title="Anopterus glandulosus">Anopterus glandulosus</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anodopetalum_biglandulosum" title="Anodopetalum biglandulosum">Anodopetalum biglandulosum</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Cenarrhenes nitida, <a href="/wiki/Telopea_truncata" title="Telopea truncata">Telopea truncata</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agastachys_odorata" title="Agastachys odorata">Agastachys odorata</a>, Comprosma nitida, <a href="/wiki/Archeria_eriocarpa" title="Archeria eriocarpa">Archeria eriocarpa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archeria_serpyllifolia" title="Archeria serpyllifolia">Archeria serpyllifolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archeria_hirtella" title="Archeria hirtella">Archeria hirtella</a>, Olearia persoonioides, Trochocarpa cunninghamii, Trochocarpa gunnii, <a href="/wiki/Richea_pandanifolia" title="Richea pandanifolia">Richea pandanifolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richea_scoparia" title="Richea scoparia">Richea scoparia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dracophyllum_milliganii" title="Dracophyllum milliganii">Dracophyllum milliganii</a></i> and <i>Prionotes cerinthoides</i> are all typical shrubs or small trees that comprise these communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brown_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-brown-9">[9]</a></sup> The ferns are dominated by <a href="/wiki/Blechnum_wattsii" title="Blechnum wattsii">Blechnum wattsii</a> but small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epiphytes" title="Epiphytes">epiphytes</a> do prevail: <i>Hymenophyllum rarum, Hymenophyllum marginatum, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammitis_billardierei" title="Grammitis billardierei">Grammitis billardierei</a> and Apteropteris applanata</i> occurs in communities where <i><a href="/wiki/Athrotaxis" title="Athrotaxis">Athrotaxis</a></i> are present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> – occur mostly in western and southwestern Tasmania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-read-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_montane_rainforest">Open montane rainforest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_temperate_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Open montane rainforest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Low and dominated by <i><a href="/wiki/Athrotaxis_cupressoides" title="Athrotaxis cupressoides">Athrotaxis cupressoides</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Athrotaxis_selaginoides" title="Athrotaxis selaginoides">Athrotaxis selaginoides</a></i> (less commonly).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cullen_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-cullen-22">[22]</a></sup> Open canopy, widely spaced trees allow bright light to penetrate the lower levels of the forest. In some communities, the canopy can be dense and instead resemble a high altitude callidendrous forest. Understorey may be dominated by either <i><a href="/wiki/Poa" title="Poa">Poa</a></i> (grasses) or <i><a href="/wiki/Sphagnum" title="Sphagnum">Sphagnum</a></i> (mosses) or commonly, low shrubs that are less than half the height of the forest. There is a high diversity of woody species but a low diversity of ferns. Multiple species from nearby treeless vegetation are present, but their classification as rainforest species is yet to be confirmed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-busby_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-busby-1">[1]</a></sup> Understorey shrubs include: <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_cunninghamii" title="Nothofagus cunninghamii">Nothofagus cunninghamii</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_gunnii" title="Nothofagus gunnii">Nothofagus gunnii</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diselma_archeri" title="Diselma archeri">Diselma archeri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Podocarpus_lawrencei" title="Podocarpus lawrencei">Podocarpus lawrencei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richea_pandanifolia" title="Richea pandanifolia">Richea pandanifolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richea_scoparia" title="Richea scoparia">Richea scoparia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richea_sprengelioides" title="Richea sprengelioides">Richea sprengelioides</a>, Orites acicularis, Orites revoluta, Microstobos niphophilus, <a href="/wiki/Tasmannia_lanceolata" title="Tasmannia lanceolata">Tasmannia lanceolata</a>, Epacris serpyllifolia</i> and <i>Baeckea gunniana</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brown_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-brown-9">[9]</a></sup> Ferns are usually poorly developed with larger ground species absent or confined to rocks, <i><a href="/wiki/Gleichenia_alpina" title="Gleichenia alpina">Gleichenia alpina</a></i> is the exception to this observation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jarman_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-jarman-4">[4]</a></sup> Small species such as <i>Hymenophyllum peltatum</i> and <i>Apteropteris applanata</i> can sometimes make an appearance. – occur on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Plateau_(Tasmania)" title="Central Plateau (Tasmania)">Central Plateau</a> but can extend as small outliners to the mountains further south.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-read-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biological_factors">Biological factors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_temperate_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Biological factors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Geology and soils are important factors in Tasmanian ecology:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read_15-4"><a href="#cite_note-read-15">[15]</a></sup> Callidendrous forest require good quality sites where fertile soils occur over rocks such as basalt, dolerite and the more nutrient rich granites; Implicate forests are at the other extreme and moslty grow in organic soils or mineral soils derived from nutrient poor rock types such as quartzites and silicous conglomerates, poorly drained areas are also suitable;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read2_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-read2-23">[23]</a></sup> The Thamnic forests tend to prefer an intermediate substrate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read3_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-read3-12">[12]</a></sup> With the exception of Open Montane forests, altitude seems to have little to no effect on the floristic differences between the different community types.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reid_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-reid-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-read_15-5"><a href="#cite_note-read-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasmanian_temperate_rainforests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1982, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Educational,_Scientific_and_Cultural_Organization" title="United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization">United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization</a> (UNESCO) designated the northern portion of the ecoregion a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>.</p>
