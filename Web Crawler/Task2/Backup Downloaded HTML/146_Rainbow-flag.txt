<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Rainbow flag</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>A <b>rainbow flag</b> is a multicolored <a href="/wiki/Flag" title="Flag">flag</a> consisting of the colors of the <a href="/wiki/Rainbow" title="Rainbow">rainbow</a>. The actual colors shown differ, but many of the designs are based on the traditional scheme of <a href="/wiki/Red" title="Red">red</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orange_(color)" title="Orange (color)">orange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yellow" title="Yellow">yellow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green" title="Green">green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blue" title="Blue">blue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indigo" title="Indigo">indigo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Violet_(color)" title="Violet (color)">violet</a>, or some more modern division of the rainbow spectrum (often excluding indigo, and sometimes including <a href="/wiki/Cyan" title="Cyan">cyan</a> instead).</p>
<p>There are several independent rainbow flags in use today. The most widely known worldwide is the <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_flag_(LGBT_movement)" title="Rainbow flag (LGBT movement)">pride flag</a> representing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LGBT_pride" title="LGBT pride">LGBT pride</a> (since 1978). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peace_rainbow_flag" title="Peace rainbow flag">peace flag</a> is especially popular in Italy (since 1961). The <a href="/wiki/International_Co-operative_Alliance" title="International Co-operative Alliance">International Co-operative Alliance</a> adopted a rainbow flag in 1925. A <a href="/wiki/Wiphala" title="Wiphala">similar flag</a> is used in Andean indigenism in <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> to represent the legacy of the <a href="/wiki/Inca_Empire" title="Inca Empire">Inca Empire</a> (since ca. 1920).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rainbow_flags_in_various_cultures_and_movements">Rainbow flags in various cultures and movements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Rainbow flags in various cultures and movements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reformation">Reformation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reformation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The reformer <a href="/wiki/Thomas_M%C3%BCntzer" title="Thomas Müntzer">Thomas Müntzer</a> (1489–1525) connected socially revolutionary claims with his preaching of the gospel. He is often portrayed with a rainbow flag in his hand. The Thomas Müntzer statue in the German town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stolberg,_Saxony-Anhalt" title="Stolberg, Saxony-Anhalt">Stolberg</a> also shows him holding a rainbow flag in his hand. In the <a href="/wiki/German_Peasants%27_War" title="German Peasants' War">German Peasants' War</a> of the 16th century, the rainbow flag together with the peasants' boot ("Bundschuh") was used as the sign of a new era, of hope and of <a href="/wiki/Social_change" title="Social change">social change</a>. The choice of the rainbow in the form of a flag harkens back to the rainbow as a symbol of <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">biblical</a> promise. According to the Bible, <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a> first created the rainbow as a sign to <a href="/wiki/Noah" title="Noah">Noah</a> that there would never again be a worldwide flood,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> also known as the <a href="/wiki/Genesis_flood_narrative#Rainbow_covenant" title="Genesis flood narrative">Rainbow covenant</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Thomas_Paine">Thomas Paine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Thomas Paine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> writer <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Paine" title="Thomas Paine">Thomas Paine</a> had proposed that the rainbow flag be used as a maritime flag, to signify neutral ships in time of war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Buddhist_flag_(1885)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Buddhist_flag_.281885.29">Buddhist flag (1885)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Buddhist flag (1885)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Buddhist_flag" title="Buddhist flag">Buddhist flag</a></div>
<p>A flag to represent <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a> was designed in <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> in 1885 and modified to its current form in 1886. In 1950 it was adopted by the <a href="/wiki/World_Fellowship_of_Buddhists" title="World Fellowship of Buddhists">World Fellowship of Buddhists</a> to be a symbol of all forms of Buddhism around the world.</p>
<p>It consists of six vertical colored segments, the first five of which are usually blue, yellow, red, white, and orange, while the sixth is a combination of the first five. Variant colors are often found.</p>
<h3><span id="First_Armenian_Republic_proposal_(1919)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Armenian_Republic_proposal_.281919.29">First Armenian Republic proposal (1919)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: First Armenian Republic proposal (1919)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Saryan---Flag-Rainbow.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="900" data-file-width="1350" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Saryan---Flag-Rainbow.png/220px-Saryan---Flag-Rainbow.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Saryan---Flag-Rainbow.png/330px-Saryan---Flag-Rainbow.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Saryan---Flag-Rainbow.png/440px-Saryan---Flag-Rainbow.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Saryan---Flag-Rainbow.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rainbow flag proposed for Armenia by artist Martiros Saryan.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Rainbow flag proposed after <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> regained independence after World War I. It was designed by famous Armenian artist <a href="/wiki/Martiros_Saryan" title="Martiros Saryan">Martiros Saryan</a>. It was not adopted as the country instead went with three stripes using the colors used in a past Armenian kingdom. The artists used muted, richer, "oriental" colors reflecting Armenian fabrics and carpets.</p>
<h3><span id="Cooperative_movement_(1921)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Cooperative_movement_.281921.29">Cooperative movement (1921)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cooperative movement (1921)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ICA_flag.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="140" data-file-width="210" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/ICA_flag.svg/220px-ICA_flag.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/ICA_flag.svg/330px-ICA_flag.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/ICA_flag.svg/440px-ICA_flag.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ICA_flag.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Until 2001, the <a href="/wiki/International_Co-operative_Alliance" title="International Co-operative Alliance">International Co-operative Alliance</a> used a rainbow flag</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A seven-colour rainbow flag is a common symbol of the international <a href="/wiki/Cooperative" title="Cooperative">cooperative</a> movement. The rainbow flag has been the cooperative emblem since 1921 when the International Co-operative Congress of World Co-op Leaders met in <a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel, Switzerland</a> to identify and define the growing cooperative movement’s common values and ideals to help unite co-ops around the world.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Essen" title="Essen">Essen, Germany</a> in 1922, the <a href="/wiki/International_Co-operative_Alliance" title="International Co-operative Alliance">International Co-operative Alliance</a> (ICA) designed an international co-op symbol and a flag for the first "Co-operators' Day," which was held in July 1923. After some experiments with different designs, a famous French cooperator, Professor <a href="/wiki/Charles_Gide" title="Charles Gide">Charles Gide</a>, suggested using the seven colours of the rainbow for the flag. He pointed out that the rainbow symbolized <a href="/wiki/Unity_in_diversity" title="Unity in diversity">unity in diversity</a> and the power of light, enlightenment and progress. The first co-op rainbow flag was completed in 1924 and was adopted as an official symbol of the international cooperative movement in 1925.</p>
<p>In 2001, the <a href="/wiki/International_Co-operative_Alliance" title="International Co-operative Alliance">ICA</a>'s official flag was changed from a rainbow flag to a rainbow logo flag on a white field, to clearly promote and strengthen the cooperative image, but still use the rainbow image. Other organizations sometimes use the traditional rainbow flag as a symbol of cooperation.</p>
<p>Like the <a href="/wiki/Rainbow" title="Rainbow">rainbow</a>, this flag is a symbol of hope and peace. The seven colours from flags around the world fly in harmony. Each of the seven colours in the co-operative flag have been assigned the following meaning:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<ul>
<li>red: stands for courage;</li>
<li>orange: offers the vision of possibilities;</li>
<li>yellow: represents the challenge that GREEN has kindled;</li>
<li>green: indicates a challenge to co-operators to strive for growth of membership and of understanding of the aims and values of co-operation;</li>
<li>light blue: suggests far horizons, the need to provide education and help less fortunate people and strive toward global unity.</li>
<li>dark blue: suggests pessimism: a reminder that less fortunate people have needs that may be met through the benefits of cooperation.</li>
<li>violet: is the colour of warmth, beauty, and friendship.</li>
</ul>
<p>The ICA has been flying a flag with its official logo since April 2001, when its Board decided to replace the traditional rainbow flag. Its use by a number of non-cooperative groups led to confusion in several countries around the world.</p>

</blockquote>
<h3><span id="Andean_indigenism_(1920s)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Andean_indigenism_.281920s.29">Andean indigenism (1920s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Andean indigenism (1920s)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Banner_of_the_Qulla_Suyu.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="700" data-file-width="700" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Banner_of_the_Qulla_Suyu.svg/220px-Banner_of_the_Qulla_Suyu.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Banner_of_the_Qulla_Suyu.svg/330px-Banner_of_the_Qulla_Suyu.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Banner_of_the_Qulla_Suyu.svg/440px-Banner_of_the_Qulla_Suyu.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

Current flag of the city of <a href="/wiki/Cusco" title="Cusco">Cusco</a>, wrongly associated with the Inca Empire.</div>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Wiphala" title="Wiphala">Wiphala</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_of_Cuzco" title="Flag of Cuzco">Flag of Cuzco</a></div>
<p>A flag with a seven-striped rainbow design is used in <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> is anachronically associated with <a href="/wiki/Inca_Empire" title="Inca Empire">Tawantin Suyu</a>, or Inca territory. The <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Cusco" title="Flag of Cusco">Flag of Cusco</a> was introduced in 1978 and is still the official city emblem.</p>
<p>In reaction to claims as to the flag's "historicity" in the 16th-century <a href="/wiki/Inca_Empire" title="Inca Empire">Inca Empire</a>, the use of similar rainbow flags has been traced to the early decades of the 1920s. According to Peruvian historiography, the Inca Empire never had a flag. <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_Rostworowski" title="María Rostworowski">María Rostworowski</a>, a Peruvian historian known for her extensive and detailed publications about Peruvian Ancient Cultures and the Inca Empire, said about this: "I bet my life, the Inca never had that flag, it never existed, no chronicler mentioned it".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Also, according to the Peruvian newspaper <i>El Comercio</i>, the flag only dates to the first decades of the 20th century,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and even the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_the_Republic_of_Peru" title="Congress of the Republic of Peru">Congress of the Republic of Peru</a> has determined that flag is a fake by citing the conclusion of the National Academy of Peruvian History:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"The official use of the wrongly called 'Tawantinsuyu flag' is a mistake. In the pre-Hispanic Andean world the concept of flags did not exist, it did not belong to their historic context".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><br/>
<small><b>National Academy of Peruvian History</b></small><br/></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In Ecuador, a rainbow flag is used by the <i><a href="/wiki/Pachakutik_Plurinational_Unity_Movement_%E2%80%93_New_Country" title="Pachakutik Plurinational Unity Movement – New Country">Pachakutik</a></i> political party (1995), which is composed mostly of left-wing indigenous people.</p>
<h3><span id="Meher_Baba_(1924)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Meher_Baba_.281924.29">Meher Baba (1924)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Meher Baba (1924)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Meher_Baba%27s_flag.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="350" data-file-width="550" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Meher_Baba%27s_flag.gif/220px-Meher_Baba%27s_flag.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Meher_Baba%27s_flag.gif/330px-Meher_Baba%27s_flag.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Meher_Baba%27s_flag.gif/440px-Meher_Baba%27s_flag.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Meher_Baba%27s_flag.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Meher_Baba%27s_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Meher Baba's flag (page does not exist)">Meher Baba's flag</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Meher_Baba" title="Meher Baba">Meher Baba</a> designed a rainbow flag on April 23, 1924. It is flown each year near his <a href="/wiki/Samadhi_of_Meher_Baba" title="Samadhi of Meher Baba">samadhi</a> (tomb-shrine) in <a href="/wiki/Meherabad" title="Meherabad">Meherabad</a>, India during the week of <a href="/wiki/Amartithi" title="Amartithi">Amartithi</a> (the anniversary of his death on January 31, 1969). Baba explained the symbolism, saying, "The colors in the flag signify man's rise from the grossest of impressions of lust and anger – symbolized by red – to the culmination in the highest state of spirituality and oneness with God – symbolized by sky blue."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Peace_movement_(1961)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Peace_movement_.281961.29">Peace movement (1961)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Peace movement (1961)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PACE-flag.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="750" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/PACE-flag.svg/220px-PACE-flag.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/PACE-flag.svg/330px-PACE-flag.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/PACE-flag.svg/440px-PACE-flag.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PACE-flag.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Peace_flag" title="Peace flag">PACE flag</a> (Italian for '<a href="/wiki/Peace" title="Peace">peace</a>')</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Peace_flag" title="Peace flag">Peace flag</a></div>
<p>This rainbow flag in Italy was first used in a peace march in 1961, inspired by similar multi-coloured flags used in demonstrations against <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">nuclear weapons</a>. It became popular with the <i>Pace da tutti i balconi</i> ("peace from every balcony") campaign in 2002, started as a protest against the impending <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_in_Iraq" title="War in Iraq">war in Iraq</a>. The most common variety has seven colours, purple, blue, <a href="/wiki/Azure_(color)" title="Azure (color)">azure</a>, green, yellow, orange and red, and is emblazoned in bold with the Italian word <i>PACE</i>, meaning "peace".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Common variations include moving the purple stripe down below the azure one, and adding a white stripe on top (the original flag from the 60s had a white stripe on top). This flag has been adopted internationally as a symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Peace_movement" title="Peace movement">peace movement</a>.</p>
<h3><span id="Bene_Ohr_Jewish_movement,_U.S.A._(1961)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Bene_Ohr_Jewish_movement.2C_U.S.A._.281961.29">Bene Ohr Jewish movement, U.S.A. (1961)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bene Ohr Jewish movement, U.S.A. (1961)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1961, Rabbi <a href="/wiki/Zalman_Schachter-Shalomi" title="Zalman Schachter-Shalomi">Zalman Schachter-Shalomi</a> designed the rainbow tallit (prayer shawl) as a symbol of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kabalah" title="Kabalah">Kabalah</a> for the members of the Jewish <i>Bene Ohr</i> ("The Children of Light"). It is a vertically presented rainbow, with each colour separated by black stripes of varying thicknesses. The colors represent aspects of God; the black stripes and white spaces represent aspects of creation and protection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span id="LGBT_(Lesbian,_Gay,_Bisexual,_Transgender)_Pride_(1978)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="LGBT_.28Lesbian.2C_Gay.2C_Bisexual.2C_Transgender.29_Pride_.281978.29">LGBT (Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender) Pride (1978)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: LGBT (Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender) Pride (1978)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gay_flag.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="316" data-file-width="512" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Gay_flag.svg/220px-Gay_flag.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Gay_flag.svg/330px-Gay_flag.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Gay_flag.svg/440px-Gay_flag.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gay_flag.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Rainbow_flag_(LGBT_movement)" title="Rainbow flag (LGBT movement)">Rainbow flag (LGBT movement)</a>, the most widely recognized version</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_flag_(LGBT_movement)" title="Rainbow flag (LGBT movement)">Rainbow flag (LGBT movement)</a></div>
<p>The rainbow flag was popularized as a symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Gay" title="Gay">gay</a> community by <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Baker_(artist)" title="Gilbert Baker (artist)">Gilbert Baker</a> in 1978. The different colors symbolize diversity in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gay_community" title="Gay community">gay community</a>, and the flag is used predominantly at <a href="/wiki/Gay_pride" title="Gay pride">gay pride</a> events and in <a href="/wiki/Gay_village" title="Gay village">gay villages</a> worldwide in various forms including banners, clothing and jewelry. Since the 1990s, its symbolism has been transferred to represent the extended "<a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a>" (<a href="/wiki/Lesbian" title="Lesbian">lesbian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gay" title="Gay">gay</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bisexual" title="Bisexual">bisexual</a> and <a href="/wiki/Transgender" title="Transgender">transgender</a>) community . For the 25th Anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Stonewall_riots" title="Stonewall riots">Stonewall riots</a>, held in 1994 in New York city, a mile-long rainbow flag was created and post-parade cut up in sections that have since been used around the world.</p>
<p>The flag was originally created with eight colors, but <a href="/wiki/Pink" title="Pink">pink</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turquoise_(color)" title="Turquoise (color)">turquoise</a> were removed for production purposes, and since 1979 it has consisted of six colored stripes. It is most commonly flown with the red stripe on top, as the colors appear in a natural rainbow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Aside from the obvious symbolism of a mixed <a href="/wiki/Sexuality_and_gender_identity-based_cultures" title="Sexuality and gender identity-based cultures">LGBT community</a>, the colors were determined to symbolize: life (red), healing (orange), sunlight (yellow), nature (green), harmony/peace (blue), and spirit (purple/violet).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The removed colors stood for sexuality (pink) and art/magic (turquoise).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the late 1980s and early 1990s, a black stripe was sometimes used to represent the AIDS victims.</p>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_of_Jewish_Autonomous_Oblast" title="Flag of Jewish Autonomous Oblast">Flag of Jewish Autonomous Oblast</a></div>
<p>Another variation of rainbow flag is used by <a href="/wiki/Jewish_Autonomous_Oblast" title="Jewish Autonomous Oblast">Jewish Autonomous Oblast</a>, situated in the <a href="/wiki/Far_Eastern_Federal_District" title="Far Eastern Federal District">Far Eastern Federal District</a> of Russia, by the Chinese border. Proportions 2:3. Adopted first of October 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Jewish_Autonomous_Oblast" title="Jewish Autonomous Oblast">Jewish Autonomous Oblast</a> has a flag with a seven-colour rainbow. The number of colours is meant to symbolize the seven-branched Jewish <a href="/wiki/Menorah_(Temple)" title="Menorah (Temple)">Menorah</a>.</p>
