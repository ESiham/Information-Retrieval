<p><b>Rainbow nation</b> is a term coined by <a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">Archbishop</a> <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Tutu" title="Desmond Tutu">Desmond Tutu</a> to describe <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Post-apartheid_South_Africa" title="Post-apartheid South Africa">post-apartheid South Africa</a>, after <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>'s first fully <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic</a> election in 1994.</p>
<p>The phrase was elaborated upon by President <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Mandela" title="Nelson Mandela">Nelson Mandela</a> in his first month of office, when he proclaimed: "Each of us is as intimately attached to the soil of this beautiful country as are the famous <a href="/wiki/Jacaranda" title="Jacaranda">jacaranda</a> trees of <a href="/wiki/Pretoria" title="Pretoria">Pretoria</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Mimosa" title="Mimosa">mimosa</a> trees of the <a href="/wiki/Bushveld" title="Bushveld">bushveld</a> – a <a href="/wiki/Rainbow" title="Rainbow">rainbow</a> nation at peace with itself and the world.."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbolic_identity">Symbolic identity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_nation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Symbolic identity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:South_Africa_-_population_migrations.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="440" data-file-width="460" height="210" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/South_Africa_-_population_migrations.svg/220px-South_Africa_-_population_migrations.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/South_Africa_-_population_migrations.svg/330px-South_Africa_-_population_migrations.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/South_Africa_-_population_migrations.svg/440px-South_Africa_-_population_migrations.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:South_Africa_-_population_migrations.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The many migrations that formed the modern rainbow nation</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The term was intended to encapsulate the unity of multi-culturalism and the coming-together of people of many different nations, in a country once identified with the strict division of white and black.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a series of televised appearances, Tutu spoke of the "Rainbow People of God". As a cleric, this <a href="/wiki/Metaphor" title="Metaphor">metaphor</a> drew upon the <a href="/wiki/Old_Testament" title="Old Testament">Old Testament</a> story of Noah's Flood, and its ensuing rainbow of peace. Within South African indigenous cultures, the rainbow is associated with hope and a bright future (as in <a href="/wiki/Xhosa_people" title="Xhosa people">Xhosa</a> culture).</p>
<p>The secondary metaphor the rainbow allows is more political. Unlike the primary metaphor, the room for different cultural interpretations of the colour spectrum is slight. Whether the rainbow has <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Newton" title="Isaac Newton">Newton</a>'s seven colours, or five of the <a href="/wiki/Nguni_people" title="Nguni people">Nguni</a> (i.e., Xhosa and <a href="/wiki/Zulu_people" title="Zulu people">Zulu</a>) cosmology, the colours are not taken literally to represent particular cultural groups.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rainbow_influence">Rainbow influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_nation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rainbow influence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:142px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_South_Africa.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="900" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Flag_of_South_Africa.svg/140px-Flag_of_South_Africa.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Flag_of_South_Africa.svg/210px-Flag_of_South_Africa.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Flag_of_South_Africa.svg/280px-Flag_of_South_Africa.svg.png 2x" width="140"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_South_Africa.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Flag_of_South_Africa" title="Flag of South Africa">South African national flag</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>Rainbow nation</i>, as a spoken metaphor for South African unity is uniquely (although not deliberately) represented by the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_South_Africa" title="Flag of South Africa">South African flag</a>, which sports 6 different colours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rainbowism">Rainbowism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_nation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rainbowism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>South African political commentators have been known to speculate on "rainbowism", whereby true domestic issues such as the legacy of <a href="/wiki/Racism" title="Racism">racism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crime_in_South_Africa" title="Crime in South Africa">crime</a>, and the like are glossed over and "sugar-coated" by the cover of rainbow peace. South African politician, academic, and noted poet <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Cronin" title="Jeremy Cronin">Jeremy Cronin</a> cautions:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;"><a href="/wiki/Identity_formation" title="Identity formation">Identity formation</a> as well as the myth of the "rainbow nation" and its performative intention have served to discursively create a national identity that has been top-down in its constitution and implementation. As a result, true reconciliation has been foregone in place of a simplified and somewhat candy-coated myth of peace that has served to reconcile those on the inside whilst pitting them against those on the outside.
<p>Allowing ourselves to sink into a smug rainbowism will prove to be a terrible betrayal of the possibilities for real transformation, real reconciliation, and real national unity that are still at play in our contemporary South African reality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
