<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Temperate rainforest</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Acer_macrophyllum_in_Hoh_Valley2b.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2900" data-file-width="2130" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Acer_macrophyllum_in_Hoh_Valley2b.jpg/220px-Acer_macrophyllum_in_Hoh_Valley2b.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Acer_macrophyllum_in_Hoh_Valley2b.jpg/330px-Acer_macrophyllum_in_Hoh_Valley2b.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Acer_macrophyllum_in_Hoh_Valley2b.jpg/440px-Acer_macrophyllum_in_Hoh_Valley2b.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<a href="/wiki/Maple" title="Maple">Maples</a> covered with <a href="/wiki/Epiphyte" title="Epiphyte">epiphytic</a> moss in the <a href="/wiki/Hoh_Rainforest" title="Hoh Rainforest">Hoh Rainforest</a></div>

</div>
<p><b>Temperate rainforests</b> are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conifer" title="Conifer">coniferous</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadleaf" title="Broadleaf">broadleaf</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forests" title="Forests">forests</a> that occur in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate_zone" title="Temperate zone">temperate zone</a> and receive heavy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainfall" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a>.</p>
<p>Temperate rain forests occur in regions around the world: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_temperate_rain_forests" title="Pacific temperate rain forests">Pacific temperate rain forests</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valdivian_temperate_rain_forests" title="Valdivian temperate rain forests">Valdivian temperate rain forests</a> of southwestern <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, the rain forests of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northwest_Europe" title="Northwest Europe">northwest Europe</a> (small pockets in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, and a somewhat larger area in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>), southern <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and the eastern <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>-<a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> to northern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. The moist conditions of temperate rain forests generally support an understory of <a href="/wiki/Moss" title="Moss">mosses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fern" title="Fern">ferns</a> and some shrubs. Temperate rain forests can be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate_coniferous_forests" title="Temperate coniferous forests">temperate coniferous forests</a> or <a href="/wiki/Temperate_broadleaf_and_mixed_forest" title="Temperate broadleaf and mixed forest">temperate broadleaf and mixed forests</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Termas_geometricas.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1717" data-file-width="3044" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Termas_geometricas.jpg/300px-Termas_geometricas.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Termas_geometricas.jpg/450px-Termas_geometricas.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Termas_geometricas.jpg/600px-Termas_geometricas.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Termas_geometricas.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Humid temperate rain forest in Termas Geométricas near <a href="/wiki/Co%C3%B1aripe" title="Coñaripe">Coñaripe</a>, Chile</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>For temperate rain forests of North America, Alaback's definition<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alaback_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alaback-1">[1]</a></sup> is widely recognized:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Definition_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Definition-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd>Annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> over 140 cm (55 in)</dd>
<dd>Mean annual temperature is between 4 and 12 °C (39 and 54 °F).</dd>
</dl>
<p>However, required annual precipitation depends on factors such as distribution of rainfall over the year, temperatures over the year and fog presence, and definitions in other countries differ considerably. For example, Australian definitions are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological" title="Ecological">ecological</a>-structural rather than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Climatic" title="Climatic">climatic</a>:</p>
<dl>
<dd>Closed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canopy_(forest)" title="Canopy (forest)">canopy</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">trees</a> excludes at least 70% of the sky.</dd>
<dd>Forest is composed mainly of tree <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> which do not require fire for regeneration, but with <a href="/wiki/Seedling" title="Seedling">seedlings</a> able to regenerate under shade and in natural openings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Floyd_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Floyd-3">[3]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p>The latter would, for example, exclude a part of the temperate rain forests of western North America, as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coast_Douglas-fir" title="Coast Douglas-fir">Coast Douglas-fir</a>, one of its dominant tree species, requires stand-destroying disturbance to initiate a new cohort of seedlings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pseudotsuga_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pseudotsuga-4">[4]</a></sup> The North American definition would in turn exclude a part of temperate rain forests in other countries.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_distribution">Global distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Global distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:452px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Temperate_rainforest_map.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="414" data-file-width="940" height="198" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Temperate_rainforest_map.svg/450px-Temperate_rainforest_map.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Temperate_rainforest_map.svg/675px-Temperate_rainforest_map.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Temperate_rainforest_map.svg/900px-Temperate_rainforest_map.svg.png 2x" width="450"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Temperate_rainforest_map.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A map showing the areas of temperate rain forest</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Temperate forests cover a large part of the Earth, but temperate rainforests only occur in a few regions around the world. Most of these occur in oceanic moist climates: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_temperate_rain_forest_(WWF_ecoregion)" title="Pacific temperate rain forest (WWF ecoregion)">Pacific temperate rain forests</a> in Western <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> (Southeastern <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> to Central <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valdivian_temperate_rainforests" title="Valdivian temperate rainforests">Valdivian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magellanic_subpolar_forests" title="Magellanic subpolar forests">Magellanic</a> temperate rainforests of southwestern <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> (Southern <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> and adjacent <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>), pockets of rain forest in <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_Europe" title="Northwestern Europe">Northwestern Europe</a> (southern <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> to northern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iberia" title="Iberia">Iberia</a>), temperate rainforests of southeastern <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> temperate rainforests (<a href="/wiki/South_Island" title="South Island">South Island</a>'s west coast).</p>
<p>Others occur in subtropical moist climates; <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knysna-Amatole_coastal_forests" title="Knysna-Amatole coastal forests">Knysna-Amatole coastal forests</a>, the Colchan rainforests of the eastern <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> region (<a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>), the Caspian temperate rainforests of <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, the mountain temperate rainforests along eastern <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>'s Pacific coast, the eastern coast of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Peninsula" title="Korean Peninsula">Korean Peninsula</a> along the length of the <a href="/wiki/Baekdudaegan" title="Baekdudaegan">Baekdu Mountain Range</a> and in the area surrounding <a href="/wiki/Jirisan" title="Jirisan">Mt. Jiri</a> and across the peninsula's southern coastline, southwest <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>'s Taiheiyo forests, coastal <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> and New Zealand's <a href="/wiki/North_Island" title="North Island">North Island</a>.</p>
<p>Additionally pockets of temperate rainforest occur in dreary climates that are not categorized by just annual precipitation but also number of cloudy days as well as number of days of measurable precipitation in the form of rain or snow. In Western North America outside the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Mountains" title="Columbia Mountains">Columbia Mountains</a> of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> (BC), northern <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a> and northwestern <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>, have more of a continental climate and have pockets of temperate coniferous rainforest.</p>
<p>In Eastern North America, there are scattered pockets of temperate rainforest also exist along the <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_Plateau" title="Allegheny Plateau">Allegheny Plateau</a> and adjacent parts of the <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Mountains" title="Appalachian Mountains">Appalachian Mountains</a> from <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>. These areas include sections of West Virginia, Western Pennsylvania, as well as Western <a href="/wiki/Upstate_New_York" title="Upstate New York">Upstate New York</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Adirondack_Mountains" title="Adirondack Mountains">Adirondack Mountains</a>. A good example of these forests are found in <a href="/wiki/Zoar_Valley" title="Zoar Valley">Zoar Valley</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_New_York" title="Western New York">Western New York</a> (nearest major city is <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>), <a href="/wiki/Cook_Forest_State_Park" title="Cook Forest State Park">Cook Forest State Park</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_National_Forest" title="Allegheny National Forest">Allegheny National Forest</a> (nearest major city is <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Cathedral_State_Park" title="Cathedral State Park">Cathedral State Park</a> in West Virginia.</p>
<p>In Eastern Asia, there are scattered pockets of temperate rainforest in what is known as the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Far_East" title="Russian Far East">Russian Far East</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ussuri" title="Ussuri">Ussuri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Outer_Manchuria" title="Outer Manchuria">Outer Manchuria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sakhalin" title="Sakhalin">Sakhalin</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> where the climate is also continental in nature, but get enough precipitation and cloud cover to harbor significant pockets of temperate rainforest. Like Eastern North America, much precipitation is in the form of rain and snow. The mountainous coniferous forests of the <a href="/wiki/Changbai_Mountains" title="Changbai Mountains">Changbai Mountains</a> bordering <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> are also a good example, containing some of the richest high-elevation coniferous evergreen forests in <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Temperate_rain_forest_regions_by_continent">Temperate rain forest regions by continent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Temperate rain forest regions by continent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pacific_temperate_rain_forests">Pacific temperate rain forests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pacific temperate rain forests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>A portion of the temperate rain forest region of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, the largest area of temperate zone rain forests on the planet, is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_temperate_rain_forests_ecoregion" title="Pacific temperate rain forests ecoregion">Pacific temperate rain forests ecoregion</a> which occur on west-facing <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_Ranges" title="Pacific Coast Ranges">coastal mountains</a> along the Pacific coast of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Kodiak_Island" title="Kodiak Island">Kodiak Island</a> in <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> to northern <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, and are part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nearctic" title="Nearctic">Nearctic</a> ecozone. In the different system established by the <a href="/wiki/Commission_for_Environmental_Cooperation" title="Commission for Environmental Cooperation">Commission for Environmental Cooperation</a>, this same general region is classed as the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Maritime_Ecozone_(CEC)" title="Pacific Maritime Ecozone (CEC)">Pacific Maritime Ecozone</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environment_Canada" title="Environment Canada">Environment Canada</a> and as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_ecoregions_in_the_United_States_(EPA)#Marine_West_Coast_Forest" title="List of ecoregions in the United States (EPA)">Marine West Coast Forest</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_ecoregions_in_the_United_States_(EPA)#Marine_West_Coast_Forest" title="List of ecoregions in the United States (EPA)">Northwestern Forested Mountains</a> Level II ecoregions by the United States <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a>. In terms of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Floristic_province" title="Floristic province">floristic province</a> system used by <a href="/wiki/Botany" title="Botany">botany</a>, the bulk of the region is the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_Floristic_Region" title="Rocky Mountain Floristic Region">Rocky Mountain Floristic Region</a> but a small southern portion is part of the <a href="/wiki/California_Floristic_Province" title="California Floristic Province">California Floristic Province</a>.</p>

<p>Sub-ecoregions of the Pacific temperate rain forest ecoregion as defined by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wildlife_Fund" title="World Wildlife Fund">WWF</a> include the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Pacific_coastal_forests_(WWF_ecoregion)" title="Northern Pacific coastal forests (WWF ecoregion)">Northern Pacific coastal forests</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Charlotte_Islands" title="Queen Charlotte Islands">Queen Charlotte Islands ecoregion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Island" title="Vancouver Island">Vancouver Island ecoregion</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Columbia_mainland_coastal_forests_(WWF_ecoregion)" title="British Columbia mainland coastal forests (WWF ecoregion)">British Columbia mainland coastal forests</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Pacific_coastal_forests_(WWF_ecoregion)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Central Pacific coastal forests (WWF ecoregion) (page does not exist)">Central Pacific coastal forests</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_and_Southern_Cascades_forests_(WWF_ecoregion)" title="Central and Southern Cascades forests (WWF ecoregion)">Southern Cascades forests ecoregion</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Klamath-Siskiyou_forests_(WWF_ecoregion)" title="Klamath-Siskiyou forests (WWF ecoregion)">Klamath-Siskiyou coastal forests</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Northern_California_coastal_forests_(WWF_ecoregion)" title="Northern California coastal forests (WWF ecoregion)">Northern California coastal forests</a> ecoregions. They vary in their species composition, but are all predominantly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coniferous" title="Coniferous">coniferous</a>, sometimes with an <a href="/wiki/Understory" title="Understory">understory</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadleaved" title="Broadleaved">broadleaved</a> <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">trees</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shrub" title="Shrub">shrubs</a>. Most of the precipitation occurs in winter but summer <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fogs</a> moisture is extracted by the trees and produces a <a href="/wiki/Fog_drip" title="Fog drip">fog drip</a> keeping the forest moist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Franklin_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Franklin-5">[5]</a></sup> The Northern California coastal forests are home to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coast_Redwood" title="Coast Redwood">Coast Redwood</a> (<i>Sequoia sempervirens</i>), the world's tallest tree. In the other ecoregions, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coast_Douglas-fir" title="Coast Douglas-fir">Coast Douglas-fir</a> (<i>Pseudotsuga menziesii</i> var. <i>menziesii</i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sitka_Spruce" title="Sitka Spruce">Sitka Spruce</a> (<i>Picea sitchensis</i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Hemlock" title="Western Hemlock">Western Hemlock</a> (<i>Tsuga heterophylla</i>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_redcedar" title="Western redcedar">Western redcedar</a> (<i>Thuja plicata</i>) are the most important tree species. A common feature of Pacific temperate rain forests of North America is the <a href="/wiki/Nurse_log" title="Nurse log">Nurse log</a>, a fallen tree which as it decays, provides ecological facilitation to seedlings. Trees such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coast_Douglas-fir" title="Coast Douglas-fir">Coast Douglas-fir</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Hemlock" title="Western Hemlock">Western Hemlock</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Red_Cedar" title="Western Red Cedar">Western Red Cedar</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Yew" title="Pacific Yew">Pacific Yew</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vine_Maple" title="Vine Maple">Vine Maple</a> are more closely related to coniferous and deciduous trees in the temperate forests of <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a>.</p>

<p>Some of the largest expanses of old growth are found in <a href="/wiki/Olympic_National_Park" title="Olympic National Park">Olympic National Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mount_Rainier_National_Park" title="Mount Rainier National Park">Mount Rainier National Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tongass_National_Forest" title="Tongass National Forest">Tongass National Forest</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mount_St._Helens_National_Monument" title="Mount St. Helens National Monument">Mount St. Helens National Monument</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Redwood_National_Park" title="Redwood National Park">Redwood National Park</a>, and throughout <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> (including British Columbia's Coastal Mountain Ranges), with the coastal <a href="/wiki/Great_Bear_Rainforest" title="Great Bear Rainforest">Great Bear Rainforest</a> containing the largest expanses of old growth temperate rainforest found in the world.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cariboo_Mountains" title="Cariboo Mountains">Cariboo Mountains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_Trench" title="Rocky Mountain Trench">Rocky Mountain Trench</a> (east of <a href="/wiki/Prince_George,_British_Columbia" title="Prince George, British Columbia">Prince George</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Mountains" title="Columbia Mountains">Columbia Mountains</a> of Southeastern British Columbia (west of the Canadian <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a> that extend into parts of Idaho and Northwestern Montana in the USA), which include the <a href="/wiki/Selkirk_Mountains" title="Selkirk Mountains">Selkirk Mountains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monashee_Mountains" title="Monashee Mountains">Monashee Mountains</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Purcell_Mountains" title="Purcell Mountains">Purcell Mountains</a>, have the largest stretch of interior temperate coniferous rain forests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> These <a href="/wiki/Inland_rainforest" title="Inland rainforest">inland rainforests</a> have more continental climate with a large proportion of the precipitation falling as snow. Being closer to the Rocky Mountains, there is more of a diverse mammalian fauna. Some of the best interior rain forests are found in <a href="/wiki/Mount_Revelstoke_National_Park" title="Mount Revelstoke National Park">Mount Revelstoke National Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glacier_National_Park_(Canada)" title="Glacier National Park (Canada)">Glacier National Park (Canada)</a> in the Columbia Mountains.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Appalachian_temperate_rain_forests">Appalachian temperate rain forests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Appalachian temperate rain forests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_temperate_rainforest" title="Appalachian temperate rainforest">Appalachian temperate rainforest</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Temperate rain forests are located in the southern <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Mountains" title="Appalachian Mountains">Appalachian Mountains</a> where <a href="/wiki/Orographic_lift" title="Orographic lift">orographic precipitation</a> causes weather systems coming from the west and from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> to drop more precipitation than in surrounding areas. The largest of these forest blocks are located in western <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> northern <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and far eastern <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> largely in the <a href="/wiki/Pisgah_National_Forest" title="Pisgah National Forest">Pisgah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nantahala_National_Forest" title="Nantahala National Forest">Nantahala</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chattahoochee_National_Forest" title="Chattahoochee National Forest">Chattahoochee</a> National Forests and nearby <a href="/wiki/Gorges_State_Park" title="Gorges State Park">Gorges State Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In addition, small areas in the highest elevations of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Smoky_Mountains" title="Great Smoky Mountains">Great Smoky Mountains</a> also receive substantial rainfall, with <a href="/wiki/Clingmans_Dome" title="Clingmans Dome">Clingmans Dome</a>, for example, collecting about 2,000 mm of precipitation per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Red spruce and Fraser fir are dominant canopy trees in high mountain areas. In higher elevation (over 1,980 metres (6,500 ft)), Fraser fir is dominant, in middle elevation (1,675 to 1,890 metres (5,495 to 6,201 ft)) red spruce and Fraser fir grow together, and in lower elevation (1,370 to 1,650 metres (4,490 to 5,410 ft)) red spruce is dominant. Yellow birch, mountain ash, and mountain maple grow in the understory.Younger spruce and fir and shrubs like raspberry, blackberry, hobblebush, southern mountain cranberries, red elderberry, minniebush, southern bush honeysuckle are understory vegetation. Below the spruce-fir forest, at around 1,200 metres (3,900 ft), are forests of American beech, yellow birch, maple birch, and oak.[9] Skunk cabbage and ground juniper are northern species that were pushed into the areas from the north.</p>
<p>The mild and wet environment supports the high diversity of fungi. Over 2,000 species live in this area and scientists estimate many unidentified fungi may be there.[10]</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_America">South America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: South America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Valdivian_and_Magellanic_temperate_rainforests">Valdivian and Magellanic temperate rainforests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Valdivian and Magellanic temperate rainforests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>The temperate rain forests of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> are located on the Pacific coast of southern <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, on the west-facing slopes of the southern Chilean coast range, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andes_Mountains" title="Andes Mountains">Andes Mountains</a> in both Chile and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> down to the southern tip of South America, and are part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neotropic" title="Neotropic">Neotropic</a> ecozone. Temperate rain forests occur in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valdivian_temperate_rain_forests" title="Valdivian temperate rain forests">Valdivian temperate rain forests</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magellanic_subpolar_forests" title="Magellanic subpolar forests">Magellanic subpolar forests</a> ecoregions. The Valdivian rainforests are home to a variety of broadleaf evergreen trees, like <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aextoxicon_punctatum" title="Aextoxicon punctatum">Aextoxicon punctatum</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Eucryphia_cordifolia" title="Eucryphia cordifolia">Eucryphia cordifolia</a></i>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_beech" title="Southern beech">southern beech</a> (<i>Nothofagus</i>), but include many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conifers" title="Conifers">conifers</a> as well, notably <a href="/wiki/Fitzroya" title="Fitzroya">Alerce</a> (<i>Fitzroya cupressoides</i>), one of the largest tree species of the world.</p>
<p>The Valdivian and Magellanic temperate rainforests are the only temperate rain forests in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. Together they are the second largest in the world, after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_temperate_rain_forests" title="Pacific temperate rain forests">Pacific temperate rain forests</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. The Valdivian forests are a refuge for the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_flora" title="Antarctic flora">Antarctic flora</a>, and share many plant <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">families</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genera" title="Genera">genera</a> with the temperate rainforests of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. Fully half the species of woody plants are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endemic_(ecology)" title="Endemic (ecology)">endemic</a> to this ecoregion.</p>
<p>In the Valdivian region the Andean Cordillera intercepts moist westerly winds along the Pacific coast during winter and summer months; these winds cool as they ascend the mountains, creating heavy rainfall on the mountains' west-facing slopes. The northward-flowing oceanic Humboldt Current creates humid and foggy conditions near the coast. The tree line is at about 2,400 m in the northern part of the ecoregion (35°S), and descends to 1,000 m in the south of the <a href="/wiki/Los_R%C3%ADos_Region" title="Los Ríos Region">Valdivian region</a>. In the summer the temperature can climb to 16.5 °C (61.7 °F), while during winter the temperature can drop below 7 °C (45 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="Knysna-Amatole_coastal_rain_forests_(South_Africa)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Knysna-Amatole_coastal_rain_forests_.28South_Africa.29">Knysna-Amatole coastal rain forests (South Africa)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Knysna-Amatole coastal rain forests (South Africa)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>The temperate rainforests of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> are part of the <a href="/wiki/Knysna-Amatole_montane_forests" title="Knysna-Amatole montane forests">Knysna-Amatole forests</a> that are located along South Africa's <a href="/wiki/Garden_Route" title="Garden Route">Garden Route</a> between Cape Town and Durban on the south-facing slopes of South Africa's Drakensberg Mountains facing the Indian Ocean. There are several coniferous <a href="/wiki/Podocarpaceae" title="Podocarpaceae">podocarps</a> that grow here. This forest receives a lot of moisture as fog from the Indian Ocean, and resembles not only other temperate rain forests worldwide, but also the montane evergreen <a href="/wiki/Afromontane" title="Afromontane">Afromontane</a> forests that occur at higher elevations in southern and eastern Africa. A fine example of this forest is in South Africa's <a href="/wiki/Tsitsikamma_National_Park" title="Tsitsikamma National Park">Tsitsikamma National Park</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Temperate rainforest occurs in fragments across the north and west of Europe in countries such as southern <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_coastal_conifer_forests" title="Scandinavian coastal conifer forests">Scandinavian coastal conifer forests</a>) and northern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Other temperate rainforest regions include areas of south eastern Europe such as mountains on the east coast of the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a>, surrounding North Western <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> along with the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>.</p>
<h4><span id="Atlantic_Oakwood_forest_(United_Kingdom_and_Ireland)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantic_Oakwood_forest_.28United_Kingdom_and_Ireland.29">Atlantic Oakwood forest (United Kingdom and Ireland)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Atlantic Oakwood forest (United Kingdom and Ireland)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The woodlands are variously referred to in Britain as Upland Oakwoods, Atlantic Oakwoods, Western Oakwoods or Temperate Rainforest, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caledonian_forest" title="Caledonian forest">Caledonian forest</a>, and colloquially as 'Celtic Rainforests'. They are also listed in the <a href="/wiki/British_National_Vegetation_Classification" title="British National Vegetation Classification">British National Vegetation Classification</a> as <a href="/wiki/British_NVC_community_W11" title="British NVC community W11">British NVC community W11</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=British_NVC_community_W17&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="British NVC community W17 (page does not exist)">British NVC community W17</a> depending on the ground flora. The majority of surviving fragments of Atlantic Oakwoods in Britain occur on steep-sided slopes above rivers and lakes which have avoided clearance and intensive grazing pressure. There are notable examples on the islands and shores of Loch Maree, Loch Sunart, Loch Lomond and one of the best preserved sites on the remote Taynish Peninsula in Argyll. There are also small areas on steep-sided riverine gorges in Snowdonia and Mid Wales. In England, they occur in the Lake District (Borrowdale Woods) and steep-sided riverine and estuarine valleys in Devon and Cornwall including the <a href="/wiki/Fowey" title="Fowey">Fowey</a> valley in Cornwall and the valley of the <a href="/wiki/River_Dart" title="River Dart">river Dart</a> which flows off Dartmoor and has rainfall in excess of 2 metres per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UK_Government_Met_Office_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-UK_Government_Met_Office-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span id="Colchian_(Colchis)_rain_forests_(Bulgaria,_Turkey_and_Georgia)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Colchian_.28Colchis.29_rain_forests_.28Bulgaria.2C_Turkey_and_Georgia.29">Colchian (Colchis) rain forests (Bulgaria, Turkey and Georgia)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Colchian (Colchis) rain forests (Bulgaria, Turkey and Georgia)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Colchian rainforests are found around both the southeast and west corners of the Black Sea starting in <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> all the way to <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> and are part of the <a href="/wiki/Euxine-Colchic_deciduous_forests" title="Euxine-Colchic deciduous forests">Euxine-Colchic deciduous forests</a> ecoregion, together with the drier Euxine forests further west. The Colchian rain forests are mixed, with deciduous <a href="/wiki/Alnus_glutinosa" title="Alnus glutinosa">black alder</a> <i>(Alnus glutinosa)</i>, hornbeam (<i><a href="/wiki/Carpinus_betulus" title="Carpinus betulus">Carpinus betulus</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Carpinus_orientalis" title="Carpinus orientalis">C. orientalis</a></i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oriental_beech" title="Oriental beech">Oriental beech</a> (<i>Fagus orientalis</i>), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sweet_chestnut" title="Sweet chestnut">sweet chestnut</a> <i>(Castanea sativa)</i> together with evergreen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nordmann_fir" title="Nordmann fir">Nordmann fir</a> (<i>Abies nordmanniana</i>, the tallest tree in Europe at 78 m), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caucasian_spruce" title="Caucasian spruce">Caucasian spruce</a> (<i>Picea orientalis</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Scots_pine" title="Scots pine">Scots pine</a> (<i>Pinus sylvestris</i>). The refugium is the largest throughout the Western Asian / near Eastern region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tgm_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-tgm-16">[16]</a></sup> The area has multiple representatives of <a href="/wiki/Disjunct_distribution" title="Disjunct distribution">disjunct</a> <a href="/wiki/Relict" title="Relict">relict</a> groups of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plants" title="Plants">plants</a> with the closest relatives in Eastern Asia, southern Europe, and even North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milne_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Milne-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Denk_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Denk-19">[19]</a></sup> Over 70 species of forest snails of the region are endemic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pokryszko_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pokryszko-20">[20]</a></sup> Some relict species of vertebrates are <a href="/wiki/Caucasian_parsley_frog" title="Caucasian parsley frog">Caucasian parsley frog</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caucasian_salamander" title="Caucasian salamander">Caucasian salamander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert%27s_snow_vole" title="Robert's snow vole">Robert's snow vole</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caucasian_grouse" title="Caucasian grouse">Caucasian grouse</a>; they are almost entirely endemic groups of animals such as lizards of genus <i><a href="/wiki/Darevskia" title="Darevskia">Darevskia</a></i>. In general, species composition of this refugium is quite distinct and differs from that of the other Western Eurasian refugia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tgm_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-tgm-16">[16]</a></sup> Genetic data suggest that the Colchis temperate rainforest, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ice_Age" title="Ice Age">Ice Age</a>, was fragmented into smaller parts; in particular, evolutionary lineages of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caucasian_Salamander" title="Caucasian Salamander">Caucasian Salamander</a> from the central and south-western Colchis remained isolated from one another during the entire Ice Age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tarkhnishvili_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tarkhnishvili-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span id="Fragas_do_Eume_(Spain)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Fragas_do_Eume_.28Spain.29">Fragas do Eume (Spain)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Fragas do Eume (Spain)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Fragas_do_Eume" title="Fragas do Eume">Fragas do Eume</a> is a natural park situated in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galicia,_Spain" title="Galicia, Spain">Galicia</a>, north-western <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. <i>Fraga</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">Galician</a> word for "natural woodland", (old-growth forest) and the park is an example of a temperate rainforest in which oak (<a href="/wiki/Quercus_robur" title="Quercus robur">Quercus robur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quercus_pyrenaica" title="Quercus pyrenaica">Quercus pyrenaica</a>) is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Climax_vegetation" title="Climax vegetation">climax vegetation</a>. The protected area extends along the valley of the river Eume within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferrolterra" title="Ferrolterra">Ferrolterra</a> municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Pontedeume" title="Pontedeume">Pontedeume</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cabanas,_Galicia" title="Cabanas, Galicia">Cabanas</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Capela,_Galicia" title="A Capela, Galicia">A Capela</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monfero" title="Monfero">Monfero</a> and <a href="/wiki/As_Pontes_de_Garc%C3%ADa_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="As Pontes de García Rodríguez">As Pontes de García Rodríguez</a>. Some 500 people reside within the park. The monastery of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_John_of_Caaveiro" title="Saint John of Caaveiro">Saint John of Caaveiro</a> also lies within the park. The area was declared a <a href="/wiki/Natural_park_(Spain)" title="Natural park (Spain)">natural park</a> (a level of protection lower than <a href="/wiki/National_park" title="National park">national park</a>) in 1997. It is one of six natural parks in Galicia. The European Union has recognised the park as a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Community_Importance" title="Site of Community Importance">Site of Community Importance</a>. There are a number of species of ferns. Invertebrate species include the <a href="/wiki/Kerry_slug" title="Kerry slug">Kerry slug</a> and it is an important site for amphibians.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="Caspian_Hyrcanian_forest_(Iran_and_Azerbaijan)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Caspian_Hyrcanian_forest_.28Iran_and_Azerbaijan.29">Caspian Hyrcanian forest (Iran and Azerbaijan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Caspian Hyrcanian forest (Iran and Azerbaijan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Hyrcanian_mixed_forests" title="Caspian Hyrcanian mixed forests">Caspian Hyrcanian mixed forests</a> ecoregion in northern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> contains a jungle in the form of a rain forest which stretches from the east in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khorasan_province" title="Khorasan province">Khorasan province</a> to the west in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ardebil" title="Ardebil">Ardebil</a> province, covering the other provinces of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gilan" title="Gilan">Gilan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mazandaran" title="Mazandaran">Mazandaran</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Golestan_Province" title="Golestan Province">Golestan</a>. The Elburz or <a href="/wiki/Alborz" title="Alborz">Alborz</a> mountain range is the highest mountain range in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> which captures the moisture of the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> to its north and forms <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a> and temperate rain forests in the northern part of <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. The Iranians call this forest and region <i>Shomal</i> which means <i>north</i> in <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>.</p>
<p>In southeast <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, this ecoregion includes the <a href="/wiki/Lankaran_Lowland" title="Lankaran Lowland">Lankaran Lowland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Talysh_Mountains" title="Talysh Mountains">Talysh Mountains</a>, the latter being evenly divided with Iran to the south. They are <a href="/wiki/Deciduous" title="Deciduous">deciduous</a> forests containing tree species such as black alder (<i><a href="/wiki/Alnus_glutinosa" title="Alnus glutinosa">Alnus glutinosa</a></i> subsp. <i>barbata</i>), hornbeam (<i><a href="/wiki/Carpinus_betulus" title="Carpinus betulus">Carpinus betulus</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Carpinus_orientalis" title="Carpinus orientalis">C. orientalis</a></i>), Caucasian wingnut (<i><a href="/wiki/Pterocarya_fraxinifolia" title="Pterocarya fraxinifolia">Pterocarya fraxinifolia</a></i>), chestnut-leaved oak (<i><a href="/wiki/Quercus_castaneifolia" title="Quercus castaneifolia">Quercus castaneifolia</a></i>), Caucasian oak (<i><a href="/wiki/Quercus_macranthera" title="Quercus macranthera">Quercus macranthera</a></i>), oriental beech (<i><a href="/wiki/Fagus_orientalis" title="Fagus orientalis">Fagus orientalis</a></i>), Persian ironwood (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parrotia_persica" title="Parrotia persica">Parrotia persica</a></i>) and Persian silk tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Albizia_julibrissin" title="Albizia julibrissin">Albizia julibrissin</a></i>).</p>
<p>The existing protected areas in Azerbaijan include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gizil-Agach_State_Reserve" title="Gizil-Agach State Reserve">Gizil-Agach State Reserve</a> – 88.4 square kilometres (34.1 sq mi)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hirkan_National_Park" title="Hirkan National Park">Hirkan National Park</a> – 427.97 square kilometres (165.24 sq mi)</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zuvand_National_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zuvand National Park (page does not exist)">Zuvand National Park</a> – 15 square kilometres (5.8 sq mi)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Girkan_State_Reserve" title="Girkan State Reserve">Girkan State Reserve</a> – 3 square kilometres (1.2 sq mi)</li>
</ul>
<h4><span id="High_elevation_mountain_rain_forests_(Taiwan)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="High_elevation_mountain_rain_forests_.28Taiwan.29">High elevation mountain rain forests (Taiwan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: High elevation mountain rain forests (Taiwan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>These forests are found in eastern Taiwan and Taiwan's Central Mountain Ranges, part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwan_subtropical_evergreen_forest" title="Taiwan subtropical evergreen forest">Taiwan subtropical evergreen forest</a> region covering the higher elevations. Most of the lower elevations are covered by subtropical broadleaf evergreen forests, dominated by Chinese Cryptocarya (<i><a href="/wiki/Cryptocarya_chinensis" title="Cryptocarya chinensis">Cryptocarya chinensis</a></i>), <i><a href="/wiki/Castanopsis_hystrix" title="Castanopsis hystrix">Castanopsis hystrix</a></i> and Japanese Blue Oak (<i><a href="/wiki/Quercus_glauca" title="Quercus glauca">Quercus glauca</a></i>). Higher elevations give way to temperate forests with large stands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_growth" title="Old growth">old growth</a> Taiwan Cypress (<i><a href="/wiki/Chamaecyparis_taiwanensis" title="Chamaecyparis taiwanensis">Chamaecyparis taiwanensis</a></i>), Camphor tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Cinnamomum_camphora" title="Cinnamomum camphora">Cinnamomum camphora</a></i>), maple (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acer_(genus)" title="Acer (genus)">Acer</a></i> spp.), Chinese yew (<i><a href="/wiki/Taxus_chinensis" title="Taxus chinensis">Taxus chinensis</a></i>), Taiwan Hemlock (<i><a href="/wiki/Tsuga_chinensis" title="Tsuga chinensis">Tsuga chinensis</a></i>), and Taiwan Douglas-fir (<i><a href="/wiki/Pseudotsuga_sinensis" title="Pseudotsuga sinensis">Pseudotsuga sinensis</a> var. wilsoniana</i>). These higher elevation forests include also giant conifers Formosan Cypress (<i><a href="/wiki/Chamaecyparis_formosensis" title="Chamaecyparis formosensis">Chamaecyparis formosensis</a></i>) and Taiwania (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwania_cryptomerioides" title="Taiwania cryptomerioides">Taiwania cryptomerioides</a></i>) Some fine examples of forests are found in Yushan (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jade_Mountain" title="Jade Mountain">Jade Mountain</a>) National Park and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alishan" title="Alishan">Alishan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Taiwan_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Taiwan-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-farjon_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-farjon-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span id="Baekdu_Mountain_Range_(Taebaek_and_Sobaek_Mountain_Ranges)_and_South_Sea_forests_(Korea)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Baekdu_Mountain_Range_.28Taebaek_and_Sobaek_Mountain_Ranges.29_and_South_Sea_forests_.28Korea.29">Baekdu Mountain Range (Taebaek and Sobaek Mountain Ranges) and South Sea forests (Korea)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Baekdu Mountain Range (Taebaek and Sobaek Mountain Ranges) and South Sea forests (Korea)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Southern_Korea_evergreen_forests" title="Southern Korea evergreen forests">Southern Korea evergreen forests</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gotjawal_Forest" title="Gotjawal Forest">Gotjawal Forest</a></div>
<p>The forests that cover the mountains and valleys of the <a href="/wiki/Baekdudaegan" title="Baekdudaegan">Baekdu Mountain Range</a> – from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baekdusan" title="Baekdusan">Mt. Baekdu</a>, in the north, to <a href="/wiki/Jirisan" title="Jirisan">Mt. Jiri</a>, in the southwest, forming the spine of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Peninsula" title="Korean Peninsula">Korean Peninsula</a> – and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Sea_(Korea)" title="South Sea (Korea)">southern coast and islands</a> of the peninsula – including <a href="/wiki/Jeju_Island" title="Jeju Island">Jeju Island</a> – feature a wide variety of conifers and broadleaf trees. Much of these forests are protected in mountain and marine national forests, such as in <a href="/wiki/Hallyeohaesang_National_Park" title="Hallyeohaesang National Park">Hallyeohaesang National Park</a>, which encompasses 150.14 km2 of mountainous forests spread out over 69 uninhabited islands and 30 inhabited islands in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Sea_(Korea)" title="South Sea (Korea)">Korea's South Sea</a> that provide a home to 1,142 plant species, including major species such as red pine, black pine, common camellia, serrata oak, and cork oak, as well as rare species such as nadopungnan (sedirea japonica), daeheongnan (cymbidium nipponicum) and the Korean winter hazel. Major animals species, such as otters, small-eared cats, and badgers also call Hallyeohaesang National Park home, and overall there are 25 mammal species, 115 bird species, 16 reptile species, 1,566 insect species, and 24 freshwater fish species found among the forested, mountains islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Seoraksan_National_Park" title="Seoraksan National Park">Seoraksan National Park</a> covers 398.539㎢ of mountainous forests near the eastern coast of the Korean Peninsula, and is a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> designated Biosphere Preservation District. Over 2,000 animal species live in Seoraksan, including the Korean goral, musk deer, and there are also more than 1,400 rare plant species, such as the edelweiss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span id="Taiheiyo_(Pacific)_rain_forests_(Japan)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Taiheiyo_.28Pacific.29_rain_forests_.28Japan.29">Taiheiyo (Pacific) rain forests (Japan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Taiheiyo (Pacific) rain forests (Japan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Japanese_temperate_rainforest" title="Japanese temperate rainforest">Japanese temperate rainforest</a></div>

<p>Southwestern <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Taiheiyo_evergreen_forests" title="Taiheiyo evergreen forests">Taiheiyo evergreen forests</a> region covers much of <a href="/wiki/Shikoku" title="Shikoku">Shikoku</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kyūshū">Kyūshū</a> Islands, and the Southern/Pacific Ocean-facing side of <a href="/wiki/Honshu" title="Honshu">Honshu</a> ("Taiheiyo" is the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, in Japanese). Here the natural forests are mainly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadleaf" title="Broadleaf">broadleaf</a> <a href="/wiki/Evergreen" title="Evergreen">evergreen</a> in lower <a href="/wiki/Elevation" title="Elevation">elevations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Deciduous" title="Deciduous">deciduous</a> in higher elevations. The limit occurs at 500–1000 metres depending on <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Satoo_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Satoo-26">[26]</a></sup> The main tree species are members of beech <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> (<a href="/wiki/Fagaceae" title="Fagaceae">Fagaceae</a>). In lower altitudes these include evergreen oaks (<i><a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">Quercus</a></i> spp.), Japanese Chinquapin (<i><a href="/wiki/Castanopsis_cuspidata" title="Castanopsis cuspidata">Castanopsis cuspidata</a></i>) and Japanese Stone Oak (<i><a href="/wiki/Lithocarpus_edulis" title="Lithocarpus edulis">Lithocarpus edulis</a></i>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Satoo_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-Satoo-26">[26]</a></sup> and in higher altitudes Japanese Blue Beech (<i><a href="/wiki/Fagus_japonica" title="Fagus japonica">Fagus japonica</a></i>) and Siebold's beech (<i><a href="/wiki/Fagus_crenata" title="Fagus crenata">Fagus crenata</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ching_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ching-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some of the best preserved examples of forest are found in <a href="/wiki/Kirishima-Yaku_National_Park" title="Kirishima-Yaku National Park">Kirishima-Yaku National Park</a> on the Island of <a href="/wiki/Yakushima" title="Yakushima">Yakushima</a> off of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ky%C5%ABsh%C5%AB" title="Kyūshū">Kyūshū</a> in a very wet <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a> (the annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainfall" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a> is 4,000 to 10,000 mm depending on <a href="/wiki/Altitude" title="Altitude">altitude</a>). Because of relatively infertile <a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">soils</a> on <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a>, Yakushima's forests in higher elevations are dominated by a giant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conifer" title="Conifer">conifer</a> species, Japanese Cedar (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cryptomeria_japonica" title="Cryptomeria japonica">Cryptomeria japonica</a></i>), rather than deciduous forests typical of the mainland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-farjon_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-farjon-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yakushima_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yakushima-28">[28]</a></sup> Other areas include <a href="/wiki/Mount_Kirishima" title="Mount Kirishima">Mount Kirishima</a> near <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima" title="Kagoshima">Kagoshima</a> in southern Kyūshū. On Southern Honshū, there is a forest with the <a href="/wiki/Nachi_Falls" title="Nachi Falls">Nachi Falls</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Yoshino-Kumano_National_Park" title="Yoshino-Kumano National Park">Yoshino-Kumano National Park</a>. This particular area of Honshū has been described as one of the rainiest spots in Japan.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oceania">Oceania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Oceania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Australian_temperate_rainforests">Australian temperate rainforests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Australian temperate rainforests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Rainforest_in_Victoria_(Australia)" title="Rainforest in Victoria (Australia)">Rainforest in Victoria (Australia)</a></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> rainforests occur near the mainland east coast and in <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>. There are warm-temperate and cool-temperate rainforests. They are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadleaf" title="Broadleaf">broadleaf</a> <a href="/wiki/Evergreen" title="Evergreen">evergreen</a> forests with the exception of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montane_forest" title="Montane forest">montane</a> rainforests of Tasmania. <a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus" title="Eucalyptus">Eucalypt</a> forests are not classified as rainforests although some eucalypt forest types receive high annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainfall" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a> (to over 2000 mm in <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reid_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reid-29">[29]</a></sup>), and in the absence of <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">fire</a> they may develop to rainforest. If these widespread <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wet_sclerophyll" title="Wet sclerophyll">wet sclerophyll</a> forests were considered rainforests, the total area of rainforest in Australia would be much larger.</p>
<p>Warm-temperate rainforest replaces subtropical rainforest on poorer soils or with increasing altitude and latitude in <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a>. Cool-temperate rainforests are widespread in Tasmania (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tasmanian_temperate_rain_forests" title="Tasmanian temperate rain forests">Tasmanian temperate rain forests</a> ecoregion) and they can be found scattered from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Heritage" title="World Heritage">World Heritage</a> listed <a href="/wiki/Border_Ranges_National_Park" title="Border Ranges National Park">Border Ranges National Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lamington_National_Park" title="Lamington National Park">Lamington National Park</a> on the NSW/Queensland border to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Otway_Ranges" title="Otway Ranges">Otway Ranges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strzelecki_Ranges" title="Strzelecki Ranges">Strzelecki Ranges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dandenong_Ranges" title="Dandenong Ranges">Dandenong Ranges</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tarra_Bulga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tarra Bulga (page does not exist)">Tarra Bulga</a> in Victoria. In the northern NSW they are usually dominated by Antarctic Beech (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_moorei" title="Nothofagus moorei">Nothofagus moorei</a></i>), in the southern NSW by Pinkwood (<i><a href="/wiki/Eucryphia_moorei" title="Eucryphia moorei">Eucryphia moorei</a></i>) and Coachwood (<i><a href="/wiki/Ceratopetalum_apetalum" title="Ceratopetalum apetalum">Ceratopetalum apetalum</a></i>) and in Victoria and Tasmania by Myrtle Beech (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nothofagus_cunninghamii" title="Nothofagus cunninghamii">Nothofagus cunninghamii</a></i>), Southern Sassafras (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atherosperma_moschatum" title="Atherosperma moschatum">Atherosperma moschatum</a></i>) and Mountain Ash (<i><a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus_regnans" title="Eucalyptus regnans">Eucalyptus regnans</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harden_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-harden-30">[30]</a></sup> The montane rainforests of Tasmania are dominated by Tasmanian <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conifer" title="Conifer">conifers</a> (mainly <i><a href="/wiki/Athrotaxis" title="Athrotaxis">Athrotaxis</a></i> spp.).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reid_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-Reid-29">[29]</a></sup> They are dominated by Ferns such as <i><a href="/wiki/Cyathea_cooperi" title="Cyathea cooperi">Cyathea cooperi</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cyathea_australis" title="Cyathea australis">Cyathea australis</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dicksonia_antarctica" title="Dicksonia antarctica">Dicksonia antarctica</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cyathea_cunninghamii" title="Cyathea cunninghamii">Cyathea cunninghamii</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cyathea_leichhardtiana" title="Cyathea leichhardtiana">Cyathea leichhardtiana</a></i>.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand_temperate_rainforests">New Zealand temperate rainforests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_rainforest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: New Zealand temperate rainforests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The temperate rainforests of New Zealand occur on the western shore of the <a href="/wiki/South_Island" title="South Island">South Island</a> and on the <a href="/wiki/North_Island" title="North Island">North Island</a>. The forests are made up of coniferous <a href="/wiki/Podocarpaceae" title="Podocarpaceae">podocarps</a> and broadleaf evergreen trees. The podocarps are abundant at lower elevations, while <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_beech" title="Southern beech">southern beech</a> (<i>Nothofagus</i>) can be found on higher slopes and in the cooler southernmost rainforests. Ecoregions include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiordland_temperate_forests" title="Fiordland temperate forests">Fiordland temperate forests</a> and <a href="/wiki/Westland_temperate_rainforests" title="Westland temperate rainforests">Westland temperate rainforests</a>.</p>
