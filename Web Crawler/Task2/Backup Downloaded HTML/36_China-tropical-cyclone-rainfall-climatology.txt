<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">China tropical cyclone rainfall climatology</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright">

<p>China is a mountainous country, which leads to rapid dissipation of cyclones that move inland as well as significant amounts of rain from those dissipating cyclones. <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Nina_(1975)" title="Typhoon Nina (1975)">Typhoon Nina</a> in 1975 caused the collapse of two huge reservoirs and ten smaller dams when 1062 mm (41.81 inches) of rain fell in Henan Province during a 24‑hour period. <a href="/wiki/1967_Pacific_typhoon_season#Super_Typhoon_Carla_.28Trining.29" title="1967 Pacific typhoon season">Super Typhoon Carla</a> was the wettest tropical cyclone on record for mainland China.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Since 1957, there has been a downward trend in tropical cyclone rainfall for the country.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mainland">Mainland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China_tropical_cyclone_rainfall_climatology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mainland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most of the rain China experiences during the year occurs during the summer months. Typhoons cause many of the intense rains seen within the country. The heavy rains occur over a large area, typically 1,000,000 square kilometres (390,000 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChiRain_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ChiRain-1">[1]</a></sup> Across China between the years of 1983 and 2006, an average of 2.9 tropical cyclones move into Guangdong province, making it the most affected province within mainland China. Hainan averages 1.3 tropical cyclones annually, while Fujian experiences 1.2 tropical cyclones annually, and Zhejiang witnessed 0.9 tropical cyclones annually.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="These are some pretty specific statistics. Where is the source? (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The wettest tropical cyclone on record for the mainland was Super Typhoon Carla, which dropped 2,749 millimetres (108.2 in) of rain over a 48-hour period. <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Nina_(1975)" title="Typhoon Nina (1975)">Typhoon Nina (1975)</a> produced the highest areal average rainfall amounts between August 4 and August 8 for the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hongru_river&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hongru river (page does not exist)">Hongru river</a> basin for most time durations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChiRain_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ChiRain-1">[1]</a></sup> The risk of tropical cyclones across Guangxi, Jiangsu, Shandong, and Liaoning provinces is significantly lower, with these provinces averaging between 0.1 and 0.4 tropical cyclones annually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BAMSapr2009_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-BAMSapr2009-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hong_Kong">Hong Kong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China_tropical_cyclone_rainfall_climatology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hong Kong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Typhoon Sam of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="1999 Pacific typhoon season">1999 Pacific typhoon season</a> became the wettest known tropical cyclone to impact Hong Kong since records began in 1884, breaking a 73‑year‑old record. A total of 23.98 inches/609 mm of rainfall fell between August 22 and August 25.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span id="Taiwan/Taipei"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Taiwan.2FTaipei">Taiwan/Taipei</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China_tropical_cyclone_rainfall_climatology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Taiwan/Taipei">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mountainous island of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> province experiences an average of 1.8 tropical cyclone landfalls each year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BAMSapr2009_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-BAMSapr2009-2">[2]</a></sup> Due to its rugged topography, Taiwan sees extreme rains from tropical cyclones, particularly in its central mountain range.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tibet_Autonomous_Region">Tibet Autonomous Region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=China_tropical_cyclone_rainfall_climatology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tibet Autonomous Region">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An early October 2004 tropical depression brought moisture into the highlands of <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a>, leading to daily precipitation of 60 mm/2.4 inches liquid equivalent to Che-Ku County all in the form of heavy snow, which was a new October daily precipitation record for both rain and snow. This led to a loss of 340,000 kg of food, 230,000 kg of forage grass, and 263 livestock in the snowstorm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PADGETTOCT2004_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-PADGETTOCT2004-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
