<p><b>Rain</b> (also: <i>Rain (Lech)</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Germany" title="Municipalities of Germany">municipality</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Donau-Ries" title="Donau-Ries">Donau-Ries</a> district, in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It is situated on the river <a href="/wiki/Lech_(river)" title="Lech (river)">Lech</a>, close to its confluence with the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>, 11 km east of <a href="/wiki/Donauw%C3%B6rth" title="Donauwörth">Donauwörth</a>.</p>
<p>Rain is on the B16 road and served by the <a href="/wiki/Danube_Valley_Railway_(Bavaria)" title="Danube Valley Railway (Bavaria)">Danube Valley Railway</a> (<i>Donautalbahn</i>), both of which run between <a href="/wiki/Ulm" title="Ulm">Ulm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regensburg" title="Regensburg">Regensburg</a>.</p>
<p>The earliest reference to Rain is in a document of the <a href="/wiki/Niedersch%C3%B6nenfeld" title="Niederschönenfeld">Niederschönenfeld</a> <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a> dated 4 July 1257, wherein it is described as a <i>"civitas nostra"</i> or ducal town. It is most probable that Rain was founded during the reign of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Otto_II_Wittelsbach,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Otto II Wittelsbach, Duke of Bavaria">Otto II Wittelsbach, Duke of Bavaria</a> (1248–1253) or at the latest in the reign of <a href="/wiki/Louis_II,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Louis II, Duke of Bavaria">Louis II, Duke of Bavaria</a> (1253–1294) before 1257.</p>
<p>Rain was founded for economic and strategic reasons. The town protected Bavaria from the northwest and collected <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">customs</a> from its <a href="/wiki/Bridge" title="Bridge">bridge</a> over the river Lech.</p>
<p>On 15 April 1632 during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thirty_Years_War" title="Thirty Years War">Thirty Years War</a> the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rain" title="Battle of Rain">Battle of Rain</a> took place. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protestant" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> <a href="/wiki/Army" title="Army">army</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gustavus_Adolphus" title="Gustavus Adolphus">Gustavus Adolphus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> defeated the army of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_League_(German)" title="Catholic League (German)">Catholic League</a> commanded by Johann Tserclaes von <a href="/wiki/Johann_Tserclaes,_Count_of_Tilly" title="Johann Tserclaes, Count of Tilly">Tilly</a>. Tilly withdrew his troops to <a href="/wiki/Ingolstadt" title="Ingolstadt">Ingolstadt</a>, where he died from <a href="/wiki/Tetanus" title="Tetanus">tetanus</a> from an infected gunshot wound in his thigh.</p>
<p>In 1914 a <a href="/wiki/Monument" title="Monument">monument</a> commemorating Tilly was erected in front of Rain's <a href="/wiki/Rococo" title="Rococo">Rococo</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_hall" title="Town hall">town hall</a>. Rain's architectural heritage also includes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parish</a> <a href="/wiki/Church_(building)" title="Church (building)">church</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_John_the_Baptist" title="St John the Baptist">St John the Baptist</a>, which features late <a href="/wiki/Gothic_art" title="Gothic art">Gothic</a> <a href="/wiki/Fresco" title="Fresco">frescoes</a> dating from about 1480. There is also the Schwabian Gate, the historic Spital with its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Saints%27" title="All Saints'">All Saints'</a> <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapel</a> and the former <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">Castle</a>.</p>
<p>Rain has three museums: the Lachner Brothers Museum, Homeland Museum and Jean-Duprai-Museum. The four Lachner brothers, Theodor, <a href="/wiki/Franz_Lachner" title="Franz Lachner">Franz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ignaz_Lachner" title="Ignaz Lachner">Ignaz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vinzenz_Lachner" title="Vinzenz Lachner">Vinzenz</a>, were nineteenth-century German <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Composers" title="Composers">composers</a>, all of whom were born in the town.</p>
<p>Other historic figures from Rain include lawyer and astronomer <a href="/wiki/Johann_Bayer" title="Johann Bayer">Johann Bayer</a> (1562–1625), humanist <a href="/wiki/Georg_Tannstetter" title="Georg Tannstetter">Georg Tannstetter</a> (1482–1535) and pianist <a href="/wiki/Michael_Raucheisen" title="Michael Raucheisen">Michael Raucheisen</a> (1889–1984).</p>
<p>Rain is the <a href="/wiki/Headquarters" title="Headquarters">headquarters</a> of the Dehner chain of garden centres, which employs 1,100 people in the town. Rain's other industries include a <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a> refinery employing 240-280 people.</p>
<p>Rain is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twin_towns_and_sister_cities" title="Twin towns and sister cities">twinned</a> with <a href="/wiki/Tougan" title="Tougan">Tougan</a>, capital of the <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sourou_Province" title="Sourou Province">Sourou</a> in <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a>.</p>
