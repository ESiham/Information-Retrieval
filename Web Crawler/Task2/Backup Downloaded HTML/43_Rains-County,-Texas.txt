<p><b>Rains County</b> is a <a href="/wiki/County_(United_States)" title="County (United States)">county</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, its population was 10,914.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QF_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-QF-2">[2]</a></sup> Its <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">seat</a> is <a href="/wiki/Emory,_Texas" title="Emory, Texas">Emory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-3">[3]</a></sup> The county (and county seat) are named for <a href="/wiki/Emory_Rains" title="Emory Rains">Emory Rains</a>, a Texas state legislator.</p>
<p>In 1970, <a href="/wiki/Recorded_Texas_Historic_Landmark" title="Recorded Texas Historic Landmark">Recorded Texas Historic Landmark</a> Number 10860 was placed in the county courthouse lawn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rains_County,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" title="U.S. Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the county has a total area of 259 square miles (670 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 229 square miles (590 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 29 square miles (75 km<sup>2</sup>) (11%) is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-5">[5]</a></sup> It is the fourth-smallest county in Texas by land area and fifth-smallest by total area.</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR8_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR8-9">[9]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 10,914 people, 4,188 households, and 2,680 families residing in the county. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 48 people per square mile (19/km²). There were 5,269 housing units at an average density of 23 per square mile (9/km²). The racial makeup of the county was 91.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 2.92% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 1.0% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 0.04% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 2.55% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.6% from two or more races. 7.7% of the population were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race.</p>
<p>There were 4,188 households out of which 28.80% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 61.90% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 9.10% had a female householder with no husband present, and 25.90% were non-families. 22.30% of all households were made up of individuals and 11.20% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.60 and the average family size was 2.92.</p>
<p>In the county, the population was spread out with 23.80% under the age of 18, 7.40% from 18 to 24, 25.10% from 25 to 44, 27.70% from 45 to 64, and 16.10% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 41 years. For every 100 females there were 99.80 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 96.40 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the county was $46,531 and the median income for a family was $40,329. Males had a median income of $31,983 versus $21,594 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the county was $22,933. About 11.40% of families and 14.90% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 17.50% of those under age 18 and 14.10% of those age 65 or over.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rains_County,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The majority of the county is served by the <a href="/wiki/Rains_Independent_School_District" title="Rains Independent School District">Rains Independent School District</a> located in Emory.</p>
<p>The far southeastern portion of the county is served by the <a href="/wiki/Alba-Golden_Independent_School_District" title="Alba-Golden Independent School District">Alba-Golden Independent School District</a>. The far northwestern corner of the county is served by the <a href="/wiki/Lone_Oak_Independent_School_District" title="Lone Oak Independent School District">Lone Oak Independent School District</a>. A portion of north central Rains County is served by the <a href="/wiki/Miller_Grove_Independent_School_District" title="Miller Grove Independent School District">Miller Grove Independent School District</a>.</p>
