<p><b>Bonnie Maxon Gray</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Name_at_birth" title="Name at birth">née</a> <b>Maxon</b>; born September 10, 1981) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> former <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a>, best known by the <a href="/wiki/Ring_name" title="Ring name">ring name</a> <b>Rain</b>. She formerly <a href="/wiki/Tag_team" title="Tag team">teamed</a> with <a href="/wiki/Lacey_(wrestler)" title="Lacey (wrestler)">Lacey</a> to form The Minnesota Home-Wrecking Crew (a play on the name of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minnesota_Wrecking_Crew" title="Minnesota Wrecking Crew">Minnesota Wrecking Crew</a>), but following Lacey's retirement, she teamed with British wrestler <a href="/wiki/Jetta_(wrestler)" title="Jetta (wrestler)">Jetta</a> to form <a href="/wiki/The_International_Home_Wrecking_Crew" title="The International Home Wrecking Crew">The International Home Wrecking Crew</a>. Rain was also the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Shine_Championship" title="Shine Championship">Shine Champion</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">Professional wrestling career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonnie_Maxon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonnie_Maxon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Maxon started working for a local promotion, Steel Domain Wrestling (SDW) in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Paul,_Minnesota" title="Saint Paul, Minnesota">Saint Paul, Minnesota</a>. Later, after gaining the experience of a wrestler, she began working for various promotions in the <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" title="Midwestern United States">Midwest</a>, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IWA_Mid-South" title="IWA Mid-South">IWA Mid-South</a>, <a href="/wiki/Combat_Zone_Wrestling" title="Combat Zone Wrestling">Combat Zone Wrestling</a> (CZW), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NWA_Midwest" title="NWA Midwest">NWA Midwest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ring_of_Honor" title="Ring of Honor">Ring of Honor</a> (ROH), <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Women_Athletes" title="Shimmer Women Athletes">Shimmer Women Athletes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Extreme_Wrestling" title="Women's Extreme Wrestling">Women's Extreme Wrestling</a> under the ring name <b>Rain</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Shimmer_Women_Athletes_(2005–2009)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Shimmer_Women_Athletes_.282005.E2.80.932009.29">Shimmer Women Athletes (2005–2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonnie_Maxon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shimmer Women Athletes (2005–2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 6, 2005, Rain made her debut for <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Women_Athletes" title="Shimmer Women Athletes">Shimmer Women Athletes</a> on the promotion's debut show, losing to <a href="/wiki/Ana_Rocha" title="Ana Rocha">Ariel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> She picked up her first victory in the promotion on <i>Volume 3</i>, which was taped on February 12, 2006, defeating <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Raczynski" title="Nicole Raczynski">Nikki Roxx</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> On May 21 on <i>Volume 6</i>, the team of Rain and <a href="/wiki/Lacey_(wrestler)" title="Lacey (wrestler)">Lacey</a>, known collectively as <a href="/wiki/The_International_Home_Wrecking_Crew" title="The International Home Wrecking Crew">The Minnesota Home Wrecking Crew</a>, made their debut as a tag team, defeating <a href="/wiki/Sara_Del_Rey" title="Sara Del Rey">Sara Del Rey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_Martinez" title="Mercedes Martinez">Mercedes Martinez</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-14"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> After another tag team victory on <i>Volume 8</i> against <a href="/wiki/Cheerleader_Melissa" title="Cheerleader Melissa">Cheerleader Melissa</a> and <a href="/wiki/MsChif" title="MsChif">MsChif</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-15"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> Rain entered the <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Championship" title="Shimmer Championship">Shimmer Championship</a> tournament on <i>Volume 11</i> on June 1, 2007, but was eliminated in the first round by Nikki Roxx.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-16"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> The following day on <i>Volume 13</i>, the Minnesota Home Wrecking Crew suffered its first loss in a match against Del Rey and Roxx.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-17"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> On October 13, Rain and Lacey were joined by <a href="/wiki/Jetta_(wrestler)" title="Jetta (wrestler)">Jetta</a>, and together they formed the stable <a href="/wiki/The_International_Home_Wrecking_Crew" title="The International Home Wrecking Crew">The International Home Wrecking Crew</a>. In their debut match as a trio, they were defeated by <a href="/wiki/Daizee_Haze" title="Daizee Haze">Daizee Haze</a>, MsChif and <a href="/wiki/Eden_Black" title="Eden Black">Eden Black</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-18"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> On April 26, 2008, on <i>Volume 18</i>, Rain and Lacey were defeated by the team of <a href="/wiki/Madison_Rayne" title="Madison Rayne">Ashley Lane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nevaeh_(wrestler)" title="Nevaeh (wrestler)">Nevaeh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-19"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Lacey's retirement, Rain and Jetta continued teaming together, and on <i>Volume 21</i>, taped on October 19, 2008, they entered a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Gauntlet_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">gauntlet match</a> to determine the first ever <a href="/wiki/Shimmer_Tag_Team_Championship" title="Shimmer Tag Team Championship">Shimmer Tag Team Champions</a>. The team entered the match fourth and eliminated <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Blake_(wrestler)" title="Jennifer Blake (wrestler)">Jennifer Blake</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genny_Goulet" title="Genny Goulet">LuFisto</a>, before being eliminated by the eventual winners, Ashley Lane and Nevaeh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-20"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> On <i>Volume 23</i>, Rain and Jetta faced Lane and Nevaeh in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Two_out_of_three_falls_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">two out of three falls match</a> for the tag team titles. During the match, Lacey's debuted as Rain and Jetta's new manager and helped them score the first fall. Lane and Nevaeh came to back to even the score, after Lacey's second interference backfired, but Jetta scored the deciding fall after using a chain given to her by Lacey. However, after referee Bryce Remsburg found out that Jetta had cheated to win, he restarted the match and Lane and Nevaeh ultimately retained the titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling_(2007–2008)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling_.282007.E2.80.932008.29">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (2007–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonnie_Maxon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (2007–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Maxon appeared as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plant_(professional_wrestling)" title="Plant (professional wrestling)">planted fan</a> on the October 18, 2007 episode of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling" title="Total Nonstop Action Wrestling">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling</a> (TNA)'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TNA_Impact!" title="TNA Impact!">Impact!</a></i>, holding up a sign reading "President of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Roode" title="Robert Roode">Robert Roode</a> Fan Club". She continued to appear in the crowd with pro-Roode signs for the following two weeks. On the November 1 episode of <i>Impact!</i>, Maxon's presence was acknowledged by TNA announcers, and she also had a stare-down with Roode's former valet, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tracy_Brookshaw" title="Tracy Brookshaw">Ms. Brooks</a>. In the aftermath of a match on an episode of <i>Impact!</i>, after Ms. Brooks and Roode defeated <a href="/wiki/Chris_Harris_(wrestler)" title="Chris Harris (wrestler)">Chris Harris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gail_Kim" title="Gail Kim">Gail Kim</a>, Brooks was about to leave the ring when she attacked and started a fight with Maxon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-21"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> Following the altercation, she was seen from far distances around the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TNA_Impact_Zone" title="TNA Impact Zone">TNA Impact Zone</a> in backstage segments. She was referred as Ms. Brooks's <a href="/wiki/Stalking" title="Stalking">stalker</a> by TNA commentators <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_West_(sportscaster)" title="Don West (sportscaster)">Don West</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Tenay" title="Mike Tenay">Mike Tenay</a>.</p>
<p>Maxon made a further appearance on the January 10, 2008 episode of <i>Impact!</i> and attacked Brooks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-22"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> Subsequently, Roode announced that she had been replaced by Maxon, under the new name <b>(Ms.) Peyton Banks</b> (later tweaked to <b>Payton Banks</b>), resulting in a feud with Brooks and a match at <a href="/wiki/TNA_Against_All_Odds#2008" title="TNA Against All Odds">Against All Odds</a>, which Brooks won.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-23"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Destination_X#2008" title="Destination X">Destination X</a>, Banks was part of the "Stand By Your Man" Strap match, where she was in the corner of Roode, and Brooks was in the corner of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Booker_Huffman" title="Booker Huffman">Booker T</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-24"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> The match stipulation dictated that the loser of the match would have their valet whipped with a strap.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-25"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> Booker T lost the match, and Banks whipped Brooks with the leather strap until Brooks was saved by Booker's wife, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sharmell_Sullivan" title="Sharmell Sullivan">Sharmell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-26"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Lockdown_(2008)" title="Lockdown (2008)">Lockdown</a>, Booker T and Sharmell defeated Roode and Banks in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_tag_team_match_types#Mixed_tag_team_match" title="Professional wrestling tag team match types">mixed tag team match</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-27"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup> After the match, Roode verbally abused Banks in the ring and prepared to hit her, but instead left her alone in the ring. Following the angle with Roode, Maxon left TNA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_2-28"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Asistencia,_Asesoría_y_Administración_(2009–2010)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Asistencia.2C_Asesor.C3.ADa_y_Administraci.C3.B3n_.282009.E2.80.932010.29">Asistencia, Asesoría y Administración (2009–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonnie_Maxon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Asistencia, Asesoría y Administración (2009–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/La_Legi%C3%B3n_Extranjera" title="La Legión Extranjera">La Legión Extranjera</a></div>
<p>On August 21, 2009, at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verano_de_Escandalo_(2009)" title="Verano de Escandalo (2009)">Verano de Escandalo</a>, Rain made her debut for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucha_Libre_AAA_World_Wide" title="Lucha Libre AAA World Wide">Lucha Libre AAA World Wide</a> (AAA) as a member of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_Legion_(wrestling)" title="Foreign Legion (wrestling)">La Legión Extranjera</a></i>, led by <a href="/wiki/Konnan" title="Konnan">Konnan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the promotion she regularly teams with Jennifer Blade and <a href="/wiki/Sexy_Star" title="Sexy Star">Sexy Star</a> as well as acting as the valet of <a href="/wiki/Alex_Koslov" title="Alex Koslov">Alex Koslov</a>. At the 2010 <i><a href="/wiki/Rey_de_Reyes_(2010)" title="Rey de Reyes (2010)">Rey de Reyes</a></i> show Rain, <a href="/wiki/Christina_Von_Eerie" title="Christina Von Eerie">Christina Von Eerie</a> and Sexy Star faced off against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinthia_Moreno" title="Cinthia Moreno">Cinthia Moreno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mari_Apache" title="Mari Apache">Mari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Faby_Apache" title="Faby Apache">Faby Apache</a>, a match which Moreno won for her team by pinning Sexy Star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAAReydeReys_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-AAAReydeReys-10">[10]</a></sup> During a post <i>Rey de Reyes</i> interview Sexy Star claimed that the Apaches and Cintia Moreno were nothing but maids, which led to AAA booking a match between Cinthia Moreno, Faby and Mari Apache against Sexy Star, Rain and Jennifer Blade in a <a href="/wiki/Tripleman%C3%ADa_XVIII" title="Triplemanía XVIII">Triplemanía XVIII</a> match where the person pinned or submitted would have to be the winning team's slave for a month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WomensMatch_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-WomensMatch-11">[11]</a></sup> At Triplemanía <i>La Legión</i> defeated Moreno and the Apaches, when Blade pinned Mari, thanks in part to the biased refereering by Hijo del Tirantes. Following the match Konnan ordered Mari Apache to begin her maid duty right away by cleaning up their dressing room. The stipulation expired on July 6, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAALive_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-AAALive-12">[12]</a></sup> Rain's contract with AAA expired on July 25, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Wrestlicious_(2009–2010)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Wrestlicious_.282009.E2.80.932010.29">Wrestlicious (2009–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonnie_Maxon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Wrestlicious (2009–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early 2009 Maxon took part in the tapings of <a href="/wiki/Wrestlicious" title="Wrestlicious">Wrestlicious</a>, under the ring name Felony, which began airing in March 2010. She debuted on March 1 in the first episode teaming with <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_Martinez" title="Mercedes Martinez">Maria Toro</a> and Bandita defeating the team of Cousin Cassie, <a href="/wiki/Lorelei_Lee" title="Lorelei Lee">Tyler Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amber_O%27Neal" title="Amber O'Neal">Charlotte</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On the April 7 edition of <i>Takedown</i>, Felony was one of the last two girls along with <a href="/wiki/Christie_Ricci" title="Christie Ricci">Glory</a> standing in a 20-girl over-the-top-rope "Hoedown Throwdown" battle royal to determine the top two contenders for the Wrestlicious Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On the May 24 episode of <i>Wrestlicious Takedown</i>, Felony lost the Wrestlicious Championship match to Glory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Women_Superstars_Uncensored_(2009–2012)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Women_Superstars_Uncensored_.282009.E2.80.932012.29">Women Superstars Uncensored (2009–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonnie_Maxon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Women Superstars Uncensored (2009–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rain debuted in WSU in January 2009 with two victories over <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Summerlyn" title="Rachel Summerlyn">Rachel Summerlyn</a> on separate DVD tapings in one night. She would go on to defeat <a href="/wiki/Malia_Hosaka" title="Malia Hosaka">Malia Hosaka</a> at WSU's 2 year anniversary show. She went to the finals of WSU's 2009 J-Cup but lost to <a href="/wiki/Angel_Orsini" title="Angel Orsini">Angel Orsini</a> in the finals. The following night she received her first WSU Championship shot in a three-way match involving Orsini and champion <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_Martinez" title="Mercedes Martinez">Mercedes Martinez</a> in which Mercedes retained her title. Rain picked up a win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nikki_Roxx" title="Nikki Roxx">Nikki Roxx</a> in June and in August, she teamed up with Orsini to challenge for the WSU tag team titles which they lost via DQ. Later that night on WSU's second DVD taping, the duo were successful against Nikki Roxx &amp; Mercedes Martinez. Rain and Orsini's partnership would not last long and they would become enemies again. On October 3, 2009, Rain defeated Angel Orsini in an uncensored rules match.</p>
<p>Rain would go on to defeat <a href="/wiki/Portia_Perez" title="Portia Perez">Portia Perez</a> to become the #1 contender to the WSU Championship. Rain would face Mercedes Martinez for the title in the main event of WSU's 3 year anniversary show in March 2010. Mercedes successfully defended her title against Rain, defeating her in an uncensored rules match on March 6, 2010. Rain would main event WSU's next show which was the first of two tapings in a loss to <a href="/wiki/Kia_Stevens" title="Kia Stevens">Awesome Kong</a>. Rain would form her own alliance known as Rain's Army with Nikki Roxx and Jessicka Havok later in 2010. At WSU's first ever internet pay per view, Breaking Barries, Rain picked up an impressive victory over Jamilia Craft in just 3 minutes. The win garnered Rain another title shot on November 6, 2010. However, Mercedes was once again successful, defeating Rain this time in an I Quit match.</p>
<p>Rain's army would come to an end when Jessicka Havok turned on Rain and challenged her to a match which Havok won at WSU's 4 year anniversary show on March 5, 2011. From there, Rain would challenge for the WSU Spirit Championship at the Uncensored Rumble iPPV but came up short, losing to champion Sassy Stephanie. Rain would enter the Uncensored Rumble match but was eliminated by Amy Lee. Rain would turn to more of a fan-favorite after as she wrestled <a href="/wiki/Serena_Deeb" title="Serena Deeb">Serena Deeb</a> at WSU's next event. Serena won the match but afterwards, they were both assaulted by tag team champions, the Boston Shore. Rain and Serena agreed to form a tag team to challenge for the titles but Serena ended up sidelined with a concussion leading Rain to call upon her longtime former tag team partner, <a href="/wiki/Jetta_(wrestler)" title="Jetta (wrestler)">Jetta</a>, to reform the International Home Wrecking Crew.</p>
<p>In September, Rain won the WSU Spirit Championship, her first title win in WSU, by defeating <a href="/wiki/Sassy_Stephie" title="Sassy Stephie">Sassy Stephanie</a>. However, she would lose the championship later that very same night when she accepted an impromptu challenge from Jessicka Havok, who defeated her for the belt. On November 19, 2011 Rain and Jetta reformed the International Home Wrecking Crew but lost to the Boston Shore (Amber &amp; Lexxus) in a tag titles match. Rain would later save <a href="/wiki/Melina_Perez" title="Melina Perez">Melina</a> from a Boston Shore attack. At WSU's 5 Year Anniversary show on March 3, 2012, Rain was pinned by Lexxus, but bounced back with a win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athena_(wrestler)" title="Athena (wrestler)">Athena</a> at the April 24th show of WSU. In which, Rain announced plans to put her career on the line in the Uncensored Rumble match on June 16, however she was forced to cancel due to an illness. WSU was then sold to new owners and Rain subsequently announced on July 14 that she could not come to a deal with the new owners and thus would not be returning to the promotion.</p>
<h3><span id="Shine_Wrestling_(2012–2014)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Shine_Wrestling_.282012.E2.80.932014.29">Shine Wrestling (2012–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonnie_Maxon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Shine Wrestling (2012–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rain made her debut for new women's wrestling promotion <a href="/wiki/Shine_Wrestling" title="Shine Wrestling">Shine Wrestling</a>, stylized as SHINE, on July 20 in Ybor City, Florida where she defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nikki_Roxx" title="Nikki Roxx">Nikki Roxx</a>. At the next event on August 17, Rain continued her winning streak by defeating Santana Garrett. Rain competed in the main event of Shine 3 against Jazz on September 21 where she suffered her first defeat. At Shine 4 on October 19, Rain defeated Christina Von Eerie and later that night she would cost Jazz her match in the main event. That led to Shine 5 on November 16 where Rain teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_Martinez" title="Mercedes Martinez">Mercedes Martinez</a> in a losing effort against Jazz and Amazing Kong. During that match, Rain suffered a broken wrist.</p>
<p>At Shine 6 on January 11, 2013, Rain did not compete due to injury but interfered in the main event, costing Amazing Kong her match against Mercedes Martinez. After the match, Rain formed her new group called The Valkyrie consisting of herself, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ivelisse_Velez" title="Ivelisse Velez">Ivelisse Velez</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allysin_Kay" title="Allysin Kay">Allysin Kay</a>, Taylor Made, and <a href="/wiki/April_Hunter" title="April Hunter">April Hunter</a>.</p>
<p>On February 5, 2013, Rain officially announced that she would retire from wrestling at the end of the year. On February 22, Rain headlined Shine 7 with a DQ victory over Amazing Kong. At Shine 8 in March, Rain led the Valkyrie to victory in an eight-woman tag team match. Shine announced a tournament to crown its first champion and Rain defeated <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Love" title="Angelina Love">Angelina Love</a> to advance to the tournament. On July 12, Rain first defeated LuFisto in the first round, then <a href="/wiki/Jessicka_Havok" title="Jessicka Havok">Jessicka Havok</a> in the semifinals and finally <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mia_Yim" title="Mia Yim">Mia Yim</a> in the finals to win the tournament and become the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Shine_Championship" title="Shine Championship">Shine Champion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShineChamp_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ShineChamp-17">[17]</a></sup> On December 13, Rain successfully defended her title against Amazing Kong in what was billed as her retirement match. However, after the match, she announced that she was not retiring after all and would continue to defend the title in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On January 24, Rain lost the Shine Championship to Valkyrie stablemate Ivelisse Vélez, who was post-match kicked out of the stable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FinalSHINE_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-FinalSHINE-19">[19]</a></sup> Following the loss, Rain officially retired from wrestling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
