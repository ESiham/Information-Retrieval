<p><b>Douglas Rain</b> (born March 13, 1928) is a <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> actor and narrator. Though primarily a stage actor, he is also known for providing the voice of the <a href="/wiki/HAL_9000" title="HAL 9000">HAL 9000</a> computer for the film <i><a href="/wiki/2001:_A_Space_Odyssey_(film)" title="2001: A Space Odyssey (film)">2001: A Space Odyssey</a></i> (1968) and its sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/2010_(film)" title="2010 (film)">2010</a></i> (1984).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Douglas_Rain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rain was born in <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg" title="Winnipeg">Winnipeg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a>. He studied acting at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banff_School_of_Fine_Arts" title="Banff School of Fine Arts">Banff School of Fine Arts</a> in <a href="/wiki/Banff,_Alberta" title="Banff, Alberta">Banff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Old_Vic_Theatre_School" title="Bristol Old Vic Theatre School">Bristol Old Vic Theatre School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.</p>
<p>As a stage actor, his association with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stratford_Festival_of_Canada" title="Stratford Festival of Canada">Stratford Festival of Canada</a> spans more than four decades. He has performed in a wide variety of theatrical roles, most notably in a <a href="/wiki/Stratford,_Ontario" title="Stratford, Ontario">Stratford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> production of <i><a href="/wiki/Henry_V_(play)" title="Henry V (play)">Henry V</a></i>, which was adapted for television in 1966.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1972, Rain was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> for Best Supporting or Featured Actor (Dramatic) for his performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Vivat!_Vivat_Regina!" title="Vivat! Vivat Regina!">Vivat! Vivat Regina!</a></i></p>
