<p><i><b>Rain</b></i> is a 1932 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Seas_(genre)" title="South Seas (genre)">South Seas</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Milestone" title="Lewis Milestone">Lewis Milestone</a> with portions filmed at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Catalina_Island,_California" title="Santa Catalina Island, California">Santa Catalina Island, California</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Pre-Code_Hollywood" title="Pre-Code Hollywood">pre-Code</a> film stars <a href="/wiki/Joan_Crawford" title="Joan Crawford">Joan Crawford</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prostitute" title="Prostitute">prostitute</a> Sadie Thompson and features <a href="/wiki/Walter_Huston" title="Walter Huston">Walter Huston</a> as a conflicted <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionary</a> who wants to reform Sadie, but whose own morals start decaying. Crawford was loaned out by <a href="/wiki/Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer" title="Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer">MGM</a> to <a href="/wiki/United_Artists" title="United Artists">United Artists</a> for this film.</p>
<p>The plot of the film is based on the 1922 play <i>Rain</i> by <a href="/wiki/John_Colton_(screenwriter)" title="John Colton (screenwriter)">John Colton</a> and Clemence Randolph, which in turn was based on the short story "<a href="/wiki/Rain_(short_story)" title="Rain (short story)">Miss Thompson</a>" (later retitled "Rain") by <a href="/wiki/W._Somerset_Maugham" title="W. Somerset Maugham">W. Somerset Maugham</a>. Actress <a href="/wiki/Jeanne_Eagels" title="Jeanne Eagels">Jeanne Eagels</a> had played the role on stage. Other movie versions of the story include: a <a href="/wiki/1928_in_film" title="1928 in film">1928</a> <a href="/wiki/Silent_film" title="Silent film">silent film</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Sadie_Thompson" title="Sadie Thompson">Sadie Thompson</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Swanson" title="Gloria Swanson">Gloria Swanson</a>, and the heavily sanitized <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Sadie_Thompson" title="Miss Sadie Thompson">Miss Sadie Thompson</a></i> (1953), which starred <a href="/wiki/Rita_Hayworth" title="Rita Hayworth">Rita Hayworth</a>.</p>
<p>In 1960, the film entered the <a href="/wiki/List_of_films_in_the_public_domain_in_the_United_States" title="List of films in the public domain in the United States">public domain in the USA</a> due to the copyright claimant's failure to renew the <a href="/wiki/Copyright_registration" title="Copyright registration">copyright registration</a> in the 28th year after publication.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(1932_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Joan_Crawford_in_Rain_4.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="552" data-file-width="554" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Joan_Crawford_in_Rain_4.jpg/150px-Joan_Crawford_in_Rain_4.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Joan_Crawford_in_Rain_4.jpg/225px-Joan_Crawford_in_Rain_4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Joan_Crawford_in_Rain_4.jpg/300px-Joan_Crawford_in_Rain_4.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Joan_Crawford_in_Rain_4.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Joan Crawford as Sadie</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A westbound ship en route to <a href="/wiki/Apia" title="Apia">Apia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a>, is temporarily stranded at nearby <a href="/wiki/Pago_Pago" title="Pago Pago">Pago Pago</a> due to a possible <a href="/wiki/Cholera" title="Cholera">cholera</a> outbreak on board. Among the passengers are Alfred Davidson, a self-righteous missionary, his wife, and Sadie Thompson, a prostitute. Thompson passes the time partying and drinking with the American Marines stationed on the island. <a href="/wiki/Sergeant" title="Sergeant">Sergeant</a> Tim O'Hara, nicknamed by Sadie as "Handsome", falls in love with her.</p>
<p>Her wild behavior soon becomes more than the Davidsons can stand and Mr. Davidson confronts Sadie, resolving to save her soul. When she dismisses his offer, Davidson has the Governor order her deported to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, where she is wanted for an unspecified crime (for which she says she was framed). She begs Davidson to allow her to remain on the island a few more days – her plan is to flee to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney,_Australia" title="Sydney, Australia">Sydney, Australia</a>. During a heated argument with Davidson, she experiences a <a href="/wiki/Religious_conversion" title="Religious conversion">religious conversion</a> and agrees to return to San Francisco and the jail sentence awaiting her there.</p>
<p>The evening before she is to leave, Sergeant O'Hara asks Sadie to marry him and offers to hide her until the Sydney boat sails, but she refuses. Later, while native drums beat, the repressed Davidson satisfies his lust with Sadie. The next morning he is found dead on the beach – a <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a>. Davidson's hypocrisy and betrayal cause Thompson to return to her old self and she goes off to Sydney with O'Hara to start a new life.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(1932_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Rain</i> was not well received – either critically or financially – upon initial release. The unglamorous role for Crawford, and bold story (religious hypocrisy being its main theme), caught <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Great_Depression" title="The Great Depression">Depression</a>-era audiences off guard.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Motion_Picture_Herald" title="Motion Picture Herald">Motion Picture Herald</a></i> commented, "Because the producers have made such a strong attempt to establish the stern impressiveness of the story, it is rather slow. In its drive to become powerful, it appears to have lost the spark of spontaneity....Joan Crawford and Walter Huston are satisfactory."</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> noted, "It turns out to be a mistake to have assigned the Sadie Thompson role to Miss Crawford. It shows her off unfavorably. The dramatic significance of it all is beyond her range.... [Director] Milestone tried to achieve action with the camera, but wears the witnesses down with words. Joan Crawford's get-up as the light lady is extremely bizarre. Pavement pounders don't quite trick themselves up as fantastically as all that. In commercial favor of <i>Rain</i> is the general repute of the theme and Miss Crawford's personal following, but the finished product will not help either."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(1932_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film earned $538,000 in the United States and Canada and $166,000 elsewhere, resulting in a loss of $198,000.</p>
