<p><i><b>Rain</b></i> is a 2006 film directed by Craig DiBona. The screenplay was written by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Neiderman" title="Andrew Neiderman">Andrew Neiderman</a>, based on the <a href="/wiki/V._C._Andrews#The_Hudson_series" title="V. C. Andrews">novel</a> by <a href="/wiki/V._C._Andrews" title="V. C. Andrews">V. C. Andrews</a>. It premiered at the Palm Beach International Film Festival, although it did not receive further domestic distribution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(2006_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A talented young pianist named Rain (<a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Sudano" title="Brooklyn Sudano">Brooklyn Sudano</a>) is attacked by a vicious street gang which kills her sister. The gang sets out to find Rain while she hides in the care of a woman who is her natural grandmother. Rain was put up for adoption because the father of the baby was black and the mother was from a rich white family. Her adoptive mother (<a href="/wiki/Khandi_Alexander" title="Khandi Alexander">Khandi Alexander</a>) sends her back because she is in danger for having witnessed her adoptive sister's murder.</p>
