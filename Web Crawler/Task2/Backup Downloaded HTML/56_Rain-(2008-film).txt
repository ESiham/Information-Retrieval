<p><i><b>Rain</b></i> is a 2008 film directed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Govan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maria Govan (page does not exist)">Maria Govan</a>. This was among the first feature productions to be shot entirely by citizens of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>. <i>Rain</i> marks the final film of actor <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Lockhart" title="Calvin Lockhart">Calvin Lockhart</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A young Bahamian girl, Rain, boards a local mail boat from <a href="/wiki/Ragged_Island,_Bahamas" title="Ragged Island, Bahamas">Ragged Island</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety-review_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety-review-4">[4]</a></sup> and sets sail for Nassau. The death of her grandmother has forced her to get out and explore the world on her own. When she arrives in <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau</a>, the sights of the big city overwhelm her, and soon she finds her idealistic illusions shattered when she finds how destructive her mother's lifestyle has truly become. Stranded in an unfamiliar environment that fills her with dread and confronted by a mother she has never known, Rain searches desperately to find her own place in the world.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Rain</i> was the first film to be directed by Maria Gowan, who raised the US$1 million budget through Bahamian investors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety-1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1-1">[1]</a></sup> It was also one of the first features to be produced entirely in the Bahamas (the first being "Filthy Rich Gangster"), which had long been a filming location for the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Bond_(film_series)" title="James Bond (film series)">James Bond</a></i> films among other efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hr-feature_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-hr-feature-5">[5]</a></sup> Govan's film was shot during four weeks on three different islands of the Bahamas, including <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcn_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-mcn-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Eleuthera" title="Eleuthera">Eleuthera</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hr-feature_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-hr-feature-5">[5]</a></sup> filming also took place in New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hr-feature_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-hr-feature-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In early September 2008, <i>Rain</i> received its world premiere at the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety-1_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hr-brief_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-hr-brief-2">[2]</a></sup> On December 4, 2008, it became the opening selection of the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Bahamas_International_Film_Festival" title="Bahamas International Film Festival">Bahamas International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety-1_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-variety-1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hr-brief_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-hr-brief-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Rain</i> was released on DVD in the U.S. on July 27, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcn_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-mcn-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the film was shown at the Toronto Film Festival, Alissa Simon of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> said: "Displaying an eye for composition, helmer Govan (who spent two years observing a crack house for docu <i>Where I’m From: HIV and AIDS in the Bahamas</i>), knows her milieu — essentially the antithesis of Bahamian tourist-board images. Martina Radwan’s gritty lensing and Denise Hudson’s detailed production design provide ample support." She, however, commented on "the script's melodramatic cliches and some weak supporting [performances]".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety-review_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-variety-review-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
