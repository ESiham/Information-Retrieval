<p><b>Rain</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_(series)" title="Mortal Kombat (series)">Mortal Kombat</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Fighting_game" title="Fighting game">fighting game</a> franchise by <a href="/wiki/Midway_Games" title="Midway Games">Midway Games</a>. Originating as a <a href="/wiki/Palette_swap" title="Palette swap">palette-swapped</a> joke <a href="/wiki/Red_herring" title="Red herring">red herring</a> inserted into <i><a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Mortal_Kombat_3" title="Ultimate Mortal Kombat 3">Ultimate Mortal Kombat 3</a></i> by <i>Mortal Kombat</i> co-creator <a href="/wiki/Ed_Boon" title="Ed Boon">Ed Boon</a> in order to stir up fan interest in the game, he became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Playable_character" title="Playable character">playable character</a> in the follow-up title <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_Trilogy" title="Mortal Kombat Trilogy">Mortal Kombat Trilogy</a>.</i></p>
<p>In the games, Rain hails from the otherworldly realm of Edenia like <a href="/wiki/Kitana" title="Kitana">Kitana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jade_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Jade (Mortal Kombat)">Jade</a>, but he does not share his compatriots' allegiance to their homeland and instead opts to serve evil Outworld emperor <a href="/wiki/Shao_Kahn" title="Shao Kahn">Shao Kahn</a>. He plays his most prominent role in <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat:_Armageddon" title="Mortal Kombat: Armageddon">Mortal Kombat: Armageddon</a></i>, where he is revealed to be a demigod and related to the protagonist of the game's storyline.</p>
<p>Since his <i>UMK3</i> debut, Rain's presence in the <i>Mortal Kombat</i> franchise has been relatively sparse, as he has been selectable in only the series' two compilation titles (<i>Trilogy</i> and <i>Armageddon</i>) and the 2011 <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_(2011_game)" title="Mortal Kombat (2011 game)">Mortal Kombat</a></i> reboot, for which he was a later addition to the character roster as <a href="/wiki/Downloadable_content" title="Downloadable content">downloadable content</a>, while he appears as a nonplayable character in 2015's <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_X" title="Mortal Kombat X">Mortal Kombat X</a></i>. General critical reception to the character ranges from mixed to negative.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_creation">History and creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Mortal_Kombat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and creation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rain's first appearance in the <i>Mortal Kombat</i> series literally spans one second, when he is spotted running up to and attacking <a href="/wiki/Shao_Kahn" title="Shao Kahn">Shao Kahn</a> on the Portal stage in the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_video_game_terms#Attract_mode" title="Glossary of video game terms">attract mode</a> sequence of 1995's <i><a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Mortal_Kombat_3" title="Ultimate Mortal Kombat 3">Ultimate Mortal Kombat 3</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A second and less frequent sighting in the sequence saw him facing Kahn on the Portal bridge and merely falling over backwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-4">[4]</a></sup> Similar to <a href="/wiki/Ermac" title="Ermac">Ermac</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_(1992_video_game)" title="Mortal Kombat (1992 video game)">first <i>Mortal Kombat</i></a>, players attempted to hunt him down to no avail, and the speculation regarding his existence was further aggravated by a message activated by a Kombat Kode that read, "Rain can be found in the Graveyard," in reference to a stage in the game where he was never actually seen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mksecrets_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mksecrets-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Series co-creator <a href="/wiki/Ed_Boon" title="Ed Boon">Ed Boon</a> explained that Rain's conception "was me being a jerk, basically."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainboon_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-rainboon-6">[6]</a></sup> A huge fan of <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>, Boon's inspiration for the character was the 1984 track <i><a href="/wiki/Purple_Rain_(song)" title="Purple Rain (song)">Purple Rain</a></i>, coupled with his jokingly wondering what color palette had not yet been used in the games. In another nod to the singer, Rain addresses himself as "Prince of Edenia" in <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat:_Armageddon" title="Mortal Kombat: Armageddon">Mortal Kombat: Armageddon</a></i>. By the time Boon had decided to include him as a selectable in <i>UMK3,</i> the game was already completed, so due to time constraints he had to hack into the attract mode to insert a snippet of the character as an unplayable red herring who was a purple <a href="/wiki/Palette_swap" title="Palette swap">palette swap</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Scorpion (Mortal Kombat)">Scorpion</a>, while creating a new font for displaying his name in his power bar; therefore, it is the only one in the game that is not italicized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainboon_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-rainboon-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rain was made a playable character with his own backstory and distinct set of moves for the home versions of <i>UMK3</i> and the 1996 home-release compilation title <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_Trilogy" title="Mortal Kombat Trilogy">Mortal Kombat Trilogy</a>,</i> the latter which was to actually include another new ninja character, Tremor, a brown palette swap who had been axed during the development process (he later made his first series appearance in the 2000 spinoff <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat:_Special_Forces" title="Mortal Kombat: Special Forces">Mortal Kombat: Special Forces</a></i>). The noncanonical biography that had been created for him was passed on to Rain instead and included in the game's instruction manual.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[note 1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances">Appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Mortal_Kombat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_video_games">In video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Mortal_Kombat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a child, Rain was smuggled away from his homeland of Edenia in the midst of the realm's takeover by Outworld emperor Shao Kahn, while his father stayed behind to perform his duties as general of Edenia's armies, but he was killed and his armies crushed in the invasion. Thousands of years later, during the events of <i>MK Trilogy,</i> Rain resurfaced during Kahn's invasion of Earthrealm prior to the third Mortal Kombat tournament, and was attacked by Kahn's extermination squads; not wanting to suffer at their hand, he elected to turn his back on his homeland and side with Kahn, who trained him as an assassin alongside fellow Edenians <a href="/wiki/Kitana" title="Kitana">Kitana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jade_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Jade (Mortal Kombat)">Jade</a> before enlisting them in the fight against the Earth warriors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> However, Kahn's would-be domination of Earthrealm is unsuccessful and Rain is absent from the series until the Konquest mode of <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat:_Deception" title="Mortal Kombat: Deception">Mortal Kombat: Deception</a></i> (2004), where he asks <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Shujinko" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">Shujinko</a> to find a dagger belonging to Goro, which he in turn keeps for himself until he is knocked out by Jade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attackfanboy_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-attackfanboy-10">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>MK: Armageddon</i> (2006) marks Rain's return as a playable after a decade. As one of only seventeen characters in the game to receive an official biography,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mksecretsbios_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-mksecretsbios-11">[10]</a></sup> he plays his largest role in the original series continuity by learning of his true Edenian heritage from Outworld sorcerer <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Quan_Chi" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">Quan Chi</a>, who lets Rain in on a secret that his late father had tried to keep from him: that he is actually a direct descendant of Argus, the protector god of Edenia, in addition to being the half-sibling of the game's protagonist <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Taven" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">Taven</a> and his brother <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Daegon" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">Daegon</a>, both of whom were favored by their father to assume his mantle of Edenia's protectors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainwarehouse_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-rainwarehouse-12">[11]</a></sup> Rain consequently starts to refer to himself as a prince of the realm, as seen in <i>Armageddon's</i> Konquest mode, but he still chooses to independently fight on the side of evil. Rain confronts Taven in Arctika but is defeated in battle and flees into a portal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainkonquest_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-rainkonquest-13">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>His storyline is altered in the 2011 <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_(2011_game)" title="Mortal Kombat (2011 game)">Mortal Kombat</a></i> reboot, which makes no mention of his father or siblings, instead describing him as having been orphaned at a young age by Kahn's conquest of Edenia and raised under the protection of Edenian resistance fighters. As he gained a reputation as an exceptional warrior, his level of arrogance followed suit, and when he was refused leadership of the resistance, he <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">betrayed them</a> to their (unidentified) sworn enemy. This caught the attention of Kahn, who offered the power-hungry Rain an army of his own in exchange for his services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kamidogu_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-kamidogu-14">[13]</a></sup> However, in Rain's ending, Kahn had no intention of fulfilling his end of the bargain. Rain therefore kills him in combat and ends up saving Earthrealm in the process, for which he is thanked by <a href="/wiki/Raiden_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Raiden (Mortal Kombat)">Raiden</a>, who also informs him that he is the son of Argus. Rain, consequently drunk with power to which he felt he was entitled, coupled with a sense of superiority from being the offspring of a god, takes over Kahn's armies to rule all realms, starting, to Raiden's horror, with Earthrealm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainMK9ending_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-rainMK9ending-15">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rain is not playable in <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_X" title="Mortal Kombat X">Mortal Kombat X</a></i> (2015), but is a supporting character in the game's story mode. He serves as Mileena's advisor and tries to help her reclaim the Outworld throne from the warlord <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kotal_Kahn" title="Kotal Kahn">Kotal Kahn</a> in the midst of a civil war. Rain plans to take the throne for himself when the war is over, but is stopped by <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#D.27Vorah" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">D'Vorah</a>. He is featured in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tanya_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Tanya (Mortal Kombat)">Tanya</a>'s noncanonical arcade ending, in which he and Tanya flee Kotal Kahn's forces in Outworld but are captured. She turns him over to Kahn in exchange for <a href="/wiki/Pardon" title="Pardon">clemency</a>, and Rain is burned at the stake.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_gameplay">Design and gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Mortal_Kombat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Design and gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>Rain was the tenth palette-swapped human ninja introduced into the <i>Mortal Kombat</i> series. His uniform in the attract mode was initially a <a href="/wiki/Burgundy_(color)" title="Burgundy (color)">burgundy</a> color,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-4">[4]</a></sup> which was altered to traditional purple in the home versions of <i>UMK3</i> and <i>MK Trilogy.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gameinformer_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-gameinformer-16">[15]</a></sup> His offense drew on the power of <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">weather</a> as he was able to harness water and lightning, while he shared <a href="/wiki/Reptile_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Reptile (Mortal Kombat)">Reptile</a>'s victory pose and the stance for Ermac's Telekinetic Slam for his lightning strike, which caused opponents to bounce into the air and thus set them up for a <a href="/wiki/Combo_(video_gaming)#Juggle_combo" title="Combo (video gaming)">juggle combo</a>. His roundhouse kick was unique to the series as it sent opponents flying onto the opposite side of the screen, while his Animality turned him into a <a href="/wiki/Pygmy_elephant" title="Pygmy elephant">pygmy elephant</a> that trumpets the skin off his opponent. Bryan Dawson of <a href="/wiki/Prima_Games" title="Prima Games">Prima Games</a> named Rain in <i>MK Trilogy</i> as one of the series' "cheapest" characters, citing his specials therein that led to lengthy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combo_(gaming)" title="Combo (gaming)">combos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-primacheap_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-primacheap-17">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>For his reemergence in <i>Armageddon,</i> Rain was given a new, distinct identity like the other ninjas from the two-dimensional games, with less emphasis on purple for his uniform and more on black and gold, giving him a more regal appearance that now included a back-length cape, while he still concealed his identity behind a mask that itself was a standalone entity as he was now shown as having long, black hair tied up in a <a href="/wiki/Chonmage" title="Chonmage">topknot</a>. In his official <i>Armageddon</i> render, he is seen brandishing a pair of curved knives but never actually uses them in the game; instead, his weapon is a sword. He was given more water-based special moves for <i>Armageddon</i> and the 2011 <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_(2011_game)" title="Mortal Kombat (2011 game)">Mortal Kombat</a></i> reboot, the latter for which he was not included on the original roster but was later added by <a href="/wiki/NetherRealm_Studios" title="NetherRealm Studios">NetherRealm Studios</a> as a downloadable character available for purchase in July 2011, three months after the game's release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attackfanboy_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-attackfanboy-10">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>Rain appeared in the 1996 animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat:_Defenders_of_the_Realm" title="Mortal Kombat: Defenders of the Realm">Mortal Kombat: Defenders of the Realm</a></i>, in the <a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat:_Defenders_of_the_Realm#Episode_4:_.22Skin_Deep.22" title="Mortal Kombat: Defenders of the Realm">fourth episode</a> ("Skin Deep"), where he was portrayed as Kitana's ex-fiancé (in turn sparking jealousy in <a href="/wiki/Liu_Kang" title="Liu Kang">Liu Kang</a>) who was thought to have been killed in battle against Shao Kahn thousands of years ago but has suddenly resurfaced. He enlists the help of the Earthrealm defenders in searching for a sacred <a href="/wiki/Scimitar" title="Scimitar">scimitar</a> in order to keep it away from Kahn, which turns out to be a ruse that enables him to kidnap Kitana and strand the Earth heroes in Outworld.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[17]</a></sup> Rain was voiced by <a href="/wiki/Rino_Romano" title="Rino Romano">Rino Romano</a> and went unmasked in the entire episode; he was given a youthful look with short black hair parted in the middle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TKP_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-TKP-19">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>While depicted as light-skinned in the cartoon and the games, Rain's live-media incarnations were portrayed by African-American actors. He was played by stuntman Tyrone Cortez Wiggins in the 1997 film <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat:_Annihilation" title="Mortal Kombat: Annihilation">Mortal Kombat: Annihilation</a>,</i> in which he partakes in the Outworld invasion of Earth while serving as the general of Kahn's extermination squads, but is killed when Kahn hammers him into a fire pit in his <a href="/wiki/Command_center" title="Command center">war room</a> for sparing the lives of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Kabal" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">Kabal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stryker_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Stryker (Mortal Kombat)">Stryker</a> (both of whom never appear onscreen) after having captured them. In a continuity violation, an outtake of his demise was edited into <a href="/wiki/Baraka_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Baraka (Mortal Kombat)">Baraka</a>'s death scene later in the film. Rain has no fight scene in the film nor the novelization based on the script, where he has a reduced role and is identified only as a "masked general" while Baraka takes his place among Kahn's subordinates in the Outworld invasion. At the start of the twelfth episode ("Shadow of a Doubt") of the 1998 television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat:_Konquest" title="Mortal Kombat: Konquest">Mortal Kombat: Konquest</a>,</i> Rain makes a brief appearance and is played by Percy "Spitfire" Brown. He has no dialogue and is sent by Kahn to kill <a href="/wiki/Kung_Lao#Great_Kung_Lao" title="Kung Lao">Kung Lao</a> before the next tournament, but is unsuccessful after being forced to flee when Taja intervenes.</p>
<p>Rain was included as an "exclusive" in a "Klassic Ninja" six-pack of 4" action figures released in 2011 by Jazwares, while a polystone statue of the character was released by Syco Collectibles in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-figurerealm_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-figurerealm-20">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainstatue_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-rainstatue-21">[20]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">DC Comics</a>' 2015 weekly <i>Mortal Kombat X</i> prequel miniseries, Rain debuts in the eighth chapter as he joins Reiko and Mileena in their war against <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Kotal_Kahn" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">Kotal Kahn</a>, manipulating the weather to block out the sun and therefore deny Kahn his primary power source, but he is severely burned by Kahn in retaliation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MKX.238_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-MKX.238-22">[21]</a></sup> He is not seen again until the twenty-first chapter, in which he is disfigured and recuperating under Mileena's care after being recovered by the Red Dragon clan. He informs Mileena of a conversation he overheard among Red Dragon clansmen regarding her former comrade <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Reiko" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">Reiko</a>'s loyalty to <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Havik" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">Havik</a>, resulting in Mileena planning an invasion of Shang Tsung's island to hunt down Reiko, Havik and the Red Dragon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MKX.2321_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-MKX.2321-23">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Mortal_Kombat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rain has become one of the series' more maligned characters from the first generation of games, mainly due to his origins. He was ranked seventh on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ScrewAttack" title="ScrewAttack">ScrewAttack</a>'s 2011 list of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ScrewAttack%27s_Top_10" title="ScrewAttack's Top 10">ten worst <i>MK</i> characters</a>, in which they praised his roundhouse but added, "No ninja should be named after a Prince song."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-screwattack_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-screwattack-24">[23]</a></sup> He tied with fellow <i>MK</i> character <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mortal_Kombat_characters#Mokap" title="List of Mortal Kombat characters">Mokap</a> for third place in a list titled "5 Terrible Fighting Game Characters That Nobody Should Ever Choose" by WhatCulture in 2013: "[He] is there as a play on the Prince album, and that is so dumb."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whatculture_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-whatculture-25">[24]</a></sup> Cheat Code Central ranked Rain eighth in their 2012 list of the ten "lamest" video game characters, which criticized <i>Mortal Kombat's</i> excessive palette swapping in general but singled out Rain as the worst of the bunch, again due to the reference to the Prince song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCC_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-CCC-26">[25]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Topless_Robot" title="Topless Robot">Topless Robot</a>, in 2009, ranked him fourth in their list "The 9 Most Pathetic Fighting Game Characters" for the same reasons, adding: "It's not that Rain is an untalented fighter. It's just that you can't help but feel like you've seen this guy somewhere before."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toplessrobot_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-toplessrobot-27">[26]</a></sup> Stephen Kelley of <a href="/wiki/VG_Chartz" title="VG Chartz">VG Chartz</a> called the character "utterly horrible".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vgchartz_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-vgchartz-28">[27]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Den_of_Geek" title="Den of Geek">Den of Geek</a>'s Gavin Jasper criticized Rain's <i>UMK3</i> attract-mode appearance as "false advertising" and "a dick move of Boon,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoGsecret_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-DoGsecret-29">[28]</a></sup> but also ranked Rain 36th in his 2015 rating of the 64 series characters, due to his <i>MK2011</i> ending ("Thanks, Raiden, dumbass") and the expansion of his backstory in <i>Armageddon</i> as "a power-hungry jerk who believed that he was owed everything."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-denofgeek_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-denofgeek-30">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/UGO_Networks" title="UGO Networks">UGO</a> ranked him 28th out of the series' top 50 characters in 2012. "Most <i>Mortal Kombat</i> characters don't get their start as an inside joke, but Rain proves to be a rare exception."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UGO_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-UGO-31">[30]</a></sup> Elijah Watson of <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> named Rain as one of the series' most brutal characters, though he cited his noncanonical <i>Armageddon</i> and <i>MK2011</i> endings as examples while interpreting the figurative description of Kahn's death in the latter ("Rain drowned the emperor in his own blood")<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainMK9ending_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-rainMK9ending-15">[14]</a></sup> in a literal sense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-complexbrutal_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-complexbrutal-32">[31]</a></sup> Fans ranked Rain the 24th-best <i>MK</i> character in a 2013 online poll held by <a href="/wiki/CollegeHumor" title="CollegeHumor">Dorkly</a> that rated the entire series roster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dorkly_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-dorkly-33">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rain's "Upside Down Uppercut" Fatality from <i>Trilogy</i>, in which he punches his opponent apart into four separate segments that fly vertically offscreen and then drop back down to perfectly reform their upended corpse, placed on <a href="/wiki/1UP.com" title="1UP.com">1UP.com</a>'s list of <i>Mortal Kombat's</i> fifteen worst Fatalities<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1UPds_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-1UPds-34">[33]</a></sup> and made <a href="/wiki/Game_Informer" title="Game Informer">Game Informer</a>'s selection of the series' seven worst finishers: "Rain was one of the many palette-swap clones in <i>Mortal Kombat Trilogy</i>, and he easily had the worst Fatality."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[34]</a></sup> James Deaux of Earth-2.net commented, "I'm sure this was meant to be a comedic Fatality of sorts, but it looks utterly stupid."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-earth2net_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-earth2net-36">[35]</a></sup> His Animality tied with that of <a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(Mortal_Kombat)" title="Scorpion (Mortal Kombat)">Scorpion</a> as the eighth-worst Fatality in the series from <a href="/wiki/GamePro" title="GamePro">GamePro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[36]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gameranx" title="Gameranx">Gameranx</a> rated his "Bubble Burst" Fatality from <i>MK2011</i> as the series' third-most gruesome finisher,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gameranx_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-gameranx-38">[37]</a></sup> and Robert Workman of Prima Games ranked it 41st in his 2014 list of the series' fifty best Fatalities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-primagames1_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-primagames1-39">[38]</a></sup></p>
