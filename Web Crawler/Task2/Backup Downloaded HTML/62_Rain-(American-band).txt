<p><b>Rain</b> was a short-lived <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> band, considered a key part of the second wave of <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Revolution_Summer_(music)" title="Revolution Summer (music)">Revolution Summer</a> movement, which itself is regarded as the original wave of emotional hardcore punk. They followed acts like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Embrace_(U.S._band)" title="Embrace (U.S. band)">Embrace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rites_of_Spring" title="Rites of Spring">Rites of Spring</a>.</p>
<p>The band was also notable for comprising members of other influential hardcore/punk acts; Bert Queiroz spent time in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Youth_Brigade_(DC)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Youth Brigade (DC) (page does not exist)">Youth Brigade</a> and Jon Kirschten had a stint with <a href="/wiki/Gray_Matter_(band)" title="Gray Matter (band)">Gray Matter</a> prior to forming Rain, while Eli Janney later formed noted post-hardcore act <a href="/wiki/Girls_Against_Boys" title="Girls Against Boys">Girls Against Boys</a> with brother Scott after Rain's dissolution.</p>
<p>The band formed as a three-piece in 1986. After playing several shows, the band recorded twice in 1987. The first session resulted in the band's demo tape, which surfaced shortly thereafter. For the second session, second guitarist Scott McCloud joined the band. "Worlds at War," from this second session, appeared on <a href="/wiki/Dischord_Records" title="Dischord Records">Dischord</a>'s 1989 compilation, <a href="/wiki/State_of_the_Union_(album)" title="State of the Union (album)"><i>State of the Union</i></a>. However, the full session was not released until 1990, when <a href="/wiki/Guy_Picciotto" title="Guy Picciotto">Guy Picciotto</a> (<a href="/wiki/Fugazi" title="Fugazi">Fugazi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rites_of_Spring" title="Rites of Spring">Rites of Spring</a>), put it out as a 12" EP on his Peterbilt label with the title <i>La Vache Qui Rit</i>.</p>
<p>They broke up shortly thereafter.</p>
<p><i>La Vache Qui Rit</i> was remastered and reissued on compact disc by Dischord/Peterbilt in late 2007.</p>
