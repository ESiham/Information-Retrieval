<p><b>Rain</b> were an <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> band from <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, England, who had a minor hit in 1991 with "Lemonstone Desired".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(British_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The band was formed at the Merseyside Trade Union Community and Unemployed Resource Centre in <a href="/wiki/Huyton" title="Huyton">Huyton</a>, Liverpool, in 1988 with a line-up of Ned Murphy (vocals, guitar), Colin Clarke (vocals, guitar), <a href="/wiki/Martyn_Campbell" title="Martyn Campbell">Martyn Campbell</a> (bass guitar, vocals), and Tony McGuigan (drums).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strong_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Larkin_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin-2">[2]</a></sup> They were signed by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> in late 1989, and began recording with <a href="/wiki/Nick_Lowe" title="Nick Lowe">Nick Lowe</a> producing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strong_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-1">[1]</a></sup> Unhappy with the results, they did not release any material until their 1991 debut single "Lemonstone Desired", which reached number 95 in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, and was the source of some controversy due to the photograph of a naked woman on the sleeve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CS_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CS-3">[3]</a></sup> The band recorded a session for <a href="/wiki/Mark_Goodier" title="Mark Goodier">Mark Goodier</a>'s <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> show in March that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garner_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Garner-4">[4]</a></sup> This was followed by debut album <i>A Taste of Rain</i>, drawing comparisons with <a href="/wiki/Cream_(band)" title="Cream (band)">Cream</a>, <a href="/wiki/R.E.M." title="R.E.M.">R.E.M.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Byrds" title="The Byrds">the Byrds</a>, and a single featuring the title track from the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strong_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Larkin_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin-2">[2]</a></sup> The album was followed by a reissued "Lemonstone Desired", which proved to be the band's final release while together, although they shared a posthumous split album with <a href="/wiki/The_Real_People" title="The Real People">The Real People</a> in 1996, featuring tracks mainly taken from their album.</p>
