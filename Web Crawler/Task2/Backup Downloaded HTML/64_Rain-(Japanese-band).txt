<p><b>Ra:IN</b> (stands for <i>Rock and Inspiration</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1stinterview_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-1stinterview-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Instrumental_rock" title="Instrumental rock">instrumental rock</a> band. Formed in 2002 by <a href="/wiki/Pata_(musician)" title="Pata (musician)">Pata</a>, Michiaki and Tetsu, the group is signed to the <a href="/wiki/Danger_Crue_Records" title="Danger Crue Records">Danger Crue</a> label. Former <a href="/wiki/Hide_(musician)" title="Hide (musician)">hide with Spread Beaver</a> keyboardist DIE joined the band in May 2007, and drummer Tetsu left in April 2014, being replaced by Ryuichi Nishida. They have released three albums, a single and a two track EP, and have toured extensively, including international shows in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Japanese_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ra:IN was formed in October 2002 with <a href="/wiki/Pata_(musician)" title="Pata (musician)">Pata</a> on guitar, Michiaki on bass and Tetsu on drums. They were named by <a href="/wiki/Masayoshi_Kabe" title="Masayoshi Kabe">Masayoshi Kabe</a>, Michiaki's bandmate in Zoku Zoku Kazoku.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1stinterview_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-1stinterview-1">[1]</a></sup> They went on their first tour in December, with their debut maxi-single "The Border" released in April of next year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-biography-2">[2]</a></sup> It was followed by their first album <i>The Line</i> in November. They had their first overseas performances in 2004; <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> on April 24-25 and one in <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a> in July. RaIN performed three shows in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> in May 2005, and at the closing of the <a href="/wiki/Hide_(musician)" title="Hide (musician)">hide</a> museum on September 9 back in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-biography-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>They released their second album <i>Before the Siren</i>, their first on popular independent record label <a href="/wiki/Danger_Crue_Records" title="Danger Crue Records">Danger Crue</a>, in March 2006 and embarked on tour in support of it, which included two more shows in Taipei. In 2007 they released the DVD <i>Hard Rain and Rocks Live</i> and performed two shows in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, one of which was at the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Pop_Festival" title="Beijing Pop Festival">Beijing Pop Festival</a>. That year keyboardist DIE joined the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-biography-2">[2]</a></sup> Their third album <i>Metal Box</i> was released in Japan in April 2008 and in France the following month. They also performed at the hide memorial summit at <a href="/wiki/Ajinomoto_Stadium" title="Ajinomoto Stadium">Ajinomoto Stadium</a> on May 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2009 Ra:IN began a lengthy European tour which took them to <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ljubljana" title="Ljubljana">Ljubljana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gdynia" title="Gdynia">Gdynia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, Paris, <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, and ended in <a href="/wiki/Hradec_Kr%C3%A1lov%C3%A9" title="Hradec Králové">Hradec Králové</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Rock_for_People" title="Rock for People">Rock for People</a> festival. The band released a special single sold only on the tour, titled "Circle/Psychogenic".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> They held the two man Rock’n Roll Vaudeville 2011 show together with <a href="/wiki/Der_Zibet" title="Der Zibet">Der Zibet</a> on June 24, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In September 2012, Ra:IN performed alongside Der Zibet, Ladies Room and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Yankees" title="Tokyo Yankees">Tokyo Yankees</a> at the Yokohama Summer Rock Fes. – Revolution Rocks 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced on April 17, 2014 that Tetsu had left the group. Ra:IN performed in the United States at <a href="/wiki/Project_A-Kon" title="Project A-Kon">A-Kon</a> on June 6–8,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and Ryuichi Nishida officially joined the band as drummer on June 28.</p>
