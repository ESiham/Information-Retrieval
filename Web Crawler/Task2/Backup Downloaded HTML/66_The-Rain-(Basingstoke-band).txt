<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>The Rain</b> were an <a href="/wiki/Indie_pop" title="Indie pop">indie pop</a> band from <a href="/wiki/Basingstoke" title="Basingstoke">Basingstoke</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, active between 1986 and the early 1990s. They later changed their name to <b>Clark Springs</b>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rain_(Basingstoke_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The band was formed by singer/guitarist Clive Stubbings, bassist Nigel Rivers later to be replaced by Tony Duckworth (ex-Junk Factory)and drummer Chris Morrell. Their first single, "Tom Paine" was self-released, but caught the ear of <a href="/wiki/Medium_Cool_Records" title="Medium Cool Records">Medium Cool Records</a>, who signed them up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcbio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcbio-1">[1]</a></sup> They contributed two tracks, "Dry The Rain" and "Seven Red Apples" to Medium Cool's <i>Edge of The Road</i> compilation album, but their first single for the label, "First Of May" never saw the light of day due to the collapse of Medium Cool's distributor, <a href="/wiki/Red_Rhino_Records" title="Red Rhino Records">Red Rhino Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcbio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mcbio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band's first album, <i>To The Citadel</i>, was released in 1989, desicribed by <a href="/wiki/Melody_Maker" title="Melody Maker">Melody Maker</a> as "glorious, honed to cut-glass perfection", and gaining comparison with <a href="/wiki/The_Byrds" title="The Byrds">The Byrds</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R.E.M._(band)" title="R.E.M. (band)">R.E.M.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sounds_(magazine)" title="Sounds (magazine)">Sounds</a>. A 12" EP, "The Watercress Girl" followed in 1991.</p>
<p>They changed their name to Clark Springs, releasing a single, "Talking Kent State" in 1993, and contributing "She Lives By The Castle" to <i>A Tribute To <a href="/wiki/Felt_(band)" title="Felt (band)">Felt</a></i> in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
