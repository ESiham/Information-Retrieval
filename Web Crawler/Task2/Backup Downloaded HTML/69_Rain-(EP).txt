<p><i><b>Rain</b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> by <a href="/wiki/40_Below_Summer" title="40 Below Summer">40 Below Summer</a>. The disc is a re-release of the band's much sought-after independent EP, before they signed with <a href="/wiki/London-Sire_Records" title="London-Sire Records">London-Sire Records</a>. The EP was reissued on July 31, 2007 via Crash Music Inc.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_reception">Background and reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The EP was recorded and mixed in July and August of 2000. It was mastered by Al Silverman at ARF Digital, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wayback_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-wayback-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In their 2001 review, <i><a href="/wiki/Blabbermouth.net" title="Blabbermouth.net">Blabbermouth.net</a></i> wrote "Although there is nothing on here that can be considered innovative or ground-breaking, 40 Below Summer possess a knack for mixing huge, hard-sounding grooves with near-first-rate quality vocal hooks, and they do so with a greater level of conviction than most of their counterparts. Among the best examples of this is the track "Endorphines", an excellently crafted number that starts out in a laid-back fashion but climaxes with what is arguably the catchiest chorus on the CD—a potential rock radio hit in the making."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The tracks "Falling Down" and "Jonesin" would be rerecorded for the band's first and only major label release <i><a href="/wiki/Invitation_to_the_Dance_(album)" title="Invitation to the Dance (album)">Invitation to the Dance</a>.</i> "Faces" was also rerecorded during the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mourning_After" title="The Mourning After">Mourning After</a></i> era as "P.S. I Hate You". It appeared on the 2003 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Backyard_Wrestling:_Don%27t_Try_This_at_Home" title="Backyard Wrestling: Don't Try This at Home">Backyard Wrestling: Don't Try This at Home</a></i>.</p>
