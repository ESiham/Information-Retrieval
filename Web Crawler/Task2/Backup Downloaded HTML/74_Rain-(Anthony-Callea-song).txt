<p>"<b>Rain</b>" is the second song released by <i><a href="/wiki/Australian_Idol" title="Australian Idol">Australian Idol</a></i> series two runner-up <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Callea" title="Anthony Callea">Anthony Callea</a>, and features on his self-titled debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Anthony_Callea_(album)" title="Anthony Callea (album)">Anthony Callea</a></i> (2005). It was released as a double A-side set features the song plus his recording of <a href="/wiki/Simon_%26_Garfunkel" title="Simon &amp; Garfunkel">Simon &amp; Garfunkel</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Bridge_over_Troubled_Water_(song)" title="Bridge over Troubled Water (song)">Bridge over Troubled Water</a>", which he performed on <i>Australian Idol</i>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/CD_single" title="CD single">CD single</a> for the song was released as a three-track standard version with the <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side" title="A-side and B-side">B-side</a> "Don't Tell Me". It was also available for a short time as a limited edition collector's 2CD tri-gatefold set with a second B-side, "Wanna Be the One".</p>

<p>In early 2006, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Ola_Svensson" title="Ola Svensson">Ola Svensson</a> covered the song and released it as his debut single. Following his participation in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Idol_2005" title="Idol 2005">Idol 2005</a></i>, the single debuted at #1 on the <a href="/wiki/Sverigetopplistan" title="Sverigetopplistan">Swedish singles chart</a> and remained there for three consecutive weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
