<p>"<b>Rain</b>" is a song by <a href="/wiki/The_Cult" title="The Cult">The Cult</a> from their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_(Cult_album)" title="Love (Cult album)">Love</a> album. It was briefly known as "Sad Rain" during its recording, and the lyrics were reportedly inspired by a <a href="/wiki/Hopi" title="Hopi">Hopi</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rain_dance" title="Rain dance">rain dance</a>. Ultimately it was the second single released from that album following "<a href="/wiki/She_Sells_Sanctuary" title="She Sells Sanctuary">She Sells Sanctuary</a>". It peaked at #17 on the UK singles chart, and has always been a live favourite, being performed at almost every gig since its release.</p>
<p>Despite the song's popularity, after performing it live in November 1989 at Wembley, singer <a href="/wiki/Ian_Astbury" title="Ian Astbury">Ian Astbury</a> said, "So you like that one?" and after the audience cheered, Astbury responded by saying, "Personally, I don't." This can be heard on <a href="/wiki/Bootleg_recording" title="Bootleg recording">bootleg</a> live audio and video recordings of the concert.</p>
<p>There is one alternative version to the song, an extended remix titled "(Here Comes The) Rain". The remix version of this song was used during a party sequence in the film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demoni_2" title="Demoni 2">Demoni 2</a></i>, and the original version plays during the end credits to the film. The original version also appeared on the soundtrack to the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variations">Variations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(The_Cult_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There were several variations of the cover artwork used throughout the world. Some versions used silver lettering, others used white, while others had a silhouette effect on the cover. In <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, a completely different picture cover was used, with trio of photos of the band: two onstage colour photos, and on the back one promotional black-and-white photo. This version was issued as both a white label promotional record, also with picture labels.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_Versions">Cover Versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(The_Cult_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cover Versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>German <a href="/wiki/Gothic_rock" title="Gothic rock">gothic rock</a>/<a href="/wiki/Gothic_metal" title="Gothic metal">gothic metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Love_Like_Blood_(band)" title="Love Like Blood (band)">Love Like Blood</a> recorded a cover of "Rain" on their 2001 album "Chronology Of A Love-Affair"</p>
<p>Italian <a href="/wiki/Gothic_metal" title="Gothic metal">gothic metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Theatres_des_Vampires" title="Theatres des Vampires">Theatres des Vampires</a> performed a cover version of Rain on their 2008 album, <a href="/wiki/Anima_Noir" title="Anima Noir">Anima Noir</a>.</p>
<p>Italian <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> band Rain recorded a cover of Rain on their 2008 album, "Dad is Dead".</p>
<p>Japanese experimental/drone/stoner band Boris, together with Ian Astbury, recorded a cover version of "Rain" on their 2010 collaborative EP, "BXI"</p>
<p>Canada-based groove metallists City of Fire recorded a cover of "Rain" on their self-titled 2010 album.</p>
<p>The album "Metal Addiction" contains a cover by Sun Eats Hours.</p>
<p>Canadian musician Philip André and Polish singer Palon released a cover of "Rain" in 2014.</p>
<p>American stoner/industrial/groove metal band <a href="/wiki/City_of_Fire_(band)" title="City of Fire (band)">City of Fire</a> recorded a cover of "Rain" on their self-titled 2011 album.</p>
<p>German <a href="/wiki/Gothic_metal" title="Gothic metal">gothic metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/ASP_(band)" title="ASP (band)">ASP</a> recorded a cover of "Rain" in their 2009 single "Wer sonst? / Im Märchenland".</p>
