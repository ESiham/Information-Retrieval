<p>"<b>Rain</b>" is a song by <a href="/wiki/Mika_(singer)" title="Mika (singer)">Mika</a>, released as the second single from his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boy_Who_Knew_Too_Much_(album)" title="The Boy Who Knew Too Much (album)">The Boy Who Knew Too Much</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mikasounds.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mikasounds.com-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MS_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MS-3">[3]</a></sup> The song was produced and mixed by <a href="/wiki/Greg_Wells" title="Greg Wells">Greg Wells</a> and features violinist <a href="/wiki/Owen_Pallett" title="Owen Pallett">Owen Pallett</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Mika_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song's lyrics are taken from a break-up letter that Mika wrote to an ex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Q_(magazine)" title="Q (magazine)">Q</a></i> magazine, Mika says:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">["Rain"] is an unapologetic '80s pop record. I bumped into producer <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Price" title="Stuart Price">Stuart Price</a> while he was working with <a href="/wiki/The_Killers" title="The Killers">The Killers</a> at <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Studios" title="Olympic Studios">Olympic Studios</a>. He helped me program this. It reminded him of <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Goes_to_Hollywood" title="Frankie Goes to Hollywood">Frankie Goes to Hollywood</a>.</td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td class="cquotecite" colspan="3" style="border: none; padding-right: 4%; font-size: smaller; text-align: right;"><cite>— Mika, <i>Q Magazine</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qmag_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-qmag-5">[5]</a></sup></cite></td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>Keira performed Mika's song at Australian Idol's grand finale along with "<a href="/wiki/We_Are_Golden" title="We Are Golden">We Are Golden</a>". On 5 February 2010 Mika performed a special operatic version of the song in a duet with opera star <a href="/wiki/Danielle_de_Niese" title="Danielle de Niese">Danielle de Niese</a> on the ITV1 show '<a href="/wiki/Popstar_to_Operastar" title="Popstar to Operastar">Popstar to Operastar</a>'.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Mika_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song received very positive reviews from most critics. Nick Levene from <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a> gave to the song 4 stars (out of 5) and said: "Those unconvinced that much had changed when Mika returned with '<a href="/wiki/We_Are_Golden" title="We Are Golden">We Are Golden</a>' this summer should be pleasantly surprised by 'Rain', the second single from his second album. The shrieking has been reined in, the nursery rhyme hooks have been ditched and instead we're treated to a concoction of shimmering synths, layered vocals and throbbing, clubby beats. As it builds towards a crescendo that's both intriguingly dark and glitteringly magical, Mika proves he's perfectly capable of channelling his hyperactivity in the right direction. Here's hoping for more of this in the future".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <i>Heather Phares</i> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> says that "Rain" is a kissing cousin to "Relax"'s pulsing, melancholy disco-pop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "Rain" was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Epping_Forest" title="Epping Forest">Epping Forest</a> in <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a> and premiered online on 16 October 2009. It was directed by Nez Khammal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mikasounds.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mikasounds.com-2">[2]</a></sup> The video is set in a dark <a href="/wiki/Enchanted_forest" title="Enchanted forest">enchanted forest</a> with Mika waking up in a colourful <a href="/wiki/Tent" title="Tent">tent</a>. He is soon joined by various strangely dressed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imps" title="Imps">imps</a> and creatures who dance around him at first but chase him out of the forest amid exploding fireworks.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charts">Charts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Mika_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Charts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The single was released on 23 November 2009 in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> in both digital and physical formats. The single peaked at #72 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, making this Mika's lowest charting UK single. Casablanca Records believed this was due to low promotional airplay on UK Radio. The song was received better in continental <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, where it peaked at #4 in Italy, being certified platinum by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federation_of_the_Italian_Music_Industry" title="Federation of the Italian Music Industry">Federation of the Italian Music Industry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It also peaked inside the Top 40 in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, and debuted at #8 in <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Wallonia</a>. The single debuted at #5 in <a href="/wiki/Syndicat_National_de_l%27%C3%89dition_Phonographique" title="Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique">French Singles Chart</a> and peaked #16 in Digital Singles Chart.</p>
