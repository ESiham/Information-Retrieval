<p>"<b>Rain</b>" is a song by South Korean singer <a href="/wiki/Kim_Tae-yeon" title="Kim Tae-yeon">Kim Tae-yeon</a>, a member of the South Korean girl group <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a>. It was released digitally by <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a> on February 3, 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_composition">Background and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Taeyeon_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2016, <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a> announced that they were to launch a digital music platform called <a href="/wiki/SM_Station" title="SM Station">SM Station</a>, in which SM artists would release a new song every week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> "Rain" and its B-side track "Secret" served as the opening single of the project and were released on February 3, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> "Rain" is described as a medium-tempo song with jazz and R&amp;B sounds. Lyrically, it uses rain as a metaphor to describe memories of past love.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arirang_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-arirang-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Taeyeon_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video for "Rain" was filmed indoors, having the concept of Taeyeon in a "room of memories", missing a past lover. It alternate between black and white scenes of Taeyeon singing alone in front of a microphone, and colored scenes of her in a flooding room.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arirang_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-arirang-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> "Rain" was the most viewed music video by a South Korean act on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> in February 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_(Taeyeon_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Rain" debuted atop South Korea's <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Digital_Chart" title="Gaon Digital Chart">Gaon Digital Chart</a>. It won the first place on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a>'s music program <i><a href="/wiki/Inkigayo" title="Inkigayo">Inkigayo</a></i> on February 14, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The song received a Digital Bonsang at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_Golden_Disc_Awards" title="2016 Golden Disc Awards">31st Golden Disk Awards</a>.</p>
