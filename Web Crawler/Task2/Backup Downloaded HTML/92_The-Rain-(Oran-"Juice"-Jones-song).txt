<p>"<b>The Rain</b>" is a 1986 <a href="/wiki/Crossover_music" title="Crossover music">crossover</a> <a href="/wiki/Hit_single" title="Hit single">hit single</a> originally performed by R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Oran_%22Juice%22_Jones" title='Oran "Juice" Jones'>Oran "Juice" Jones</a>, which he released on and from his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Juice_(Oran_%22Juice%22_Jones_album)" title='Juice (Oran "Juice" Jones album)'>Juice</a>.</i></p>
<p>The song's lyrics include a long recitation at the end; written by Vincent Bell, who also composed the music, they involve a man confronting his girlfriend regarding her infidelity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rain_(Oran_%22Juice%22_Jones_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The single was Jones's biggest hit. It reached number one on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">R&amp;B chart</a> for two weeks, making it the first #1 R&amp;B hit issued by the then newly created <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam</a> record label. The single also peaked at number nine on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and reached #4 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>.</p>
<p>"The Rain" was nominated for two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a>, but Jones never had another Hot 100 hit and left the music business after his third album failed to chart. "The Rain" is ranked #63 on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s 100 Greatest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/One_hit_wonder" title="One hit wonder">One-Hit Wonders</a> of the '80s. During the show, Jones was asked about his one-hit wonder status. His reply was: "Oran 'Juice' Jones, he came, he saw, he conquered, he moved on. You dig?"</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_use">Later use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rain_(Oran_%22Juice%22_Jones_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song produced a novelty, answer record, titled “Thunder &amp; Lightning,” performed by Miss Thang and released in 1986 by <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Boy_Records" title="Tommy Boy Records">Tommy Boy Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Another response version was done by an artist who went by the name Pamala, released in 1986 by <a href="/wiki/Evejim_Records" title="Evejim Records">Evejim Records</a>, entitled "Walkin in the Rain, Yes You Saw Me.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Yet another answer record was released in 1986 titled "The Drain" recorded by Leot Littlepage on <a href="/wiki/Select_Records" title="Select Records">Select Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, the song was covered by NYC band <a href="/wiki/Si*S%C3%A9" title="Si*Sé">Si*Sé</a> on their self-titled debut album. In 2005, jazz artist <a href="/wiki/Ledisi" title="Ledisi">Ledisi</a> covered "The Rain" on the album <i>Def Jazz,</i> which featured vocals from Jones.</p>
<p>The song was sampled for "Standing in the Rain" by <a href="/wiki/Action_Bronson" title="Action Bronson">Action Bronson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dan_Auerbach" title="Dan Auerbach">Dan Auerbach</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Keys" title="The Black Keys">The Black Keys</a> ft <a href="/wiki/Mark_Ronson" title="Mark Ronson">Mark Ronson</a> featured in the 2016 film <i><a href="/wiki/Suicide_Squad_(film)" title="Suicide Squad (film)">Suicide Squad</a></i>.</p>
