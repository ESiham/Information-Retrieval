<p>"<b>The Rain (Supa Dupa Fly)</b>" is a 1997 hit song recorded by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Missy_Elliott" title="Missy Elliott">Missy "Misdemeanor" Elliott</a>. It was written and composed by <a href="/wiki/Don_Bryant_(songwriter)" title="Don Bryant (songwriter)">Don Bryant</a>, Bernard "Bernie" Miller, Elliott, and producer <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a> for her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Supa_Dupa_Fly" title="Supa Dupa Fly">Supa Dupa Fly</a></i> (1997) and contains a sample of <a href="/wiki/Ann_Peebles" title="Ann Peebles">Ann Peebles</a>' 1973 single "<a href="/wiki/I_Can%27t_Stand_the_Rain_(song)" title="I Can't Stand the Rain (song)">I Can't Stand the Rain</a>", whose lyrics serve as the <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">chorus</a>.</p>
<p>The song was released to US radio stations on May 20, 1997, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hype_Williams" title="Hype Williams">Hype Williams</a>-directed video was released to video shows starting June 3, 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The song entered the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Airplay" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Airplay">Hot R&amp;B Airplay</a> chart in mid-June, and peaked at #6 the week of August 9, 1997. It peaked at #51 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_100_Airplay_(Radio_Songs)" title="Hot 100 Airplay (Radio Songs)">Hot 100 Airplay</a> chart the following week.</p>
<p>The song was released as the album's <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> on Jun 29, 1997 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>(see <a href="/wiki/1997_in_music" title="1997 in music">1997 in music</a>), and reached the top twenty in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> ranked the song 99th on <i>VH1's 100 Greatest Songs of the '90s</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> And in 2010 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a> included the song at number 33 on their <i>Top 200 Tracks of the 90s</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2000, "The Rain (Supa Dupa Fly)" was named the fourth-best single of 1999 by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s annual-year end <a href="/wiki/Pazz_%26_Jop" title="Pazz &amp; Jop">Pazz &amp; Jop</a> critics' poll.</p>
<p>The song's moans are used as a sample in <a href="/wiki/Method_Man_%26_Redman" title="Method Man &amp; Redman">Method Man &amp; Redman</a>'s "The ?".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Rain_(Supa_Dupa_Fly)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The breakout <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was the first of Missy's career directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hype_Williams" title="Hype Williams">Hype Williams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The most notable aspect of the video is the inflated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trash_bag" title="Trash bag">trash bag</a> that Missy wears during a <a href="/wiki/Fisheye_lens" title="Fisheye lens">fisheye lens</a> shot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tamara_%22Taj%22_Johnson-George" title='Tamara "Taj" Johnson-George'>Tamara "Taj" Johnson-George</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SWV_(band)" title="SWV (band)">SWV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yo-Yo_(rapper)" title="Yo-Yo (rapper)">Yo Yo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil' Kim">Lil' Kim</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Total_(band)" title="Total (band)">Total</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/702_(band)" title="702 (band)">702</a>, <a href="/wiki/Da_Brat" title="Da Brat">Da Brat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Cease" title="Lil' Cease">Lil' Cease</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Puff Daddy</a> all make cameos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The video was nominated for Best Rap Video at the <a href="/wiki/1997_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="1997 MTV Video Music Awards">1997 VMAs</a>.</p>
