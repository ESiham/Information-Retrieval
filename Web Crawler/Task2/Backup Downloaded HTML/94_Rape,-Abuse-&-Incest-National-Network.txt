<p>The <b>Rape, Abuse &amp; Incest National Network</b> (<b>RAINN</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Sexual_assault" title="Sexual assault">anti-sexual assault</a> organization, the largest in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> RAINN operates the National Sexual Assault Hotline and carries out programs to prevent sexual assault, help victims, and to ensure that rapists are brought to justice.</p>
<p>RAINN was founded in 1994 by Scott Berkowitz. <a href="/wiki/Tori_Amos" title="Tori Amos">Tori Amos</a> was the organization's first spokesperson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edition.cnn.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-edition.cnn.com-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-books.google.com_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.com-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Christina_Ricci" title="Christina Ricci">Christina Ricci</a> is RAINN's current national spokesperson and a member of its National Leadership Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Sexual_Assault_Hotline">National Sexual Assault Hotline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rape,_Abuse_%26_Incest_National_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: National Sexual Assault Hotline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The National Sexual Assault Hotline is a 24-hour, <a href="/wiki/Toll-free_telephone_number" title="Toll-free telephone number">toll-free</a> phone service that automatically and anonymously links callers to the nearest RAINN-associated <a href="/wiki/Rape_crisis_center" title="Rape crisis center">rape crisis center</a> with a counselor able to respond to the call. More than 1,100 local partnerships are associated with RAINN to provide sexual assault victims with free, confidential services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In the summer of 2006, RAINN received its one millionth caller.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Since 2008, RAINN has provided anonymous, on-line crisis support through its National Sexual Assault Online Hotline via <a href="/wiki/Instant_messaging" title="Instant messaging">instant messaging</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wrestling legend <a href="/wiki/Mick_Foley" title="Mick Foley">Mick Foley</a> is also a member of RAINN's national leadership council and has worked as a volunteer on its online hotline. During a 15-month period ending in April 2011, Foley logged more than 550 hours talking to victims online. The same month, Foley offered to mow anyone's lawn who donated up to a certain amount to the organization, stating, "If you want to help survivors of sexual assault, or just want to see a big guy with long hair mowing your lawn in front of your friends, please take part..."</p>
